Introduction

Hello all, this guide has been written explicitly for NovaRO. This guide is aimed at new players looking for help regarding gear sets for various Warlock builds. My hope is to arm the reader with enough information to allow them to create their own builds and possibly even forge new territory as the game progresses through various updates. If you are not already aware, playing and gearing a warlock can be quite tricky because there are various viable skills with different requirements in terms of gear, especially cards. At the same time I also think it is the most interesting class to play in terms of equipment because there are so many options and unique builds. It is important to note, however, that you may need to create multiple sets of equipment to be able to play different warlock archetypes. Lastly I should point out that this guide mostly just covers Chain Lightning, Comet, Jack Frost and Soul Expansion builds.

Key Concepts

Key Concepts

When creating Warlock builds I would say there are 6 main concepts to be aware of: 1. Damage, 2. Cast time, 3. After Cast Delay (ACD), 4. Skill Cool Down, 5. Attack Speed (ASPD), and 6. Magic Defense (MDEF) Bypass. I will address each of these issues separately below.

1. Damage

Increasing damage can be quite complicated as there are several things to consider: Flat Matk, Matk %, Magic Property damage, Race Modifiers, Element Modifiers, Size modifiers, skill modifiers, and class modifiers. Most of these modifiers are compounded rather than added which implies it is best to spread things out rather than focus only on one. I highly recommend using a damage calculator like Muh's Damage Calc. To make use of this calc you can open a chat window in game and type @bs. This will show all of your battle states. Then you can type /savechat. This will save the output of your battle stats to a file in our NovaRO chat folder. You can then copy/paste the results into the Import section of the Damage Calculator.

2. Cast Time

Cast time is incredibly important. It describes the time with which it takes for you to cast one of your many dope-ass spells. There are two components of cast time. The first is variable cast time (VCT) and the second is Fixed Cast Time (FCT). Most spells will be affected by both of these, but others may rely on only one. For more information, see the Cast Time Wiki. It is very important to understand that in order to reach instant cast for a skill that has both of these components (VCT and FCT), you must meet the requirements for both of them.

2a. Variable Cast Time (VCT)

VCT can be reduced through stats or gear/skills. The goal is usually to reach instant cast (IC), although this is not always necessary depending on the build, as we will see. There are advantages to either approach, but generally speaking, the tradeoff for reaching IC through gear is usually damage, and the tradeoff for reaching IC through stats is usually the need for consumables/buffs. Just to reiterate from the link above, to reach IC through stats you need your Dex*2 + Int stats to reach 530. For an example of gear that provides VCT reduction, look at Automatic Armor B (it provides 15% VCT reduction). For an example skill that provides VCT reduction, look at Poem of Bragi from Minstrels (20% VCT reductions). The biggest thing to remember is that you can ONLY get IC through stats OR gear/skills. They act multiplicatively, not additively. So if you have 60% reduction through stats and 40% reduction through gear/skills, your total VCT reduction will be 0.6 + (1-0.6) * 0.4 = 0.76 = 76% total VCT reduction (which means an example skill that has a 10 second variable cast time will be reduced by 7.6 seconds).

One important thing to consider is that the formula for calculating VCT reduction through stats is actually sublinear, meaning initial gains are more meaningful than later ones. I.e., there are diminishing returns, so to speak. Perhaps this can best be understood by looking at this scuffed graph I created. The x-axis is the part you have control over: your stats. The y-axis is a number between 0 and 1. Multiple this by 100 to get your VCT percentage reduction.


2b. Fixed Cast Time (FCT)

FCT can be affected by gear/skills that either reduce FCT by a percentage or an absolute number of seconds. It is important to note that for the former, only the highest percentage reduction is used, whereas the latter is additive. This means that if you have two pieces of gear that give 50% and 25% reduced FCT respectively, only the 50% will be applied to your skills. If, however, you have two pieces of gear that reduce FCT by 0.2 and 0.3 seconds respectively, that will result in 0.5 seconds FCT being reduced from all skills. Most of our skills have 1 second or less FCT. For this reason, it is usually prudent to focus on absolute reductions in FCT as opposed to percentage based. Currently there is no way to reduce FCT through stats.

2c. Examples

So, lets look at some examples of warlock skills, and how they are affected by reductions:

  • Soul Expansion: VCT: 2 secs. FCT: None. Total Cast Time: 2 seconds
    • The casting time for Soul Expansion relies only on variable cast time. There is no fixed casting time portion.
  • Chain Lightning: VCT: 3 secs. FCT: 1 sec. Total Cast Time: 4 seconds
    • Chain lightning has both VCT and FCT portions that make up its total cast time of 4 seconds.
  • Energy Coat: VCT: None. FCT: 5 seconds. Total Cast Time:5 seconds
    • Lastly, Energy Coat is an example of a skill that only has a FCT component, no VCT. These types of skills are rare.

3. After Cast Delay (ACD)

Ahh, the elusive after cast delay. What does this mean? Well, it is exactly what it suggests. ACD signifies the time you must wait after casting a spell before you can cast any other spell. This is why it is also referred to as a global cool down. So, an ACD of 0.5 seconds means you must wait 0.5 seconds after completing a spell before you can cast it or any other spell again.

4. Skill Cool Down (CD)

Some skills have a CD period that works in a similar way to ACD except that it applies only to the given skill. It is important to note that ACD and CD are not additive, they overlap and occur at the same time. So, for example let's take a look at Comet. Comet has a CD of 20 seconds and ACD of 1.5 seconds. This means that after completing a cast of comet, 1.5 seconds later you can cast any other spell, and 20 seconds from the initial cast you can recast Comet. If you are still confused, the following timeline should help to illustrate how things work.

Comet is cast              Any spell (besides Comet)         Comet can be cast again

5. Attack Speed (ASPD)

ASPD might not seem important to Warlocks because we are not auto attacking, but in fact it is extremely important for spamming skills faster. ASPD informs animation speed, and the faster your skill animations are, the faster you can recast the spell in succession. For a better treatment of this topic, see Mel's Sorcerer Guide

6. MDEF Bypass

Well, I saved the best for last. It is easy to overlook MDEF Bypass but it is incredibly important and "reasonably" easy to obtain nowadays. As far as I am aware, this is the MDEF formula currently being used on NovaRO (If this is wrong feel free to edit).

Here is the infamous Mdef graph from the link provided. Observe that it follows a hyperbolic growth pattern. This means that MDEF becomes less impactful the more that you have.

Okay I want to spend a bit of time illustrating the importance of MDEF Bypass for Warlocks. I will do so through some examples. For the 5 examples below we will assume the player damage on an enemy with 0 MDEF is 3,000 per hit. This is a low number but it is easier to work with. Also, we assume the monster in question has 100 MDEF. These two values never change. The only thing that changes is the amount of bypass the player possess. So, in Example 1 we assume 0 MDEF Bypass. Example 2, we assume 25% Bypass. This progressively goes up until we reach 100% bypass in Example 5. 100% bypass effectively turns the monster's MDEF to 0.

Summary

To summarize, as a Warlock we are interested in increasing our damage and bypassing enemy MDEF so that we can inflict more pain per each cast of our spells. We very much love reaching instant cast so that we can cast our skills faster and avoid being interrupted. Lastly, we try our best to reduce skill cool downs and general after cast delay while increasing attack speed in order to reduce the time we have to wait between casting spells.

Warlock Builds.

Warlock Archetypes (builds)

Introduction

Here we discuss some common Warlock builds. There may be some topics in this section that have already been covered, but we have kept them in for those who wish to jump directly to this section to get an overview of how to play a warlock. The approach here is very holistic.

This is a link for a google spreadsheet with the equipments for early game (crimson rock build); mid game (transition to comet); end game comet (both celine set and automatic armor variants); chain lightning (both solo and party setup) and jack frost. To be able to see all the builds, remember to MAKE A COPY OF THE SPREADSHEET. NOTE: THIS SPREADSHEET IS STILL A WORK IN PROGRESS. I WILL REMOVE THIS NOTE WHEN IT IS COMPLETELY FINISHED

At the time of writing, warlock is one of the best classes for cleaning in end-game instances, for its powerful area damage and debuff in the form of magic intoxication. An important warning: this class is not intended for people who like “budget” classes or who don't have a very reasonable amount of zeny to spend. The return on investment only happens when most, if not all, parts of the build are complete. I say this because the class isn't the best to field farming, so I personally use it the most on end-game instances, which is where I think it truly shines. In this guide, I will cover the following builds: Chain Lightning; Comet; early game Crimson Rock; Jack Frost and Soul Expansion.

There is some information that is relevant before we go to the practical part of the guide:

  • The instant cast (ic) formula on renewal servers is divided into two parts: variable cast (vct) and fixed cast (fct).
    • INT + 2*DEX = 530 or Equipment with VCT% reduction. The two ways to achieve 100% variable cast reduction do not add up, so the ideal is to choose 1 of them.
    • Two of the three relevant skills ( Reading Spellbook and Chain Lightning) have fixed cast of 1s in addition to vct, whereas Soul Expansion only has vct.
  • Fixed cast reductions by percentage do not add up, only the highest reduction present is applied. The percentage reduction is applied after any reductions in nominal values, such as 0.5s from temporal boots of dexterity
    • The class has a passive called Radius, which helps a little in the early game with 20% fct, but won't be taken into account later on.
  • ACD, after cast delay or global cooldown is the down time after some skills are used, and in this period, no other skills can be used. This concept is different from cooldown, which is the cooldown of a specific skill.

Skills in RO can have all 2, only 1, or none.

The class skill tree varies depending on taste and the skills you are going to use, so feel free personalize yours. Despite this, some skills are essential: Mystical Amplification, Radius, Recognized Spell. Some honorable mentions: Fire Wall, Safety Wall, Quagmire. What these three have in common is that they keep you safe in some way or another. These skills + Energy Coat are the greatest tools to staying alive as a Warlock. After all, a dead character deals no damage.

It is important to remember that the Recognized Spell buff does not change its damage increase values ​​in subsequent levels, only its duration and cd. Skill level 1 has 1 minute of duration -which is the same as Mystical Amplification and has 1 minute of cooldown. So while it is annoying to have to rebuff every 1 minute, it is better than the warlock dying and having no access to the buff because it is on a long cooldown.


The class brings about a change in playstyle due to the new skills Reading Spellbook, aided by Freezing Spell and Release that allows the bypassing of the high cd of skills, instead relying on acd.

Reading Spellbook, as the name implies, reads the skill book and stores it in the mind slots. You can release the skill via Release lv 1. Do note that even if the skill is AoE in nature, like Comet, it still needs a target and it can be stopped if the target is inside Land Protector. Mind slots are guaranteed by Freezing Spell. The formula for how many skills you can store is as follows: (Freezing Spell Level × 4) + Floor(Base Level ÷ 10) + Floor(Total INT ÷ 10). Additional levels in this skill increase your slots by 8 for each level. Each skill stored has a different cost which can be found here.

Reading Spellbook has 1s fct and 5s vct. It's acd depends on the skill read, which is 1.5s in the case of the comet. This skill is used in conjunction with Release (lv 1), which casts a random skill guarded by the reading spellbook.

The learned level of the skill is used at the time of release, so it is recommended that you maximize its level.

Crimson Rock (Crock)

Crimson Rock is a Fire element skill, with an AoE of 7x7 and usable with the Reading Spellbook mechanic. I have never used this skill after the early game, because it has an inherent weakness against holy and shadow monsters. With more gears and shadow gears that support it now, I think it's possible to take it to the next level, albeit not to end-game instances for two reasons: the monsters are usually a mix of holy and shadow property and the magic intox debuff from comet is too strong to not have. The starting set of eden academy (noblesse, imperial and grace) makes this a viable early-game build that you can use to farm until you transition to a comet build.
If you want to build it past that point, the theoretical requirements are 72% acd (check on training chamber for a more accurate value) and high aspd.

Comet

Comet is a 13x13 AoE skill that deals neutral damage, is used with the Reading Spellbook mechanic and has a strong debuff, Magic Intoxication (magic intox), which reduces all elemental resistances of the affected monsters by 50% for 10 seconds. This skill is the bread and butter of any end-game warlock due to it's huge aoe and no inherent weakness except against ghost monsters -which can be shored up via Soul Expansion swap-. For end-game instances, the goal is to kill the monsters in 2 or 3 releases.

Theoretical requirements are: 91% acd (go to training chamber to check which value works best for your ping) and benefits from high aspd, but is not mandatory to have. The high aspd penalty from rods can be somewhat remedied by the use of +9 pks (+2 aspd, +10 aspd) and Berserk Potion. It is not required to be able to achieve instant-cast, but it is good to have it.

Chain Lightning (CL)

Chain Lightning is a single target Wind element skill which will bounce to between nearby monsters if there are any. When there are no enemies to bounce, the skill will do 4 ticks. Each individual tick counts as a separate instance of damage for the purpose of Lex Aeterna and similar skills. This is the best skill in the Warlock arsenal for sustained damage per second (dps), but the high requirements in terms of acd and aspd make this skill not ideal to use in a solo scenario.

For the full requirements: 193 aspd, 96% acd, 1s fct and 100% vct. As with all other builds, check on training chamber for your accurate acd and aspd requirements. A first time user of the skill might be alarmed that it has no animation when you've reached instant cast, but that's how it works.

Jack Frost (JF)

Jack Frost is a single target skill with an AoE of 13x13 and uses the Reading Spellbook mechanic as well. It's damage can be increased via the debuff Frost applied with the skill Frost Misty. This is a very niche skill, which I only use on some farming spots, like Thanatos 12th floor due to the inherent advantage the water element has in that floor.
Build requirements are 72% acd and high aspd.

Soul Expansion (SE)

Soul Expansion is a single target skill which has an AoE of 5x5 cells and Ghost element. It's small AoE and the ghost element being in a bad spot (only having advantage over ghost element monsters and having a huge disadvantage against holy, shadow and neutral monsters, make it very niche. I like carrying a swap for this skill if I'm hunting in a map with varied elements, so I can Comet the majority of the monsters and finish them off with SE. The Intense Telekinesis buff is a powerful tool, allowing you to deal massive amounts of damage while active. It's worth remembering that the buff doesn't go away with dispel, but it goes away if the character dies.

The build prerequisites are 72% acd, 193 aspd, and 100% vct for theoretical max spam. As always, do check on training chamber for your accurate value. This skill has no fct, but it's a good idea to have fct due to energy coat and other buffs. The class having access to Berserk Potion greatly helps in achieving the high aspd this skills requires you to have.

Gameplay

The way to play with the class is relatively simple and can be divided according to the content you are doing.

  • In bio5, the goal is to always accompany the tank, so as not to have a problem with the Land Protector (LP) that is used to protect supports. You can achieve this via Guyak Pudding and leaving the ghost monsters to other cleaners, such as Genetics.
  • In end-game intances it's always good to communicate with the other dpsers to stay close to the warlock, because not all the comets are released in the ideal time, due to the drop in fps generated by the large amount of effects on the screen. Also talk to your Sorcerer, so he/she doesn't cast LP in panic when monsters get close to the group.
  • The command /timer is very important, because if configured correctly in NovaEXT (look for the name SI_SPELLBOOK and enable all 7), you will know exactly how many spells you have stored, knowing when to stop and read again
  • Always keep your buffs active (mystical amp and recog spell). I recommend activating at least one of them in nova ext as well.
  • Other important buff to have on your NovaEXT timer are SI_MAGICPOWER (mystical amplification), SI_POEMBRAGI (Poem of Bragi).
  • Keep Energy Coat always active. Not only will the buff make you survive longer, but there is a strange and undocumented -but empirically verified- interaction that at certain points in aspd (189 specifically) your spam is higher with the buff than without it.
  • Don't forget about abilities like Quagmire to give lesser chances of monsters getting close to you and Fire Wall to have an almost guaranteed proc of your boot effects
  • Safety Wall is a defensive skill that can be important too, so always keep some blue gems in your inventory.

Training Chamber

Training Chamber

Okay, so lets talk about the training chamber. This is one of the most useful in-game tools we have access to. To enter the training chamber, go to Prontera and talk to the NPC just left of the centre fountain. You have to pay 100k zeny for 10 mins (it is not free, sadly).

Now, you may be asking yourself, "why should I pay money to enter a training chamber when I can use training dummies for free in the basement of main?" Well, the training chamber offers much more than a few training dummies with which to attack.

  1. You can customize various aspects of the training dummy itself: size, race, element type, element level, boss/normal class, Def, and Mdef,
  2. There's a special Monster Hunter mode, which attempts to emulate the unique properties present during high rank missions, with monster presets for Nysori and Garronath,
  3. It is possible to control many buffs for the player, such as Variable Cast Time, After Cast Delay, stat foods, and damage variance,
  4. All NPCs are available for use in a single place (tool dealer, Kafra storage, stat/skill reset, stat/skill save/load, card removal, etc),
  5. You can check damage per second (DPS), which is useful for MvPs in instances.

So, how are we going to use the training chamber for our benefit? Well, there are two general uses for the training chamber that I want to go over with you. The first is how to customize the training dummy to match the monsters you may be fighting so that you can determine your potential damage. The second is to go over strategies that you can use to determine how much ASPD, ACD and Cast Time reductions you need in order to maximize your damage. This second topic is quite interesting because it is not as deterministic as the first. What I mean by this is that damage per hit is deterministic based on gear, stats, skills, and consumables, while hits per second can vary depending on computer hardware, turbo mode oddities, and latency. Here is another way to think about it: If you loan your gear to a friend, they will have the exact same damage per hit as you, but they may have a slightly different number of hits per second, which influences DPS.

Calibrating Hits Per Second (Important for Chain Lightning)

As mentioned previously, another use for the training chamber is to determine how to maximize your hits per second. What is hits per second? Well it simply means the number of skill hits you use in a second (keep in mind that Chain Lightning actually hits 4 times, so the number of hits for one cast of this spell is 4. Here we do not actually care about damage and DPS (both of which are important, but separate from what we wish to accomplish in this section of the guide). So, it is time to introduce plant mode! What is plant mode? Well it is a unique preset for the training dummy that reduces all incoming damage to one. Using this mode, damage per second (DPS) essentially implies hits per second.


Hits per second is incredibly important for some skills, most notably Chain Lightning and Soul Expansion. I will be using Chain Lighting to help illustrated the importance of hits per second and how it relates to damage, as well as the way in which you can calibrate your hits per second without needing incredible gear.


How to Calibrate/Test Hits Per Second

First, use the monster customization NPC to convert the training dummy into plant mode. Next, make sure you have reduced your FCT by at least 1 second. In my example screenshots below I am using  Automatic Leg B with  Automatic Module (Fixed Casting Time) for 0.8 seconds of FCT reduction and The full Warlock Onyx II costume stone set for an additional 0.5 seconds of FCT reduction. Okay, at this point you can reset your stat points to provide a blank canvas with which to work. From this point on you can play around with changing your ASPD, ACD, and VCT to see how your hits per second change.


How do we manipulate these variables?

  • ACD: Use the Support NPC to change ACD in chunks of 10. For finer granularity you can use any ACD gear that is available to you. Some examples are: Reload Shadow Gear, Sinful Rings, and Reload Costume stones. To be honest, you dont have to check every single possible combination (although if you do I would be interested in the results). Just test enough to convince yourself of some optimal values to aim for.
  • VCT: If you want 100% VCT just use the Support NPC to set your Variable Cast Time to 100%. Otherwise you can use stats to complement the VCT reductions obtainable from the NPC. Its up to you, but I usually like to assume Instant Cast for spammable skills like Chain Lightning.
  • ASPD: This one is tricky. Here is what I did in my examples below (feel free to use your own method): I reset my stats and maxed Agi and Dex for a reasonable baseline of ASPD. The rest went into Int for SP (Chain Lightning is an SP sponge). From here I increase my ASPD in chunks by using three consumables one at a time (Guarana Candy, Enrich Celermine Juice, and Berserk Potion) and also the 20 and 10 stat foods from the Support NPC. You can play around with all of these tools to try out different ASPD settings.

Implications of Hits Per Second

Often times it is tempting to focus solely on Damage per Hit. I hope that I have been able to convince you through examples of the importance of maximizing hits per second for a spell like Chain Lightning. This also applies to Soul Expansion to a lesser degree, and even your ability to release multiple comets at the same time (often called "comet spam"). It is pretty crazy to think that at 179 ASPD going from 90 ACD to 93 ACD literally increases your DPS by 50%. 3 ACD! This is because the after cast delay of Chain Lightning is 3 seconds, so even 3 ACD equates to roughly 0.09 seconds reduced after cast delay. More specifically, 90% ACD reduces after cast delay to 0.3 seconds while 93 ACD reduces after cast delay to 0.21 seconds (~50% reduction). Obviously there are diminishing returns at some point as you approach the maximum hits per second for the spell.

How to Calculate Damage

Calculating Damage

2. Calculating Damage Modifiers

Now lets talk about calculating damage. Have you ever had questions like:

  • "How much better is  Automatic Armor Type B than  Illusion Armor Type B?"
  • "Which piece of gear should I buy next?"
  • "Should I refine my garment to +12 or get better enchants on my accessories?"
  • "What are the best enchants for my  Welding Wand?"

We can answers these types of questions by using a damage calculator like Muh's Damage Calc. But first we need to figure out our MATK, weapon refine, and damage modifiers. There are two general approaches to finding out these numbers:

  1. Using the in-game command @bs and importing the information directly into the calculator.
  2. Building a spreadsheet from scratch and manually inputting these numbers into the calculator.

For the first method I recommend reading Janpai's Matk Calculator Guide. The guide is written for performers but it applies to warlocks as well. In it, Janpai shows you how to get the output of the @bs command, how to import this information into the damage calculator, and then a nice treatment of easy ways to compare pieces of gear.


The remainder of this guide, however, will focus on the second method: filling out a spreadsheet manually from scratch.

Then the full spreadsheet will be presented, followed by explanations on how we got these numbers for each piece of gear. Lastly we put these numbers into the damage calculator and compare the results with actual in-game results to see how accurate we are.

FAQ

FAQ
  • Do combo effects stack?
    • A combo of any kind, be it card or equipment, only activates its bonus effects once. For example, the 17.2 combo with roaming spellbook card, even if you add another one of this card, you'll not get the combo effects, just the base effects.
  • Celine vs Auto Armor?
    • From our tests, we found that Celine Set + Welding is good vs the majority of the monsters, whereas the Illusion Thorn combo + Comet Armor shines vs Angels, Demons and Dragons, due to having access to Racial 5 enchants on Sinful and Brilliant Light Accessories.
  • What automatic modules should I buy?
    • The Magic Magic ACD armor is the most generic one. It's not bad, but skill modules outshine the magic ones in most situations. For example, having access to Comet modules on the armor allows us us to not use the 17.2 card combo for comet, thus gaining us a slot on the weapon, boots and accessory.
    • Another consideration to make is ACD vs Magic vs Shadow Spell as the 3rd module. I'd recommend ACD if you only or mostly play solo, so every bit of acd helps here. For party-play I'd recommend Magic module since it's cheap and the damage difference vs Shadow Spell is about 1% for either module, depending on the rest of the setup. If you are at this point, make use of the calculator skills you've gained in this guide to make the best decision possible.
  • Which gear should I get next?
    • Well this is sort of the whole point of this guide. Use the tools you have learned here to determine which gear you should get next. Calculate the damage difference and compare market prices. For example, if you are using KSDPI and Sinful Ring with Horror Lv 1 enchant as your accessories and you are thinking about upgrading from magic/magic to comet/comet Automatic Armor, I can almost guarantee that upgrading your Sinful Ring to Horror level 5 will yield a greater increase in damage for a fraction of the price.
    • Some things to think about: usually a good weapon with class modifiers (boss/normal) is a huge improvement. Followed by going from Illusion Armor to Automatic Armor. Lastly Garment, going from Fallen Warrior Manteau to Int Manteau is an upgrade in damage, but also quite expensive.
  • Why is Warlock so expensive?
    • Due to the constraints of needing elemental damage multipliers, we need dedicated gears for each build that is of a different element. Eg, comet uses purple pitaya on garment, while chain lightning uses deep sea phen. That is the case for almost all the card slots, making it nigh impossible to share gears if you want to have a swap available.
  • Why is Warlock the best class?
    • Comet has huge AoE and also immediately applies Magic Intox, making your 2nd cast of the skill deal much more damage
