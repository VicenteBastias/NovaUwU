Overview

Why hello there. I'm Yomi and some of you guys might know me ingame as Sara Bloodlily. I've decided to start a guide to introduce players into Trapper Ranger and more or less pass down my knowledge of everything I know about this particular build so far.

Before starting out on Nova, I was an avid Ranger player on officials, since Archer/Hunter was the first class I started playing in RO. I never really payed much attention to the trapping tree though and usually just went with Arrow Storm/Aimed Bolt or the Fear Breeze/Auto-Warg builds.

Coming to Nova, I wanted to try something different and did some reasearch on Trap Ranger before I decided to give this particular build a try.

Some people might think that it's not the most optimal build for the class. Having it played for several months now, I personally feel like it actually adds more versatility to the Ranger Class, since you'll be using it in some kind of combination with another skill tree anyway.

So, if you want to play something that deals high amounts of damage when wearing a bow and boasts of extreme tankiness when wearing a dagger and shield, be my guest and I'll tell you how.

General Information about Traps

Before we start out with the different builds and other stuff for Trapper Rangers, I'll give you an overview on how traps work on NovaRO. Some of this information is applicable for all traps, while other is specific to Hunter or Ranger traps.

General Mechanics

All traps on NovaRO have or at least should have the following mechanics in common:

  • Damage from traps is considered melee and physical
  • You can only place them every two sells ( Talkie Box being the only exception here)
  • Traps can be damaged by certain skills but not directly
  • Traps can be knocked back by a select number of skills
  • Traps cannot be removed by Land Protector and even be placed on it to disturb or damage your enemies
  • Traps can activate HP/SP drain effects from equipment like Hunter Fly Card and  Rideword Hat
  • Traps do not activate HP/SP drain effects from cards/equipment similiar to the Earth Deleter Card, where you need to kill an enemy with a melee attack
  • Traps do not activate effects like the  Hawk's Eye enchantment from Temporal Boots
  • Damage increasing buffs like Odin's Power and Impositio Manus will increase Trap damage slighly
  • Traps last 4 times as long in WoE
  • Traps will affect anyone during WoE/PvP so be careful when placing them since you can damage your allies
  • If the caster is affected by Manhole, placed traps will not activate when walking through them

Specific Mechanics

These trap mechanics are specific to their respective jobs or the traps you're using:

  • Hunter Traps can be removed by any trap removal/destroying skills, while Ranger Traps cannot be removed/destroyed by Crazy Vines, Spring Trap and Remove Trap
  • You can damage yourself with Hunter Traps during WoE/PvP. This does not apply to the Ranger Traps
  • Ranger Traps (apart from Electric Shock and Cluster Bomb) can be placed directly under anyone while Hunter Traps can't
  • Damage blocking skills like Parry, Auto Guard and Weapon Blocking will work against Ranger Traps. Hunter Traps bypass these effects

Trap Damage

Trap damage generally consists of

  • the total ATK you have, which includes ATK from your weapon, ATK from upgrades, buffs or any direct ATK increases from cards or equipment
  • your total INT as well as
  • your total DEX

Each point into INT or DEX will increase your trap damage by ~16 at level 175 and with Trap Research maxed out, regardless of the equipment you're using. Each point of ATK you get will increase your trap damage by 1, which means that 16 ATK = 1 INT/DEX.

To elaborate further on this, we'll take a closer look at one of the main headgears for a Trapper on NovaRO, which is  Autumn Headband [1]. This little baby increases trap damage for Fire Trap and Ice Bound Trap by 50% regardless of it's upgrade. If upgraded to +9, it will increase the damage dealt with those traps by another 25% for 75% total. This basically means that with no upgrade on it, you should get between 24-25 points of damage per INT/DEX and at +9 you'll get between 29-32 damage per INT/DEX. This will also affect your ATK-Value as well so for +7 it would be 1,5 ATK and for +9 1,75 ATK then.

You should therefore prioritize your equipment for PvM the following way to maximize your damage with traps:

  • INT = DEX > Flat ATK > %ATK

Then there's also a slight difference in behaviour for Hunter Traps and Ranger Traps and in which way their damage is dealt:

This is the damage formula for Claymore Trap:

[DEX × (3 + Base_Lv ÷ 100) × (1 + INT ÷ 35)] × Skill_Lv ± 10% + (Trap_Research_Lv × 40)

This is the damage formula for Fire Trap:

ATK 100% + [{(Fire Trap Skill Level x Caster’s DEX) + (INT x 5)} x (1.5 + Caster’s Base Level / 100)] x {(Trap Research Skill Level x 20) / 100 }

Here we can see the following:

  • Ranger trap damage is divided into two parts instead of one. Because of this, Hunter Traps deal specific elemental damage, while Ranger Traps only deal "pseudo-elemental" damage

Now, Hunter Traps deliver the damage dealt in one total portion while Ranger Traps have two different portions in which damage is dealt to the target.

How does all of this information translate into pratical use?

Basically the reason why Hunter Traps will "miss" their target depending on their Element, is because their damage is dealt in one portion and will therefore always have the same element, whereas Ranger Traps deal their damage in two different portions and only one of these, the ATK-Portion, is affected by the traps element. This portion can and will miss depending on the element, but the Stat-Portion of Ranger Traps will never miss, because it bypasses elemental and other modifiers.

Stats

In this section, I'll be going over the stats, giving general information about them and their importance when playing a Trap Ranger. The importance of each stat will be rated with low, mid or high. Because I'll be showcasing some builds later on, most of the stats will probably have two ratings because their importance varies depending on the trapping build you want to use.

  • Low will indicate, that the stat will be somewhere between 1-40 after using stat points
  • Mid will indicate, that the stat will be somewhere between 41-100 after using stat points
  • High will indicate, that the stat will be somewhere between 101-130 after using stat points
Stat Importance General Information Notes
STR Mid
  • Increases ATK when using melee weapons
  • Slightly increases ATK when using ranged weapons
  • Increases Weight Limit
 Because you'll be using a melee weapon most of the time as a Trapper, I suggest that you invest some points into this stat. How many? That's for you to decide. I usually tend to get 90 STR because of the ATK-Bonus that a  Heroic Backpack provides when upgraded accordingly.
AGI Low/High
  • Increases attack speed
  • Increases flee
  • Slightly increases physical defense
 Since trapping relies on your survivability, you won't really need much points into AGI. If you want to go Trapper/Auto-Warg, you should invest as much AGI as possible though.
VIT Mid/High
  • Increases maximum HP
  • Increases physical defense
  • Increases natural HP-Recovery as well as the effectiveness of HP-Recovery items
  • Increases resistance to the following status effects: Stun, Poison, Deadly Poison, Burning, Freezing
  • Slightly increases magical defense
 One of the more essential stats for a Trapper. You want to be as tanky as possible so I'd suggest to invest at least 90 VIT so you can get that  Heroic Backpack bonus to make you even more tankier.
INT Mid/High
  • Increases mATK
  • Increases maximum SP
  • Increases Magical Defense
  • Increases natural SP-Recovery as well as the effectiveness of SP-Recovery items
  • Increases resistance to the following status effects: Blind, Sleep, Chaos
  • Slightly decreases the variable cast time of skills
 Trap Damage for both Hunter and Ranger Traps heavily relies on INT. I suggest you to invest as much points into INT as possible.
DEX Mid/High
  • Increases ATK when using ranged weapons
  • Increases accuracy (Hit-Rate)
  • Decreases variable cast time of skills
  • Slightly increases ATK when using melee weapons
  • Slightly increases mATK and magical defense
 Ranger Traps heavily rely on DEX for damage as well, so investing as much points into DEX as possible would be best to maximize trap damage. Because your DEX will be pretty high and is generally a main stat for any Ranger Build, you'll also be able to use bows as a secondary option for damage if necessary.
LUK Low/Mid
  • Increases critical hit rate
  • Increases resistance to the following status effects: Blind, Curse, Frozen, Poison, Silence, Sleep, Stone Curse, Stun
  • Increases chance of using Blitz Beat or Warg Strike automatically when attacking
  • Increases perfect dodge and critical hit defense (aka Crit Shield)
  • Slightly increases ATK, mATK, Hit and Flee
 I'm not really sure what to say about this one, since you'll probably only be getting points into this depending on the build you're going to run. For a tankier build that doesn't rely on auto-warging, you probably won't be investing any points into this stat.

Skills

In this section, I'll be giving you an overview of all the skills available to Rangers as well as an explanation on how or when you'll be using those skills (Yes, ALL of them~). Skills that are important for a Trapper or a specific Trapper-Build later on will be highlighted with a ☆.

The following format will be used to display general information about the Skill:

Skill Name Levels Type

- Skill Description and some numbers~ -

First Job Skills

Arrow Shower 10 Offensive
  • This skill is a AoE that deals ranged damage and slightly knocks back enemies that are hit. Damage increases with each Level learned of this skill.
  • Usage: PvM only for First and Second/Transcendent job leveling. Becomes irrelevant once you hit Ranger.
Double Strafe 10 Offensive
  • This Skill deals ranged damage to a single target. Damage increases with each Level learned of this skill.
  • Usage: PvM only for First and Second/Transcendent job leveling. Becomes irrelevant once you hit Ranger.
Improve Concentration ☆ 10 Supportive
  • This skill buffs yourself, increasing AGI and DEX by 12% at Level 10. This increase only effects DEX and AGI from stat points, job bonuses, passive skills and equipment bonuses. Card bonuses and other buffs do not count towards this increase. This skill, apart from removing it with Dispell, can also be removed by Quagmire.
  • Usage: PvM/PvP since it's an essential self-buff at every stage of the game.
Owl's Eye ☆ 10 Passive
  • Each point into this skill increases your DEX by 1. Because DEX is a main-stat for Rangers regardless of their build, you want to max this out no matter what.
Vulture's Eye 10 Passive
  • Each point into this skill increases the attack range for bows by 1. Other than that, there's not really much to say about this skill.

Second Job Skills

Ankle Snare ☆ 5 Supportive
  • Sets a trap that will immobilize targets for a set duration when they step into it. The immobilize duration depends on the skill level and the target's AGI. Targets with higher AGI will be immobilized for a shorter duration. The minimum immobilize duration for this trap is 4 seconds. Boss monsters and MVPs can only be immobilized for the minimum duration, regardless of skill level. Targets immobilized by Ankle Snare will receive additional damage from Aimed Bolt.
  • Usage: Good in WoE/PvP because it's great for disrupting enemies on their way through the castle. Because of its interaction with Aimed Bolt, it's also useful in PvM as well as for MVPing or instancing.
Beast Bane 10 Passive
  • Increases your damage against Insect and Brute type monsters by a flat amount. Each level of this skill increases the damage slightly.
Blast Mine 5 Offensive
  • Sets a trap that deals Wind Elemental Damage in a 3x3 area when activated. Damage dealt is dependent on the level of this skill. This trap has a fixed cast time and is therefore not really practical for leveling purposes. I wouldn't say that it's impossible to level with this skill though since it still deals a good chunk of damage against Water Elemental Enemies.
  • Usage: Good for one-shotting lower level Water Elemental Enemies but doesn't have much use apart from that.
Blitz Beat 5 Offensive
  • This skill commands your falcon to attack in a 3x3 area around the selected target. The level of this skill affects the number of hits your falcon will do. Falcon damage heavily relies on INT and to some extend also on DEX. The Level learned of Steel Crow also determines how much damage your falcon will deal. This skill has a chance to auto-cast which increases depending on your LUK-Stat when using auto attacks. The level of auto-cast Blitz Beat depends on your Job-Level and also on the level you actually have this skill learned on. As a Ranger you will get a way better version of this skill which will be using a warg.
  • Usage: Because of the warg skills you can get as a Ranger, this skill is basically obsolete and doesn't have any use for PvM/PvP. While leveling your Hunter/Sniper, using Arrow Shower or Focused Arrow Strike are more efficient. If you want to have some fun with an auto-attack build early on though, I'd recommend you get this then.
Claymore Trap 5 Offensive
  • Sets a trap that deals Fire Elemental Damage in a 5x5 area when activated. Damage dealt is dependent on the level of this skill. This trap has a fixed cast time and is therefore not really practical for leveling purposes. I wouldn't say that it's impossible to level with this skill though since it still deals a good chunk of damage against Earth Elemental Enemies. Apart from that, this trap is also able to destroy any traps as long as they're inside it's explosion area. If you plan on using this trap to level up, you should place them far enough apart as to not let have one destroy another.
  • Usage: Good for one-shotting lower-mid level Earth Elemental Enemies but doesn't have much use apart from that.
Detect 4 Supportive
  • This skill commands your falcon to scout an area for hidden or cloaked enemies. This skill will not detect enemies that are using a skill where they cannot be unhidden. The range of this skill increases with each Level.
  • Usage: Because you'll be using a Warg most of the time during the later stages of the game, this skill is pretty useless (because no falcon and stuff~ Otherwise it's pretty ok).
Falconry Mastery ☆ 1 Passive
  • This skill enables the use of a falcon. It is also necessary because it's a requirement to unlock the skill True Sight
Flasher 5 Supportive
  • Sets a trap at a target location that will blind enemies that activate it. The chance to blind an enemy increases with each level of this skill.
  • Usage: This trap is one of the more not so useful ones besides Sandman and Skid Trap. You won't use this in PvM and people that PvP or WoE will most likely have a resistance against the blind status effect.
Freezing Trap 5 Offensive
  • Sets a trap that deals Water Elemental Damage in a 3x3 area when activated. This trap also has a chance of inflicting the frozen status to enemies in it's AoE. Duration and chance of the frozen status increase with each level. The damage of this trap, compared to Blast Mine and Claymore Trap is pretty low though, so I wouldn't really recommend to use it for leveling purposes.
  • Usage: Not really good for PvM since it's damage is pretty low. Because most people use armors compounded with a Marc Card during WoE, it's not really useful there as well unless you get lucky and someone without such an armor actually activates the trap.
Land Mine 5 Offensive
  • Sets a trap that deals Earth Elemental Damage when activated. This trap also has a chance of stunning the enemy that activated it. I wouldn't really recommend to use it for leveling purposes because it's damage compared to Blast Mine and Claymore Trap is slightly lower and it's also not an AoE trap. It should still be able to one shot lower level Wind Elemental Enemies.
  • Usage: Decent for PvM at least against Wind Elemental Enemies but comes with the drawback of not being an AoE-Trap. Because most people have resistance to stun in PvP/WoE, it's not really useful there as well unless you get lucky and someone without that resistance actually activates the trap.
Remove Trap ☆ 1 Supportive
  • Enables you to remove traps when using this skill. With Remove Trap you're only able to remove Hunter Traps though, which makes this skill kind of obsolete for most situations. Since you'll be using Ankle Snare to defend as castle during WoE though, it is still important enough to get it (in case you accidentally trap someone of your guild or need to remove your trap carpet).
  • Usage: Not really useful for PvM. The only time you're going to use it in PvM is for removing your Talkie Boxes after you're finished with tanking stuff. Otherwise it's still a relatively reliable Skill during WoE/PvP in some situations.
Sandman 5 Supportive
  • Sets a trap that when activated induces sleep on the target with a certain chance. This chance increases with each Level of this skill.
  • Usage: A bit more useful than Flasher and Skid Trap, but you still won't be using this in PvM or PvP/WoE as much as other traps. It's still good enough for use during PvP/WoE because targets with low AGI are vulnerable to the sleep status.
Shockwave Trap ☆ 5 Supportive
  • Sets a Trap that when activated will drain SP from the target. At Level 5, this Trap will drain 80% of the targets Max SP.
  • Usage: Again another Trap that's pretty useless for PvM but it's actually one of the few good ones for WoE or PvP. You can use this Trap directly in front of Barricades , around Guardian Stones or the Emperium in WoE to hinder people to break them for a short amount of time, since they won't be able to use Skills unless they have SP to spare.
Skid Trap 5 Supportive
  • Sets a trap that will knockback the target that activates it. The knockback direction depends on how the target walks onto the trap. Knockback distance is dependent on this skills level.
  • Usage: This trap is one of the more not so useful ones besides Sandman and Flasher. You won't use this in PvM since Boss Monsters or MVPs can't be knocked back and during WoE knockbacks are also disabled.
Spring Trap 5 Supportive
  • Commands your falcon to destroy traps from a distance. The range of this skill increases with each level learned. This skill only works against Hunter Traps.
  • Usage: Again, because you won't be using a falcon as a Ranger this skill doesn't have any practical use and it's pretty much irrelevant when you're leveling your Hunter/Sniper.
Steel Crow 10 Passive
  • Increases the damage your falcon deals to enemies.
Talkie Box ☆ 1 Supportive
  • Sets a trap that when activated, will replay any message you typed into it before placing it. I like to call it "The Life Insurance Trap". As I explained in the General Mechanics section of this guide already, traps can be damaged by different sources or skills but never directly. Because traps have this mechanic, as long as you place them somewhere where they can get hit by the AoE of Fire Traps, you can use them to heal yourself up when using Hunter Fly Cards and/or a  Rideword Hat. Because Talkie Box lasts 10 minutes and basically has infinite HP unlike other traps, it's the perfect candidate for the purpose of self healing. Oh, and you can also stack this trap on the same cell without having to worry to search for a free cell to place it on.
  • Usage: Mostly for PvM/MvPing to be able to tank hard hitting mobs for longer periods of time.

Transcendent Job Skills

Falcon Assault 5 Offensive
  • Commands your falcon to deal one heavy hit to the enemy. Damage is calculated the same way as Blitz Beat and also has a separate damage modifier that increases with each level learned of this Skill. Depending on the element your weapon/arrow has, this skill can also ignore damage reducing equipments/cards like Raydric Card.
  • Usage: If you plan on using this to level up your Sniper then go ahead. Mind you, that the SP-Cost of this skill is pretty high though and I'd therefore not advise on using it, since there are other ways to level more efficiently. And again, because you won't be using a falcon as a Ranger, this skill won't be mandatory for you.
Focused Arrow Strike 5 Offensive
  • Shoots a piercing arrow at your target that will hit it, as well as all enemies in a 3x12 area. This skill has a 2 second cast time and a 20% chance to critically hit enemies damaged by this skill. Your own crit rate affects the critical hit chance of this skill as well.
  • Usage: For PvM when leveling your Sniper but becomes obsolete once you are a Ranger. Be wary when using this skill though because if the cast time gets interupted by anything, it will put you in an animation lock and you'll need to move to be able to cast again, making Arrow Shower a safer option for leveling.
True Sight ☆ 10 Supportive
  • This skill increases your ATK, hit and crit rate as well as all primary stats by 5. The duration of the buff is 30 seconds and it has no cast time. It's one of the few Self-Buffs with which you are able to increase your trap damage directly.
  • Usage: PvM/PvP since it's an essential Self-Buff once you're a Sniper/Ranger.
Wind Walk ☆ 10 Supportive
  • This skill increases your and your allies movement speed and flee while active. The buff of this skill can be removed by Quagmire but not by Decrease Agility (and vice versa) and doesn't stack with Increase Agility. Because of the movement speed increasing effect, it is one of the more essential skills for a Sniper/Ranger in general. It's especially important for a Trapper because Ranger Traps can only be placed in melee range. In combination with the skill Warg Rider, you'll be able to move almost as fast as a Genetic with Cart Boost active.
  • Usage: Good for PvM to enhance your own movement speed. Also good for WoE/PvP in case there is no Arch Bishop around to buff your allies with Canto Candidus.

Third Job Skills

Aimed Bolt ☆ 10 Offensive
  • This skill deals high damage to a single target. If the target is/has been immobilized by Ankle Snare, Electric Shock or Warg Bite, this skill will deal additional hits with the same amount of damage depending on the targets size. If you're going for a Trap/Bow-Ranger build, this skill will be your secondary source of damage but only for specific situations, where tanking an MVP or Instance Boss isn't really an option for you.
  • Usage: Mainly for PvM and MVPing. It's also pretty good in WoE or PvP as long as you manage to immobilize your target and also have the right equipment.
Arrow Storm ☆ 10 Offensive
  • This skill deals high damage to the target and other enemies in the area around it. If the target(s) are not immobilized, this skill will generally deal more damage than Aimed Bolt. Depending on what equipment you can afford, this can also be a viable secondary damage source besides your traps but again, only for specific situations.
  • Usage: Mainly used for leveling up your Ranger but can also be used for MVPing if your equipment is good enough. During WoE this skill is also a great option for breaking barricades.
Camouflage 5 Supportive
  • This skill puts you in stealth, increasing your ATK and Crit-Rate for your next auto-attack or skill. Using higher levels of this skill enables you to walk around while it is active. If you get hit by any skills or attacks, this skill will deactivate. Basically the Ranger-Version of Cloaking.
  • Usage: Great for PvM to reposition yourself and hide from enemies that aren't bosses or Insects/Demons. Decent for WoE/PvP to increase the damage of the next skill you're going to use. Sadly, not really important for a trapping oriented build.
Cluster Bomb ☆ 5 Offensive
  • Sets a trap that deals high damage in a 7x7 area when activated. You can have a maximum of 3 traps of this type placed at once. Unlike Fire Trap, Ice Bound Trap and the elemental changing traps, you cannot place this trap directly under enemies.
  • Usage: Because you cannot place this trap directly under enemies and it only vanishes after 2 seconds have passed, it isn't really good for PvM when compared with Fire Trap. It is still useful during WoE/PvP considering a little trick that I'll be explaining later in this guide.
Cobalt Trap 1 Supportive
  • Sets a trap that changes the element of enemies hit to Water. This trap can be placed directly under enemies. The effect does not work against Boss or MVP-Monsters and also not against players.
  • Usage: If anything, you can only use this trap for PvM and nothing else. I personally haven't played with this trap because I don't see any use for it, since Ranger Traps deal pseudo-elemental damage, but it might be good in a party setup if you need this specific element.
Detonator ☆ 1 Supportive
  • Allows the remote activation of traps that you have placed. Only affects the following Traps: Blast Mine, Claymore Trap, Sandman, Talkie Box, Cluster Bomb, Fire Trap and Ice Bound Trap.
  • Usage: Great for removing traps that get knocked back during PvM by skills from enemies, since you're only able to place a limited number of each Ranger Trap. This point also takes into consideration that they might be too far away to be removed with Sensitive Keen. You can also do some gimmicky stuff during PvP and WoE that requires you to detonate all traps at the same time.
Electric Shock ☆ 5 Supportive
  • Sets a trap that immobilizes the first enemy to walk into it, gradually draining their SP until this traps effect ends. The duration of said immobilization depends on this skills level and the targets AGI and VIT-Stats. The activation area of this trap is 3x3. You can have a maximum of 3 traps of this type placed at once. Unlike Fire Trap, Ice Bound Trap and the elemental changing traps, you cannot place this trap directly under enemies and it can also not be knocked back.
  • Usage: Mainly used for PvP/WoE since it combines Ankle Snare and Shockwave Trap but comes with the drawback that you're only able to place 3 of these at once.
Fear Breeze 5 Supportive
  • When activated, enables you to shoot additional arrows when using ranged attacks. The number of additional hits depends on this skills level. Each number of additional hits have their own chance of being triggered.
  • Usage: Mainly used for PvM when going for a ASPD/Warg-Build with bows, which we will not be covering in this guide.
Fire Trap ☆ 5 Offensive
  • Places a trap that damages enemies in a 5x5 area and also inflicts the burning status. This trap can be placed directly under enemies. The burning status does not affect Boss or MVP-Monsters. This is basically the most important skill for a Trapper, since you'll be using this trap all the time. It does a pretty decent amount of damage and also applies the burning status effect to enemies hit with a 100% chance at level 5. Because traps placed with this skill vanish instantly when activated, trapping speed should be heavily reliant on your attack speed, which is only half the truth though. This trap has a slight cooldown (should be around 0.2 or 0.3 seconds) but because of the animation delay when placing a trap, you are only able to place one of these traps every 0.5 seconds (~160 attack speed needed) when you don't get hit by attacks. If you get hit by any attacks or multi-hit skills, the animation of you receiving damage will cancel out the animation you're in when placing/after having placed a trap, therefore allowing for you to trap as fast as your attack speed allows you too.
  • Usage: Mostly for PvM and MVPing. PvP and WoE for barricade breaking or as a supportive trap to remove crystalize.
Full Throttle ☆ 5 Supportive
  • When activated, fully recovers your HP/SP and boosts all of your base main stats by 20% but has a 30 minute cooldown. Besides Improve Concentration and True Sight, this skill is basically your only way to boost your trap damage temporarily.
  • Usage: Mostly PvM for a short damage boost
Ice Bound Trap ☆ 5 Offensive
  • Sets a trap that damages enemies in a 5x5 area and also inflicts the freezing status. This trap can be placed directly under enemies. The freezing status does not affect Boss or MVP-Monsters. This trap has the same cooldown as Fire Trap and the chance of inflicting the freezing status is 100% at Level 5, but because it only vanishes one second after being activated, it is not really recommended to use this for leveling unless you need to slow down enemies. It is still a great trap for WoE or PvP because Fire Trap sadly removes the crystallization status from enemies and as to prevent that, you'd use this one instead.
  • Usage: Mainly for WoE and PvP because of it's vanishing delay.
Magenta Trap 1 Supportive
  • Sets a trap that changes the element of enemies hit to Fire. This trap can be placed directly under enemies. The effect does not work against Boss or MVP-Monsters and also not against players.
  • Usage: If anything, you can only use this trap for PvM and nothing else. I personally haven't played with this trap because I don't see any use for it, since Ranger Traps deal pseudo-elemental damage, but it might be good in a party setup if you need this specific element.
Maze Trap ☆ 1 Supportive
  • Sets a trap that changes the element of enemies hit to Earth. This trap can be placed directly under enemies. The effect does not work against Boss or MVP-Monsters and also not against players.
  • Usage: If anything, you can only use this trap for PvM and nothing else. I personally haven't played with this trap so far because even though it would increase Fire Trap damage slightly so that you can deal the maximum damage possible at all times, it doesn't work against MVP's which is kind of a drawback. Nevertheless it might be good in a party setup if you need this specific element.
Ranger Main 10 Passive
  • Increases your attack power and resistance to Brute, Fish and Plant-Type monsters by a flat amount.
Sensitive Keen ☆ 5 Supportive
  • This skill removes any traps and reveals hidden enemies around the user. There's also a chance that Warg Bite will be used automatically after activating this Skill. This is basically your only way to remove your own Ranger Traps to be able to pick them up again, since Remove Trap only works on Hunter Traps. If you don't mind destroying your own traps, you can skip this and just use Detonator instead to remove your Ranger Traps.
  • Usage: This is basically more of a PvP/WoE skill because of how it works. If you get blocked by traps you can remove them fast and efficiently with this. Revealing enemies with a chance to immobilize them with Warg Bite (if it even auto casts after using it) is nice as well but you shouldn't put your expectation up too high for this to happen.
Tooth Of Warg ☆ 10 Passive
  • Increases the ATK of damage-skills that you can use with your warg. Also enhances some of the extra effects of warg related skills. You should consider getting this for the Auto-Warg/Trapper build even if it's only a slight damage increase.
Trap Research ☆ 5 Passive
  • Increases the damage you deal with traps significantly. Also increases your INT and MaxSP with each Level. You need to get this when you're playing a Trapper.
Unlimit ☆ 5 Supportive
  • Increases ranged damage significantly at the cost of all your defense. This buff has a duration of 1 minute, a cooldown of 5 minutes and is basically a must-have for the Aimed Bolt/Trapper build and all other Ranger builds that use Bows as their main weapon.
  • Usage: Can be used for both PvM and PvP to deal absurd amounts of damage for a short period of time.
Verdure Trap 1 Supportive
  • Sets a trap that changes the element of enemies hit to Wind. This trap can be placed directly under enemies. The effect does not work against Boss or MVP-Monsters and also not against players.
  • Usage: If anything, you can only use this trap for PvM and nothing else. I personally haven't played with this trap because I don't see any use for it, since Ranger Traps deal pseudo-elemental damage, but it might be good in a party setup if you need this specific element.
Warg Bite ☆ 5 Offensive
  • Commands your warg to deal moderate damage to a single target with a chance of immobilizing it. The chance of immobilization depends on this skills level, the level learned of Tooth Of Warg as well as the targets AGI-Stat and can't go below 50% chance.
  • Usage: I haven't tested this skill for PvM so far since tanking most stuff as a Trapper is always the better option, but this is definitely a great skill for WoE/PvP. If you immobilize someone with this and follow it up with Aimed Bolt, depending on your and their equip you might be able to one-shot your target.
Warg Dash 1 Supportive
  • Commands your warg to run into one direction until you hit a wall or an enemy. If you hit an enemy, this skill will deal damage in a 3x3 area. Damage is dependent on ATK, weight and the learned level of Tooth Of Warg. Movement speed of this skill depends on how fast you were when activating it. If you get hit or rooted by anything, this skill will still be active and you need to cancel it yourself to do anything again.
  • Usage: You can basically skip this. I personally haven't found any use for this skill so far but if there is, feel free to let me know.
Warg Mastery ☆ 1 Passive
  • Enables the summoning/use of a warg. Usually a mandatory skill to get for a Ranger because a warg does everything better than a falcon.
Warg Rider ☆ 3 Supportive
  • Enables you to ride your warg. This skill is pretty much a mandatory get, especially for a Trapper. Not only are you able to move around really fast when you combine this skill with Wind Walk, you're also able to use most trapping skills while riding your warg. So apart from buffing yourself with Improve Concentration, True Sight and Wind Walk, there'll never be really a need to dismount from your warg.
  • Usage: Good for PvM and PvP, since being able to move around faster always has it's perks
Warg Strike ☆ 5 Offensive
  • Commands you warg to deal moderate damage to a single target. This is the secondary damage skill you'll be using when running a Trapper/Warger build. Reuse-Speed of this skill is dependent on your attack speed , so the more attack speed you have the faster you can use this skill. Damage is slightly dependent on the current level of Tooth Of Warg learned. Since this skill functions the same way as Blitz Beat, there's also a chance of casting this skill when using auto attacks. Drain effects from Hunter Fly Cards and  Rideword Hat work as well when using this skill. While being a crucial part for the Trapper/Warger build, this skill is also your free "get out of jail" card. Since you'll be riding your Warg almost all the time as a Trapper, you can use this skill to basically get out of hit-lock from several mobs, or if you're rooted by something like Thorn Trap to "move around".
  • Usage: Mostly during PvM to deal damage with it either by normal use or as an auto cast. Otherwise for PvM and PvP to move around in certain situations

Quest/Platinum Job Skills

Arrow Crafting 1 Supportive
  • Enables you to create arrows from various items. Some arrows are only obtainable through this skill.
  • Usage: Great if you are in need of arrows and have some materials at hand which can be used for crafting.
Arrow Repel 1 Offensive
  • Deals low damage to a single target and pushes it back by 6 cells
  • Usage: None
Phantasmic Arrow 1 Offensive
  • Deals damage to a single target without consuming an arrow and pushes it back by 3 cells. Basically a weaker version of Arrow Repel.
  • Usage: None

Soul Link Skills

Beast Strafe 1 Offensive
  • An advanced form of the skill Double Strafe that deals moderate damage to Insect or Brute monsters. Can only be used after Double Strafe.
  • Usage: Because it's a skill that requires you to be under the effect of Hunter Spirit, it's a bit impractical unless you have a Soul Linker on a second account. You could use this instead of Double Strafe for leveling during the Hunter/Sniper stages though.
