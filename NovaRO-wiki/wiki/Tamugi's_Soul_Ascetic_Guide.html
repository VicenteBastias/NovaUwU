Introduction

Yahoo!

This is a guide made for Soul Ascetic which has very little representation in that regard, despite being a fantastically fun and amazingly strong class.


I'm just another enjoyer of the Class, and someone who has stuck around it ever since Soul Linker existed (even through the dark ages of Soul Reaper). While I don't know everything, I hope this will be helpful to you.

Why Soul Ascetic?

Fun aside, Soul Ascetic will always be looked for in parties for their buffs, along with their cleaning abilities making multiple SA's in a party a viable choice too. They're among the strongest farming classes in the game, not only being able to kill lots of mobs in short amounts of time with their abilities, but also needing few healing and SP potions for it.

For both Solo and Party, they're an incredible class to have.

Pros:

  • Strong AoE Abilities
  • Amazing Buffs for self and Party
  • Arguably strongest mobility in the game with High Jump
  • High Survivability with various buffs
  • S Tier Outfits with Soul Linker and Soul Ascetic on both Male and Female Characters
  • Little to no need for Consumables while farming
  • Can use almost all Elements in the game
  • Versatile

Cons:

  • Very difficult for newbies due to gear requirements
  • Limited Skill Points
  • Bit of a learning curve to understand all its abilities
  • High Jump disabled in any content that also disables Teleport, ala Instances and some Dungeons like Bio 5, leaving you with your Mount only
  • Some of the AoE Spells are fairly short range as they're focused on your Character
  • Bad class for MH till we get 4th classes in there

Skills

These skill Builds are based around Soul Ascetic. Use the skill as you need for leveling. (Mostly relevant for the DMG skills of the individual classes)

Taekwon

 











Warm Wind is the Skill we use to change our Element, the order based on skill level is as follows:

  1. Earth
  2. Wind
  3. Water
  4. Fire
  5. Ghost
  6. Shadow
  7. Holy

As this skill casts instantly upon using, you will have to set up all 7 of them on individual Hotkeys. In order to back to Neutral, you have to take off your Weapon. This may not work in some Patches, making you unable to swap back. ( This is the case currently)

An easy way to remember the first 4 levels is that each Element counters the higher leveled one. Earth > Wind > Water > Fire, only works if you already know the order in which Elements counter each other however. Otherwise this is a good way to remember that if you remember the Skill order first.

This skill works with all of our attack skills BUT Curse Explosion from Soul Reaper, which is forced Shadow. More about that in the next section though.


Tumbling is a great defensive tool that lets you dodge ranged and magic attacks at a 20% chance automatically. Remember to reactivate it upon reskilling though, easy to do as this buff is not dispelled upon death even.

Sprint and High Jump are your two Mobility skills. High Jump has a relatively small cast time and no after cast delay, so it can be spammed based on your ASPD. If there are no obstacles in your way, this is the fastest way to go through a map without Teleporting. Mobs can block your Jump if they're on the Square you would land on. This skill can pass through any walls as long as your range is far enough. This skill is also disabled in any place that disables Teleport, like Instances and Bio 5. You can also macro "/bingbing" and "/bangbang" to change the direction your character is looking, thus changing the direction you jump towards. Wether you use this, movement or both is both situational and preference, really.

Sprint, on the other hand, is not quite as useful due to it being barely faster than Mount + Agi. You can however Sprint and Mount up while sprinting, giving you more speed than either individually. That is annoying to stop however because you need to dismount first and then stop Sprint. It may also bug out at times, stopping you completely and forcing you to end and restart Sprint.

Happy Break needs to be maxed ( which is why Sprint is left on 9) due to two of our items being based on this Skill ( Taekwon Shadow Shield and Upper Costume Stone, more on this later).

All Kick Skills are unusable for Soul Linkers and thus not of interest for us. You can use them for leveling however as a Taekwon and later change it. Peaceful Break and Kihop are needed for us to skill Warm Wind.

Soul Linker

 












Class Links:

Most of them are pointless nowadays, making you able to ignore them to the most part. There are a few however that still come in nicely, and others that are a must have. They're called Links / Souls usually.

For the Must Haves:

  • Bard and Dancer Spirit / Performer Spirit: Makes them able to use Skills from the other 2nd class. Trouveres are able to use Bragi, while Troubadours can go SP or Crit Song. If there is only one of them in the party, make sure to give them this buff.
  • Soul Linker Spirit: Makes Soul Ascetics able to use Kaahi, Kaizel and Kaupe on allies. All 3 of these skills are strong survival skills that we will look into in a moment. If you have 2 Soul Ascetics in a party, talk real quick about which of you wants to take a more supportive role ( if not both of you) and hand out the buff accordingly. You cannot use this skill on yourself.
  • Taekwon Master Spirit: This skill can remain on Level 1 as Sky Emperors lose this Link the moment they start flying. You can give them any of the Soul Reaper Links after. However, listen to your Sky Emperor wether they want this Link in the first place, as not all of them use the skill due to its massive drawback.

For the Nice To Have's:

  • Super Novice Spirit: Allows Hyper Novices to use gear they usually would be unable to wear. Most Novices that play around that however tend to have their own Link Character on the ready. I'm also uncertain how relevant this still is for Hyper Novices to be honest.
  • Rogue Spirit: Some Abyss Chasers like this buff as it makes them faster while tunneled and immune to Dispell. Usually more of a PvP Link I imagine, but it does have its uses in PvE aswell.
  • Sage Spirit: I've been asked once by a Professor for this Link in Monster Hunter. Only happened once, but I imagined it's not a bad idea to mention.


Kaahi, Kaizel and Kaupe are skills you can only use on yourself, other Soul Ascetics and Family Members unless you are Linked with your own Class Spirit by another Soul Ascetic.

Kaahi heals you for 1400 HP each time you're physically attacked by enemies (no Cooldown or Delay inbetween) at the cost of 35 SP each time. The SP drawback is by far not as bad anymore with our 10k+ SP pools, while the 1400 HP are sadly not as strong as they used to be anymore. Still a very nice Skill to have and helps your overall survival a lot. When given your Class Spirit, do not blindly use it on everyone as some people may get their SP drained a lot more than they expect, I usually only use it on other Magic Classes with big SP pools. This Skill gets a slight cooldown and has its heal doubled in Monster Hunter. It can also be applied to others there with the help of Soul Unity, a Soul Reaper skill. Use Soul Unity first, then Kaahi on yourself to apply it to everyone.

Kaizel is an instant self revive. Upon Dying, you gain back 70% of your HP and a Kyrie Eleison Shield for 3 seconds. Upon reviving, if you do not move, you cannot be attacked again as if you just teleported into an area. Just like with Teleport, you're immediatly free to be attacked again once you take an action. Even with you get instantly revived, you lose all your buffs, Souls, AP and 1% EXP due to dying. The casttime of this Skill can also not be reduced with stats, and only with % VCTR. It's a good safety net, but do not rely on it too much. If you have an Osiris Card, you can use it to regenerate your SP and HP entirely with Kaizel, if that is something you wish to do. The buff itself lasts for 30 minutes ( or until you die), so you do not have to refresh it all that often. With your Class Link, just put that skill on everyone at any point. This skill is changed into a Kyrie Eleison effect for 60 seconds upon dropping below 30% HP, and also gets a massive cooldown of 5 minutes.

Kaupe makes you immune against the next attack you take. Sounds broken in theory, used to be very good aswell, but with us taking on a lot of mobs at once nowadays, with all of them also attacking quite rapidly, this skill lost a lot of its value. Some skills can even ignore it, though that is quite a rare occurence, I've only noticed it in Ancient Juperos 3 thus far. Skills like Earth Quake also easily penetrate through it, sadly. Still a decent skill to have that can be quite helpful at times. This skill is spamable based on your movement, you will have to cancel it's animation to use it again.


Eska and Eske are skills pretty much for Monster Hunter only. If you do not play Monster Hunter, you can essentially ignore these skills and put the points elsewhere.

Eska decreases ASPD and Movement Speed of the enemy by a bit. It does also increase its defense, but that should not be an issue as everyone should have Full Pen.

Eske is changed for Monster Hunter specifically. It has a cooldown of 28 seconds and increases the magical damage taken by the target from your Warm Wind Element by 2% or 10% for Soul Ascetics. Lasts for 35 seconds. As this skill does not increase the damage Monsters do in MH, you do not have to worry about that.


Kaina simply increases your SP pool a bit if you don't have skillpoints to spend elsewhere.

Eswoo is a weaker version of a 3rd class skill, Esha, so you don't have to worry about this unless you want to make monsters smaller for the fun of it. ( Unsure wether that affects their size directly, which would be relevant for damage reasons, as most DPS classes use Medium and Large Size dmg, gonna try at a later point)

Kaite PvP only skill. Using this in PvE is actually griefing as you increase the physical damage you ( or others) take by 400% with no benefit whatsoever. "High Level" mobs ignore the reflection, essentially removing that part of the skill quite early.


Estin, Estun and Esma are Soullinkers damage skills. Esma is the only of which that deals damage, but you need to use either Estin or Estun beforehand in order to use it. It's spamable as far as you have ASPD and casttime reduction, but these skills are vastly outmatched by Espa, Eshwoo and Curse Explosion, the damage skills of Soul Reaper. Use these for leveling your Soul Linker and nothing else.

Soul Reaper

 












Soul Gathering gets you a Soul every 20 seconds. Technically not needed to max, as we have Soul Gathering with Soul Ascetic now, but there isn't much else to put points into.

Soul Energy increases the amount of Souls you can have from 5 to 20. Must Max.

Soul Reaper: Prerequisite for Espa. Needed for leveling as a Soul Reaper, max it out then, don't use it as a Soul Ascetic at all as you do not need it for Soul Generation anymore and its animation does lag people out.

Esha: Useful skill to have as it is a strong AoE slow. Helps a lot with kiting MVPs / large groups of mobs.

Espa: Main DMG Skill for Soul Reaper. No ACD, so spam is entirely based on your casttime and ASPD. Needs one second Fixed Cast time reduction. Single Target. I continue skilling it as I don't have anything else to put points into, and it can still be quite useful to farm weaker mobs due to its spamability + range + single target. Takes one Soul to cast until it's level 10. This skill is affected by Warm Wind.

Eshwoo: One of the 2 AOE DPS skills SR does have, one second cooldown, 0,5 seconds ACD (ignoreable to the most part), need to use Espa once before being able to use this. Pretty much only a skill for leveling Soul Reaper. Takes 2 Souls to cast until Level 10, at which point it takes 1 only. This skill is affected by Warm Wind.

Curse of Wicked Souls: An AOE debuff that increases Shadow Damage enemies take by 100% or 20% for bosses. This applies to all sources, so other peoples damage also increases if they use shadow. Despite being a strong debuff, due to Shadow not being quite that good for most PvE content (being weak against Shadow, Poison and Undead elements, all 3 of which can be commonly found in enemies), its use is limited. However, when opportunity does arise, it's an incredible skill to have. This skill also increases damage dealt by Curse Explosion and Exorcism of Wicked Souls (SA skill). Takes 3 Souls to cast.

Curse Explosion: The other AOE spell of Soul Reaper, forced Shadow Element, meaning Warm Wind does not affect this skill, 1 second cooldown. These 2 details are the reason why the skill is quite bad. With the new Elemental Tables, it has gained some use for leveling in the earlier stages of Soul Reaper, however. Can be used in areas like Juperos, for example. You do not need to necessarly use Curse of Wicked Souls beforehand, even if it does increase the damage significantly. Does not take Souls to cast at any point, making it easier to use for the early levels of SR.

Soul Unity: This skill is a 9x9 AOE buff for yourself and allies that heals you for 1050 HP every 3 seconds. It also allows you to use Kaute on them. With its 4 seconds Fixed Cast Time, low range and low healing, it's a fairly bad skill in normal PvE. I only really use it while soloing for a bit more healing across the board if I find myself driven into a corner. In Monster Hunter, however, this skill is a lot more important as it allows you to use Kaahi on others. As SP is not an issue in MH, the SP drawback of Kaahi is ignoreable, leaving you with nothing other than a 2800 heal each time you get attacked. Use Soul Unity with everyone in range first, then use Kaahi on yourself to apply it to everyone. Costs 10 Souls, painful as Soul Reaper, not a problem as Soul Ascetic.

Kaute: Sacrifices 20% of your HP to regenerate 20% SP. Costs 5 souls. As soul generation is no problem for SA's, this skill essentially gives you infinite SP. Technically useable on others when they're under the effect of Soul Unity, but not exactly the most useful (or easiest) thing there is.

Soul Resolve: Allows you to remove Souls / Spirits / Links from yourself or other party members. Mostly useful when you've put a wrong Link on someone. Or if you really love your Solo Performer, you can give them their Class Link, then after they've buffed, remove it and give them Golem / Fairy / Falcon Soul, depending on their preferences.


Souls: As they're still Links / Spirits, you cannot apply them together with Class Links. Exception to that is Monster Hunter, more on that in a bit.

  • Golem Soul: The Bread and Butter of Links. No one with special wishes? No idea who to give what? Slap Golem Soul on them (and yourself), it's quite a strong defensive buff and always useful to have.
  • Shadow Soul: Increases Crit by 20 and ASPD by 3. Crit classes may request this buff at times. This buff is also good for soloing / DPSing thanks to the 3 ASPD, both for leveling Soul Reaper with Espa and for Talisman DPS builds.
  • Fairy Soul: An Okay buff. 50 MATK are not that big of a damage boost, and 10% VCTR can be useful, but tend not to be unless it gets you actual instant cast. In party play, Golem is mostly prefered, for soloing either Golem or Shadow tend to be better, aswell. If the 50 MATK give you more secure oneshots however, there is nothing much speaking against it. Or if you already maxed out ASPD / play an Exorcism build and don't feel you need the Defense of Golem Soul either, it does well to increase your dmg just a little. Some Magic Classes may request this soul.
  • Falcon Soul: Similar as Fairy Soul, the 50ATK don't exactly do all that much. Some Physical Classes may still request this Soul.


Soul Division and Soul Explosion: PvP only skills. As I do not play PvP, I will not cover them.

Soul Ascetic

This is not an actual build, it just shows all the skills in their colours.

 















I will Split the Skills into 3 sections : Talisman Buffs, Talisman DPS and Souls. There is also the Totem of Tutelary that is kind of a standalone skill, but very important, so I will cover it here real quick:

Totem of Tutelary

Freely place a Totem Tree anywhere on the ground. While you and your party members stand in it, you regenerate a few thousand HP and few hundred SP every 3 seconds. Just like with Talisman of Protection, the healing may be increased by CRT (Creative Parameter), but the difference is too insignificant to actually put points into it. Its Cooldown is shorter than its duration, but that does not mean you can place 2 Totems at the same time. Also, just like Kaizel, the variable cast time of this skill is not reduceable by stats.

Most importantly with this skill is that it improves two of your other skills while you stand in it: Soul of Heaven and Earth and Exorcism of Malicious Souls. More about this in their respective skill descriptions.

Talisman Buffs

(The ones on the right side)

These skills do not have any limitations as to how many of them you can put on a person, nor are they at odds with Links. Meaning you can use both Links and Talisman buffs together.

  • Talisman Mastery: A passive skill that increases your S.MATK by one every skill level, also increases the healing of Totem of Tutelary, Talisman of Protection and increases the damage of every DPS Talisman Skill.
  • Talisman of Protection: A useful healing skill that, depending on the level of it and Talisman Mastery, heals around 8 - 12k every 3 seconds. Can be used on self and party members. It mentions that CRT (Creative Parameter) increases its healing, but even on 100CRT the difference is only 700 healing per 3 seconds, so completely ignoreable. The healing is also not increasable by % healing Equipment, but can also not be reduced by debuffs.
  • Talisman of Warrior: 10 P.ATK buff. Quite a significant amount, put it on every Physical Class in your party.
  • Talisman of Magician: 10 S.MATK buff. Quite a significant amount, put it on every Magical Class in your party, including yourself.
  • Talisman of Five Elements: Increases damage dealt against Neutral, Earth, Wind, Water and Fire enemies by 20% for both physical and magical damage. Whenever you face enemies with mentioned elements, use it on every DPS in your party. Obviously really good for yourself aswell.
  • Soul of Heaven and Earth: Our Supportive Ultimate. At level 10, it increases Melee, Ranged and All Property Magical Damage by 25%. While standing in Totem of Tutelary, it also regenerates 30AP for your entire party (including yourself). For this effect, only you need to stand in the Totem, everyone else just needs to be in range of the Ultimate (23x23 around you).
Talisman DPS skills

(The ones in the middle)

Other than Talisman of Soul Stealing and Circle of Directions and Elementals (our DPS ultimate), these skills do not have any After Cast Delay and only have individual Cooldowns. They're also boosted in damage by the buff of our DPS Ultimate. All these skills are also affected by Warm Wind.

  • Talisman of Soul Stealing: A single Target attack skill that deals some damage and regenerates some of your SP. It's damage and SP regeneration are both quite weak, along with it having a significant cooldown and some ACD, so it's not a skill worth investing into nor using. If you want to regenerate SP, use the Soul Reaper skill Kaute.
  • Talisman of Blue Dragon: A single Target attack skill. 0,3 seconds cooldown. Up to 7500% MATK per cast.
  • Talisman of White Tiger: A 15x15 AOE centered around you. 0,3 seconds cooldown. Up to 5750% MATK per cast.
  • Talisman of Red Phoenix: A targeted 7x7 AOE skill. 0,45 seconds cooldown. Up to 7250% MATK per cast.
  • Talisman of Black Tortoise: A non targeted 11x11 AOE skill. 0,7 seconds cooldown. Up to 9500% MATK per Cast.
  • Talisman of Four Bearing Gods: A targeted 11x11 AOE skill. 1 second cooldown. Up to 12250% MATK per cast.
  • Circle of Directions and Elementals: A targeted 11x11 AOE skill. Our DPS Ultimate, 150AP cost, significant Cooldown. Approximately 42375% MATK, depending on skillbuild. In order to use it, you need to be under the "Blessing of the North" Buff. In order to get this buff, you need to use the Talisman Skills Blue Dragon, White Tiger, Red Phoenix and Black Tortoise once each in this order.

Once using this Ultimate, we get the "Blessing of Four Directions and Five Elements" Buff, which lasts for 300 seconds, gives us 25 S.Matk and significantly strengthens all Talisman attacks other than Talisman of Soul Stealing.

Soul Skills

(The ones on the left)

There are only 3 of them, and they're based around the Soul System Soul Reaper has introduced.

  • Soul Mastery: Increases your SPL by one each rank. Also increases the damage of Exorcism of Malicious Soul, Circle of Directions and Elementals and Talisman of Soul Stealing.
  • Soul Gathering: Immediatly builds up your maximum amount of Souls. Increasing the skill Level lowers its cooldown from 5 to 1 second. This skill is the reason why Soul Collect and Soul Reaper are now irrelevant with us now having essentially infinite Souls. However, in order to cast this, you need to have Soul Collect enabled anyway. Has 0,5 seconds ACD
  • Exorcism of Malicious Soul: A 13x13 AOE skill centered on you. Deals Damage based on the amount of Souls you have. Given you have 20 (the maximum amount), it will deal up to 25800% MATK. Has 0,5 seconds ACD and 1 second cooldown. Thanks to Soul Gathering, you can spam this skill every second with maximum Souls, making this an incredibly potent damage skill. This skill is also affected by Warm Wind.

Builds[OUTDATED]

Currently, Exorcism of Malicious Soul appears to be both a bit stronger and easier to build than Talisman DPS builds, being both strong for solo farming and party play. Also less punishing to die on. Does not mean that Talisman DPS builds are not good already though, but it will likely take a bit more commitment. It will also see significant buffs in the future. It does win out in MVP dps already , and the various ways Talisman Skills can be used give them their own little advantages.

For the more Supportive side of this Class, Full Support is essentially a choice for people with little gear that are unable to do proper DPS, or find themselves in parties with plenty of DMG already and choose to focus on survival instead. Less of a Zeny commitment than DPS, it still takes a bit of money to properly support. The Exorcism Semi DPS Support meanwhile is able to hand out all the buffs along with having a strong cleaning DPS skill, though is also a bit more difficult to play while also using expensive DPS gear all the same.

All these builds are solid and strong in their own ways.

Now, that's a lot of technical stuff, stats and all that. Let's go through each of them real quick and sort out their strengths and weaknesses, in a more gameplay aspect.

  • Talisman of Soul Stealing: Does not get buffed by our Ultimate, has a high CD, some ACD, deals little damage and regenerates little SP. In other words, please do not ever bother with this skill.
  • Talisman of Blue Dragon: The first of the 5 actual attack spells we have for this build, single target and having the same range as Espa, this is the closest we have to our previous SR gameplay. Among all of our skills, it is one of two that have the lowest cooldown of all, too, at 0,3 seconds. Not much else to say about this than that this is a stronger Espa on a bit of cooldown, making it more useable for people with higher ping, and giving you the opportunity to spam another skill inbetween -
  • Talisman of White Tiger: The second skill of the tree and the skill we will be focusing on the most. This is the only skill not needing to select a target and casting instantly upon use. Along with being as spammable as Blue Dragon, you can use this skill on Turbo in the NovaEXT settings and use any of the other spells along with it, both of which will be then used on cooldown (given enough ASPD of course). In order for that to work, the other skill needs to be on Smart Cast, I will explain that stuff in an additional section in a bit. Alternatives [OUTDATED]

First of all, the Celine set itself (  The Gloves,  Brooch,  Ribbon and  Dress) are of course mandatory, if you don't want to go for this, skip ahead to the other Equipment section of this build.

All the Shadow Equipments also do not have any alternatives, you can however focus on the  Gauntlet and  Shield first, especially the Shield, and ignore the rest of it for the time being. The shield is important as it gives you a lot of Normal penetration for essentially free, even on +0. It's a massive damage boost for little Zeny, as these 2 items are based on older Monster Hunter enemies.

Then you can go for the Shawl and Brooch to get another 40% pen against everything flatout. The shoes are only there for the 7 stats bonus combo.


Now, for things that actually do have alternatives:

For the Middle Headgear, you can simply use a  Magical Booster [1] with 3% Matk and any other stat. The difference of 3% Matk is quite small, but the difference in price between this and the  Sunglasses is humongous.

Same thing for the Lower Headgear , where your alternatives are  Rainbow Scarf and  Rosary in Mouth. Technically there is also  Magical Rosary in Mouth, but that one is really expensive aswell. Overall you can ignore the lower headgear till you have everything else, as all of the options are not really worth the money until then.

As for the Weapon Slot, there is obviously the downgrade of  Patent Spirit Pendulum, but also a popular option for Mages in general - the  Illusion Thorn Staff of Darkness. Being cheap to aquire, upgrade and enchant, it definitely is a very good option to start out with.

For Shields, there are a few alternatives aswell : First of, the Illusion items  Illusion Guard and  Illusion Silver Guard make for strong and cheap alternatives, enchant them with 2 Level 1-2  Spellbound Nives and they will do you well. The Guard gives you attackspeed, while the Silver Guard gives you after cast delay reduction. Both stats aren't exactly needed, but convenient to have, so go with whichever you have / prefer. As an additional option, there is the  Mad Bunny, the cheapest but also weakest of all shields.

Robes only really have 3 alternatives, the  Illusion Engine Wing B-Type and  Automatic Engine Wing B-Type. The former is fine for beginners to go for, with  Caster x2 and  Above All x1 enchants, but I wouldn't quite deem this necessary either, unless the Int Manteau is absolutely unreachable for some reason. The Automatic Engine should only be used if you already have it from another character, I do not see a reason of investing money into this unless you also want to go a Talisman DPS build, in which case it's a viable alternative with Attackspeed enchants. An alternative for newbies however would be  Grace Magic Manteau from the tutorial. Can't get a cheaper alternative than that.

As for Shoes, your alternatives are  Illusion Leg B-Type and  Automatic Leg B-Type. As we have 1,5 seconds fixed cast time, these 2 are the best options. The enchants are cookie cutter with  Fixed Casting  Spell Buster and  Lucky Strike or  Vital instead of Lucky Strike for Illusion Leg.
