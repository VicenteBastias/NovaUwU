The Star Emperor (SE) is the class upgrade to the Star Gladiator (also known as TaeKwon Master).

Star Emperors fight primarily with melee skills in the form of stances, which unlocks associated skills corresponding to each line. They have rapid area type attacks, can cloak themselves and can power up their skills with a temporary Light buff. They are also capable of resetting the chosen maps and targets of the Perception and Opposition skills, granting them almost free access to the Star Gladiator's skills.

In PVP, they can deal immense single target damage or temporarily remove an enemy from the fight with Gravity Control.

Player Guides

Builds

Solar Burst

Solar Burst is the strongest PVM area damage skill that the Star Emperor has. This build is intended for levelling and farming quickly by increasing the strength of Solar Burst as much as possible so that the Star Emperor can easily destroy mobs of enemies. Combined with a Minstrel's A Poem of Bragi paired with After Cast Delay reduction gear, Solar Burst can be used extremely rapidly against more tanky enemies such as bosses for strong DPS.

Note that the SP usage of Solar Burst can be extremely heavy. For general farming, leeching gears are almost a necessity, and when using Solar Burst for sustained DPS, a large supply of SP restoring items or a Sorcerer in the party becomes very important. In addition, Solar Burst spamming builds do not synergize well with the Star Gladiator's Wrath skills, which knock back enemies, nor with the Tae Kwon's kicking skills, as you do not trigger kick stances when using skill attacks.

As a melee type build, STR is the primary stat for damage. While the cast time for sun-line skills are all 0, they are also affected by FLEE so DEX becomes important to hit enemies. VIT and AGI are both useful to help survivability. Due to Solar Burst's after skill delay, taking AGI for ASPD is not as important unless you expect to always have a Minstrel on hand. Finally, LUK is a good all-rounder stat but not very important as Solar Burst does not perform critical hits.

Gearing should focus on physical attack damage bonuses. Leeching gear is also a good idea.

ASPD Meteors

Falling Star has the highest potential single-target DPS of the Star Emperor's PVM skills. This build focuses on rapid fire autoattacks to trigger as many star drops as quickly as possible. You can combine this with the Star Gladiator's Warmth skills (eg, Warmth of the Sun) against enemies that cannot be knocked back, or when you have enemies pushed up against the wall. In addition, the high ASPD of this build lends itself naturally to the Tae Kwon's kicking skills, so if a player's reactions are good, they can incorporate those into their Star build to further power their attacks.

The drawbacks is the generally single target nature of the build. While falling stars do area damage, the Emperor is still going to be primarily focused on one target at a time. Also, the Star Emperor has to be very efficient with their Star Token management through effective use of Flash Kick, which can prove difficult if enemies are not all clustered together. Furthermore, the damage potential of the build drops considerably for boss type monsters that do not have constantly respawning slaves, such as certain instance MVPs. Finally, the melee nature of the build can make it difficult to attack certain bosses safely due to their aggro mechanics of attacking anyone in melee range as opposed to staying only on the party's tank.

Lunar Leveling

Using the Stance of the Moon the player is able to do massive damage to mobs in an area while staying invisible after the first Area of Effect attack. Set up by casting New Moon Kick then following up with Full Moon Kick is a powerful 1-2 combo easily used by new players to the class to hit enemies, turn invisible then knock out while leveling on masses of aggressive monsters. The usual skills and buffs apply to this such as Warm Wind and the party skill Khop. This build does not need any attack speed what so ever so if you were having a tough time with other builds for such a reason try this out and focus on power instead. The draw back to this build is that monsters will lose target on you after you go invisible but fortunately you can move while in this state.

PVP Cleaner

Nova Explosion is a terrifying skill, capable of extremely high damage that cannot be reduced by any effect, including DEF, racial resistance or other percentage based reduction effects. This makes it exceptionally useful against ordinarily tanky enemies who many other damage-type builds would have trouble defeating, such as Shadow Chasers or Steel Body Suras (with  Golden Thief Bug Card). While the cool down and skill reuse penalty of Nova Explosion are heavy, Star Emperors can regularly mitigate those drawbacks using Book of Dimensions, allowing them to spam Nova Explosion for a short duration and almost guaranteeing a few kills. Star Emperors are also equipped with Gravity Control, a targeted disable spell that can be used to set up Nova Explosion on your target or temporarily remove an enemy's support. With Flying Side Kick, Star Emperors can very effectively target certain enemies to defeat at extremely high speed.

Note that the Star Emperor, being a primarily melee class, will often have trouble surviving if it is not careful with its distance from the enemy stack. New Moon Kick can be used to help escape some bad situations, but ultimately very strong positioning, efficient gear swapping from resistances to offence, and careful enemy targeting is necessary to make best use of the class.

Gameplay

Tae Kwon Kicks

Tae Kwons have 4 pairs of stances and kick skills related to autoattacking. The skills are:

  • Tornado Stance and Tornado Kick
  • Heel Drop Stance and Heel Drop
  • Roundhouse Stance and Roundhouse Kick
  • Counter Stance and Counter Kick

The kicks cannot be used on their own. When a stance is toggled active, auto attacks will gain a chance to proc the stance. During a brief window (roughly 0.5 sec), the user must use the corresponding kick skill.

Note that for that stance window, your character will stop autoattacking. If you do not react with the correct kick quickly enough, your overall DPS may drop as your character continuously pauses for the stance trigger. If you press the wrong kick skill during the stance window, your character will stand still for the rest of the stance trigger's duration, even if you press the correct kick afterwards, so it's important to get the kick correct on the first try.

Sun, Moon, Stars

Star Gladiators have skills focused on the three celestial bodies. The important marking skills are:

  • Feeling of the Sun, Moon and Stars - designate a map as under the sun, the moon or the stars. You can only designate one map for each. Instance, PVP and Siege maps can be marked.
  • Hatred of the Sun, Moon and Stars - designate a monster as an enemy of the sun, the moon or the stars. You can only designate one enemy for each. Player classes can be marked with no size or HP restrictions.

These settings have three possible reset options:

  • Triggering the Angel of the Sun, Moon and Stars
  • Main Office second floor has an NPC who you can use to reset the Feelings and Hatred targets.
  • Star Emperor's Document of the Sun, Moon and Stars can reset Feelings, Hatred or both depending on the skill level used.

Otherwise, Feelings and Hatred persist after death and log outs, and using the Feeling and Hatred skills will fail if there already exists a target designated by the mark attempted.

Being on a marked map allows usage or triggers the benefit of these skills:

  • The Warmth skill corresponding to the map's marking: Warmth of the Sun, Warmth of the Moon, Warmth of the Stars
  • The Comfort skill corresponding to the map's marking, if the day is correct: Comfort of the Sun (on even numbered days), Comfort of the Moon (on odd numbered days), Comfort of the Stars (on days divisible by 5)
  • Knowledge of the Sun, Moon and Stars will provide its passive effect on any marked map

Fighting against a marked enemy triggers the benefit of these skills:

  • The Anger skill corresponding to the enemy's mark: Anger of the Sun, Anger of the Moon, Anger of the Stars
  • The Blessing skill corresponding to the enemy's mark, if the day is correct: Blessing of the Sun (on even numbered days), Blessing of the Moon (on odd numbered days), Blessing of the Stars (on days divisible by 5)

Miracle of the Sun, Moon and Stars

Whenever a Star Gladiator or Star Emperor attacks, there is a very low probability of triggering a Miracle of the Sun, Moon and Stars. When a Miracle occurs, the screen will dim as if it were night time, or if you were under a Soul Link. All Warmth and Comfort skills become usable, regardless of whether the map was designated correctly or if the day is correct. In addition, all monsters will be recognized as an enemy of the Stars.

The Miracle will last for one hour, or until the map is changed.

Angel of the Sun, Moon and Stars

When a Star Gladiator or Star Emperor is benefiting from increased SP regeneration from Happy Break and uses the /doridori command, there is a very low probability that an Angel of the Sun, Moon and Stars will descend upon them. When it appears, the Angel will reset all map and monster designations from using the Perception and Opposition skills.

Star Emperor Stances

Star Emperors have multiple stance toggle skills that control what skills they can currently use, along with providing some passive bonuses. The skills are:

  • Stance of the Sun - Access to Prominence Kick, Solar Burst, Light of the Sun
  • Stance of the Moon - Access to New Moon Kick, Full Moon Kick, Light of the Moon
  • Stance of the Stars - Access to Flash Kick, Falling Star, Light of the Stars
  • Stance of the Universe - Access to all skills.

Unlike the Tae Kwon's stances, these cannot be overlapped. If a stance is used while another is already active, the current one is dispelled and replaced with the new one. In addition, all stance restricted buffs are immediately cancelled if any are still active (ie, Light skills, New Moon, Falling Star, Book of Dimensions). While Stance of the Universe can use any of the skills, switching to or from the Stance of the Universe will still dispel every stance-restricted status buff.

Also note that while Stance of the Universe gives access to every Light skill, they cannot overlap with each other. If a Light skill is attempted while another type of Light is already active, the existing one is dispelled and replaced with the new one.
