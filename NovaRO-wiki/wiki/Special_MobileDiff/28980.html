===Primary Assessment===
It is the question "What is killing my patient first?" during any situation. Our goal as Supports is to conserve the fighting strength and a Primary Assessment is the way we go about that as a guideline. Although it is easy to focus in an ABC order (Airway, Breathing, Circulation / Clementia, Canto Candidus, Cluseo Heal) most of the time these will actually hinder your patient(s). In a way you will perform your primary assessment in the way your patient needs it most. Consider these situations:
You are called to a responsive patient who dropped a concrete block on his foot and is in considerable pain. (Burst damage)
You are eating in a restaurant and observe a man with what appears to be a complete air obstruction. (Damage Over Time Critical)
You are called for a "man down" and see a person on the ground who does not appear to be moving or breathing. (Dungeon and Dragons inspired term "Actively Dying" and the damage did not exceed their max HP).
You are called for an industrial accident and find a man who looks pale, sweaty, and about to pass out. He has blood spurting from his thigh. (Active hemorrhaging/bleeding to death/D.O.T.)
Remember you will not be alone in a party setting and will often work in a team environment in which multiple tasks can be accomplished immediately and simultaneously. It is also possible that a the patient himself may be able to help control bleeding while you do other important tasks.
Always ensure you are going into battle with the correct personal protective equipment~ Which will be a baseline of Eye Pro and Gloves as a minimal (Headgear/Weapon)
=Scene Size Up=
S: Safety - always ensure you your personal safety is at it's highest chance before you arrive to a scene.
N: Nature of Illness and Mechanism of Injury - Finding the Nature of Illness is usually indicative to finding the Mechanism of Injury. You want to identify what is killing my patient first.
N: Number of Patients
A: Additional support from other supports or advance support from more medical inclined supports? (Advance Life Support)
C: Consider Cervical Spine injuries. The spine forms a vital link between your brain and the rest of the body. Usually in the form of movement, we want to preserve the spine the best we can for our patient or risk this vital communication hub for their sync ratio.
===Triage===
In order to prioritize your patients during a mass casualty incident or to prevent one look for "ID ME"
Immediate (This group needs priority to prevent death or major disability within two minutes to two hours. Focusing on patients with a good chance of survival WITH IMMEDIATE INTERVENTION. These interventions can be completed on the Airway Obstruction or there is a potential to compromise the patent airway. Tension pneumothorax, uncontrolled hemorrhaging, torso, neck and head injuries WITH SHOCK.
Delayed (This group needs surgery and there is no immediate risk to life, limb or eyesight. For example, no airway compromise, fractures which lead to no life, limb or eyesight compromises, burns which place them in the "What is killing my patient first" category.
Minimal (Manpower resource for the injured, have a priority to secure first as they provide such manpower, minor lacerations, abrasives, fractures to small bones)
Expectant (Overwhelms medical resources at the cost of par patient care. These include patients who arrive without vital signs, transcarnial gunshot wound with coma, uncontrolled pelvis bleeding, high spinal cord injuries, burns without reasonable chance for survival AND RECOVERY.
Of course triaging is a fluid condition that requires flexibility at any moment to any category.
