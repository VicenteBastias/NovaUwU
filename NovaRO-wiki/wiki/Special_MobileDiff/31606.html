Loyalty builds motivation.
Jane McGonigal's book SuperBetter shows that when humans are tasked to represent something bigger than themselves or to someone, such as striving to be the person your child sees you as, what appears to be an infinite amount of motivation.
When asked to undertake these roles in a simulation, there was an undeniable amount of evidence showing higher amounts of motivation and self-efficacy amongst the participants who devoted themselves to a group that doesn't have a clear face to it but rather, a symbol.


Exercising duty can stave off despair.
During the Valley Forge, General George Washington had the troops watch Cato, a Tragedy, about Stoicism. In this play, a fundamental philosophy boils to God grant me the serenity to accept the things I cannot change, courage to change the things I can, and wisdom to know the difference. Instead of falling victim to despair during the valley forge, General George Washington encouraged his men to focus not on the weather they had no control over nor their enemies' actions at that very moment. But instead, they focus on what they could change, such as resisting taking shortcuts and concentrating on their responsibilities to change the tides of war.

Integrity: Makes the world a better, more interesting place
Measuring beliefs against actions in a stressful environment was how researcher Trudi West measured integrity amongst the Clipper Round teams, which races identically made yachts; thereby, the only factor is the crew. The failing teams were unable to keep the moral principles they set before the waves. For example, one wishes to have a "Happy Boat" regardless of winning, but the leadership wanted to win. So we have this dichotomy between doing and saying. Trudi West, through monitoring the bio-metrics of individuals, was able to find a correlation of these in-congruent decisions and anxiety amongst the group. To solve this, Trudi West proposes we observe ourselves in stressful environments to understand what we truly believe is right. For "People are like tea bags, they don't know their strength until they get into hot water." Thereby refining your integrity over time allows you to do and say nothing that deceives others, affecting your relationships and fundamentally acceptance of yourself. For every time someone steps up and says who they are, the world becomes a better, more interesting place.
Personal Courage: A quote from Albus Dumbledore, "Dark times lie ahead of us, and there will be a time when we must choose between what is easy and what is right."
This doesn't mean you're not scared of the darkness, but taking the step into it is what I believe shows personal courage in all of us here.
We see Rosa Parks and Gandhi, who do not exude the most outward of energy in history. Through their personal courage, they still chose to fan the sparks of history into flames.
