== (Solo) Exorcism of Malicious Soul[OUTDATED] ==
This Build is focused on Solo Farming. There are a few ways to play this, depending on what gear you have, how much damage you need, which corners you can cut, yada yada yada. Let's start with the skills first.
=== Skill Builds [OUTDATED]===
I personally use 3 different ones. One focused on damage with the Ultimate buff, one that sacrifices a little damage for more healing from '''Talisman of Protection''' and one that completely cuts out the Ultimate for more survival / consistency. I will explain things based on 2 different ones though, and you take the rest from there yourself, based on your preferences, gears, needs, etc.
[[File:Exorcism Solo Build 1.png|frame|left]]
The idea behind this skillbuild is essentially that I do not rely on either Ultimate, get the maximum amount of Healing from '''Talisman of Protection''' and have the maximum amount of DMG for '''Exorcism of Malicious Soul''' that you can get without ultimate. A build that cuts some damage corners for when you have more than plenty of it to begin with. I don't really have to worry about dying with this even in Ancient Juperos 3.
[[File:Exorcism Solo Build 2.png|frame|left]]
Meanwhile this build focuses on taking advantage of the Buff '''Circle of Directions and Elementals''' gives us (25 S.Matk). This is the highest amount of damage reasonably possible, given you're using the ultimate buff. The third build I use that I talked about earlier is simply exchanging the additional points I've had left from '''Soul Mastery''' to '''Talisman Mastery'''. This reduces your damage by a bit, but increases the healing of '''Talisman of Protection'''. You technically could even remove the points from '''Totem of Tutelary''' to put it into either of the Mastery skills, though that's not really an option I am a fan of personally, as the Totem can help you heal up a bit if you ate a few nukes from Monsters and also is useful for stronger MVP battles, though you could simply use the Soul Reaper Skill '''Curse of Wicked Souls''' instead for this situation. Means this is more of a preference thing than anything.
Of course, a question that will come up now is "Why '''Cirlce of Directions and Elementals''' instead of '''Soul of Heaven and Earth'''?", which is a fair question. The answer is simply that the 25 S.Matk give me more damage than the 25% property damage. This may be in part because I use Celine set, which gives a pretty big amount of Elemental damage already. It may be different with the Automatic Armor + Thanatos Accessory combo, but more on all that later.
=== Stats and Traits[OUTDATED] ===
Not going to take a screenshot here because this fairly individualistic, based on your enchants, gears, needs, preferences etc.
Soo, just a bit of talk about the idea behind what's going on here and you take it from there.
This being a farm build, if you can force some points into '''STR''' it will always be a useful choice.
'''AGI''' is not necessarly needed due to '''Exorcism of Malicious Souls''' not being a spamable skill, but can be useful to put some points into for ASPD to High Jump at a faster pace. How much you feel is right here is up to the individual, personally I aim at around 170 ASPD, sometimes less, sometimes more. 
'''VIT''' is a stat I try to greed away from as much as possible, to a degree that I used to farm Ancient Juperos 2 with 1 VIT only. Currently I do use 100 of it however in Jupe 3, for convenience sake. Helps that I don't need as much ASPD anymore as I did back in Espa days.
'''Int''' I pretty much always throw to 130. Faster cast, more SP, more damage. Especially the SP part. The bigger our SP pool, the more effective Kaute is.
'''Dex''' I try to go as much as I need for stat instant cast. If I can't reach stat instant cast with some food, I usually go with 120 or 125, or whatever amount needed around that area to get that additional MATK. Currently I use 130 of it actually because I get stat instant cast without food. More on that in my example build later.
'''Luk''' is either a stat that just adds a bit more DMG or a stat you absolutely need depending on wether the area you farm in has mobs that curse you or not. Either a lot or barely any.
For '''Traits''', I just max '''SPL''' to either 99 or 100, depending on my build, based around how much I need for the last S.Matk. Then, if I can greed away from damage, I go for '''STA''' and '''WIS''' for more resistances, otherwise just the usual '''CON''' for more damage, nothing more special than that.
=== Equipment[OUTDATED] ===
Oh boy, here we go.
Currently, there are 2 BiS builds, one using Automatic Armor, Thanatos Accessories and Bacsojin Hat-LT, which is best against Demons, Angels and Dragons, and one based around the Celine set, which is better against everything else. Both have high damage against everything however, so it depends on personal preference which you want to go for. The Celine set is overall going to be more expensive to start out with, so Newbies may be adviced to go for the other build path, as that one has more and cheaper alternatives to start with.
Do however note, if you do also use the Talisman Build and like going for '''Blue Dragon''' and '''White Tiger''', we will be getting a new Accessory likely soon-ish that will combo with {{Item List2|id=550068|item=Spirit Pendulum-LT [2]}} to give both these skills a damage boost. You may want to go for the Automatic Thanatos option in that case to have an easier time replacing your Accessories.

===== Alternatives [OUTDATED]=====
First of all, the Celine set itself ( {{Item List2|id=2980|item=The Gloves}}, {{Item List2|id=34697|item=Brooch}}, {{Item List2|id=18849|item=Ribbon}} and {{Item List2|id=450179|item=Dress}}) are of course mandatory, if you don't want to go for this, skip ahead to the other Equipment section of this build.
All the Shadow Equipments also do not have any alternatives, you can however focus on the {{Item List2|id=24284|item=Gauntlet}} and {{Item List2|id=24315|item=Shield}} first, especially the Shield, and ignore the rest of it for the time being. The shield is important as it gives you a lot of Normal penetration for essentially free, even on +0. It's a massive damage boost for little Zeny, as these 2 items are based on older Monster Hunter enemies.
Then you can go for the Shawl and Brooch to get another 40% pen against everything flatout. The shoes are only there for the 7 stats bonus combo.
Now, for things that actually ''do'' have alternatives:
For the '''Middle Headgear''', you can simply use a {{Item List2|id=32879|item=Magical Booster [1]}} with 3% Matk and any other stat. The difference of 3% Matk is quite small, but the difference in price between this and the {{Item List2|id=410080|item=Sunglasses}} is humongous.
Same thing for the '''Lower Headgear''' , where your alternatives are {{Item List2|id=5463|item=Rainbow Scarf}} and {{Item List2|id=32200|item=Rosary in Mouth}}. Technically there is also {{Item List2|id=32201|item=Magical Rosary in Mouth}}, but that one is really expensive aswell. Overall you can ignore the lower headgear till you have everything else, as all of the options are not really worth the money until then.
As for the '''Weapon''' Slot, there is obviously the downgrade of {{Item List2|id=550015|item=Patent Spirit Pendulum}}, but also a popular option for Mages in general - the {{Item List2|id=550030|item=Illusion Thorn Staff of Darkness}}.
Being cheap to aquire, upgrade and enchant, it definitely is a very good option to start out with.
For '''Shields''', there are a few alternatives aswell : First of, the Illusion items {{Item List2|id=460017|item=Illusion Guard}} and {{Item List2|id=460018|item=Illusion Silver Guard}} make for strong and cheap alternatives, enchant them with 2 Level 1-2 {{Item List2|id=29101|item=Spellbound Nives}} and they will do you well. The Guard gives you attackspeed, while the Silver Guard gives you after cast delay reduction. Both stats aren't exactly needed, but convenient to have, so go with whichever you have / prefer. As an additional option, there is the {{Item List2|id=32369|item=Mad Bunny}}, the cheapest but also weakest of all shields.
'''Robes''' only really have 3 alternatives, the {{Item List2|id=20934|item=Illusion Engine Wing B-Type}} and {{Item List2|id=480021|item=Automatic Engine Wing B-Type}}. The former is fine for beginners to go for, with {{Item List2|id=39538|item=Caster}} x2 and {{Item List2|id=39542|item=Above All}} x1 enchants, but I wouldn't quite deem this necessary either, unless the Int Manteau is absolutely unreachable for some reason. The Automatic Engine should only be used if you already have it from another character, I do not see a reason of investing money into this unless you also want to go a Talisman DPS build, in which case it's a viable alternative with Attackspeed enchants. An alternative for newbies however would be {{Item List2|id=480019|item=Grace Magic Manteau}} from the tutorial. Can't get a cheaper alternative than that.
As for '''Shoes''', your alternatives are {{Item List2|id=22197|item=Illusion Leg B-Type}} and {{Item List2|id=470023|item=Automatic Leg B-Type}}. As we have 1,5 seconds fixed cast time, these 2 are the best options. The enchants are cookie cutter with {{Item List2|id=310111|item=Fixed Casting}} {{Item List2|id=310122|item=Spell Buster}} and {{Item List2|id=310126|item=Lucky Strike}} ''or'' {{Item List2|id=39531|item=Vital}} instead of Lucky Strike for Illusion Leg.
