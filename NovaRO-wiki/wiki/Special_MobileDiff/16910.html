===Hit Points===
'''Hit Points''' (HP) is a number that determines how much damage a character can take before they can't fight anymore. Characters start with a low maxHP, and gains more as they level up.
When a character's HP hits 0, they fall unconscious and ''Bleeding Out'', and must do ''Death Saving Throws''. On 3 failed throws, the character dies; on 3 successful throws, the character becomes stable (stops bleeding) but remains unconscious at 0 HP, until another character heals them using spells or potions.
For enemies, usually when they hit 0 HP they just die.
HP can be partially recovered when a character takes a Short Rest (at least 1 hour outside of battle) using Hit Dice (explained later).
HP is fully recovered when a character takes a Long Rest (at least 8 hrs outside of battle, usually role-played as sleeping at night)

===Spells===
===Spells===
Some classes of characters have the ability to cast spells. Each spell has a level: level 1 up to level 9. Higher level spells are usually more powerful than low level spells.
To cast a spell, a character must spend a '''Spell Slot''' of the correct level (kinda the MP system in D&D). Different classes have different amounts of spell slots depending on their level. All spell slots are recovered when the character takes a Long Rest (sleeping at night).
*A Lv1 Wizard character has 2 Lv1 Spell Slots.
**This means he can cast a level 1 spell 2 times in battle before needing Long Rest.
*A Lv5 Wizard character has 4 Lv1 Spell Slots, 3 Lv2 Spell Slots, and 2 Lv3 Spell Slots.
**This means he can cast a level 1 spell 4 times, a level 2 spell 3 times, and a level 3 spell 2 times before needing Long Rest.
Cantrips (basically level 0 spells) can be cast as many times as you want without needing to spend Spell Slots.
'''Higher Casting''' is when you spend a higher Spell Slot to cast a lower level spell. This usually improves the effect of the spell. For example, casting Cure Wounds (level 1, healing 1d8 HP) using level 2 Spell Slot will make it heal 2d8 HP. Each spell has its own rules about Higher Casting.
===Ability Checks & Saving Throws===
In or out of combat situations, characters will sometimes need to do Ability Checks and/or Saving Throws. This is done by rolling a d20 and adding modifiers determined by your character's stats. The result of the roll is compared with a Difficulty Class (DC) value, explained later. If the roll is higher than the DC, it is successful.
Ability Checks and Saving Throws will be based on one of these 6 abilities. Your character's Ability Score may add or subtract from the value of your dice roll.
*Strength: Physical power or brute force, for example moving a big rock, or saving against being grappled/restrained.
*Dexterity: Agility and reflexes, for example dodging a trap, or saving against tumbling(kesrimpet).
*Constitution: Physical endurance, for example running for 1 hour non-stop, or saving against being poisoned.
*Intelligence: Book smarts and memory, for example remembering what you read 3 days ago, or (rarely) saving against mental attacks.
*Wisdom: Street smarts or attention-span, for example judging if a forest is dangerous at a glance, or saving against psychic/magical manipulation.
*Charisma: Force of personality, for example getting a crowd's attention, or (rarely) saving against certain magical effects


===Other Ability Checks===
Beyond Ability Checks, there are also Skill Checks which are an extension of abilities. Aside from your Ability Scores giving a bonus, you also gain bonuses if your character is ''Proficient'' with the skill, explained later. Skills include:
 
*Strength based:
**Athletics: climbing, jumping, swimming
*Dexterity based:
**Acrobatics: balancing on a rope, wall-jumping, running across a slippery surface
**Sleight of Hand: slipping poison into someone's drink, stealing someone's wallet unnoticed
**Stealth: Hiding or moving unnoticed
*Intelligence based:
**Arcana: About spells or magical items
**History: About historical persons or events
**Nature: About plants, animals, terrains
**Religion: About religions and religious rituals
**Investigation: Looking around for clues, searching for hidden or misplaced items, looking for a weak point of a structure to hit.
*Wisdom based:
**Perception: Awareness, noticing things or stealthy enemies, lip-reading from a distance
**Insight: judging if someone's lying or honest, judging if someone's good or evil.
**Animal Handling: calming a horse, controlling a horse-pulled carriage, taming animals
**Medicine: diagnosing illness, stabilising a dying person
**Survival: wilderness instinct, tracking animals or people
*Charisma based:
**Deception: Lying, bluffing
**Intimidation: influencing someone in a hostile way or by threatening
**Persuasion: influencing someone in a friendly or diplomatic way
**Performance: entertaining people or creatures by music, dance, storytelling, etc.
 
'''Example Scenario:'''
 
Character A walks through a forest and is asked by the DM to do a Perception Check. She rolls a 14 on the d20, and due to her high Wisdom score, gets a +3 bonus, making it a 17, which beats the DC of 15 that was set by the DM.
 
The DM narrates her noticing a boulder that's blocking a cave. Character A chooses to approach it, then she notices a symbol on the boulder. The DM asks her to do a Religion Check, but fails by rolling a 3 against DC of 10, which means she can't seem to recognise the symbol.
 
Character A then decides to move the boulder, so the DM asks her to do a Strength Check. With a 18 roll and a +4 Strength bonus against DC of 20, she successfully moves it and opens the way into the cave.
 
She then decides to move in stealthily, so the DM asks her to do a Stealth Check, which she rolls a 13 with a +1 Dexterity bonus, total of 14.
 
Inside, she sees 2 Undead skeletons. The DM rolls a Perception check for the skeletons, and one of them rolls a 18, which means it notices her coming in despite her stealth because it rolled higher than her Stealth roll.


===Initiative===
===Initiative===
'''Initiative Rolls''' are used to determine who goes first in combat. The roll itself is just a d20 roll plus Dexterity bonus.
For example, as Character A goes into combat with the 2 skeletons, She and her 2 enemies all roll Initiative. Skel A rolls 21, Character A rolls 17, and Skel B rolls 4. This means that in each turn of combat, Skel A gets to move/attack/cast spells first, then Character A, then Skel B.
