== Overview ==
In Pre-Renewal, Ninjas were usually just the underdogs of the game that had low damage throwing skills, a bad version of {{Skill List |id=271 |skill=Asura Strike}}, and could mainly be used as a ping pong ball with its dodging. After a little time has passed in Renewal, Kagerou/Oboro came and changed everything. We Ninjas can now have plenty more versatility in our builds thanks to our new skills and how they affect the old ones as well. These builds include:
* Tank/Killing Strike
* Magic
* Throwing
* Dual Dagger Auto Attacker (Not Implemented into this guide until further testing)
We get more options to choose for builds but our gears are still lacking compared to the 3rd jobs. There is enough to choose from still, along with our very own exclusive Ninja gears. Ninja specific gears can mostly be enchanted quite easily.

[http://irowiki.org/wiki/Ninja_Weapon_Quests Ninja Weapon Quests]

Look forward to the explanation to these builds and how to make your own, especially my favorite, '''Tank Ninja!'''
=== PROS ===
* Able to dodge nearly any physical damage.
* No fixed cast time with a certain buff active.
* Good mobility.
* Can spam spells pretty hard
* Great Ninja gear specific enchants.
* '''You get to be a Ninja!'''
=== CONS ===
* Lack of gear options compared to most classes.
* Low HP pool.
* Lower stat points to allocate due to not being able to transcend.
* Without your buffs, you will not do too much.
* You can be flung around like a ping pong ball.
* '''You are not that silent...'''
== Stats and Status Resists ==
DISCLAIMER: I do not take any credit to the video in this section of the wiki. It is thanks to  ''Munbalance''<nowiki/>'s video that helps make this section.
I HIGHLY recommend looking in this section if you want to learn more about how to resist some status effects and what each stat does. It is not necessarily needed to play Ninja but it does help for any player to learn about these things.
'''STR''' (Carry Weight, ATK, Decreased Vacuum Extreme duration)
WHY: This stat is only used for carry weight or if you end up using a throwing Ninja.
'''AGI''' (ASPD, FLEE, Damage, Masquerade resist, Shortened Animation Delay)
WHY: The shortened animations are a great help to mainly caster Ninjas.
'''VIT''' (HP, Stun resist/immunity, Poison resist/immunity, lowered duration of Crystallize)
WHY: Tank Ninja will use this as its main stat, the rest wont need it as much.
'''INT''' (MATK, Soft MDEF, Cast Speed, Silence resist/immunity, Sleep resist/immunity, Blind resist/immunity, Chaos resist/immunity)
WHY: This will help any build with its resist to silence and is the main damage for magic Ninja.
'''DEX''' (HIT, Damage, Cast Speed, ASPD, MATK, Soft MDEF)
WHY: Used for better {{Skill List |id=3008 |skill=Rapid Throw}}, better cast speed for magic, and helps with damage/HIT for throwing builds.
'''LUK''' (Resistance to nearly everything, Perfect Dodge, Curse resist/immunity, ATK, MATK)
WHY: This is best used for {{Skill List |id=3008 |skill=Rapid Throw}} damage and some extra resist but not much else besides that.

== Kagerou Vs. Oboro ==
The big question that people think about before making their Ninja is to be a Kagerou or an Oboro. These are both great options with only a 3 skill difference but those 3 skills can change a LOT.
====== Kagerou: ======
{{Skill List |id=3023 |skill=Shadow Trampling}} - The main reason for a Kagerou over an Oboro is this skill. This skill allows you to reveal AND snare invisible targets around you, only for PvP though.
{{Skill List |id=3024 |skill=Empty Shadow}} - Stopping damage reflection IS good but most of the time, you will not need to be concerned about it due to your skills being mostly range or magic. Also has a low chance to make the affected target fail his skills.
{{Skill List |id=3025 |skill=Shadow Warrior}} - <s>HAHAHAHAHA, no.</s> Only for messing around with an auto attack build or buffing some random Ranger.
====== Oboro: ======
{{Skill List |id=3026 |skill=Distorted Crescent}} - Here is the reason that most pick Oboro. At even HP, this will immensely increase your physical damage, the same for magic with even SP. It is almost like holding another 1 handed weapon almost.
{{Skill List |id=3027 |skill=Moonlight Fantasy}} - An interesting skill that will make any magic damage done to the affected target, spread to nearby enemies. Pretty good if you want to take care of multiple people sticking too close together or casting it on yourself and sticking to mages to share the damage dealt to you. Can not be used on monsters.
{{Skill List |id=3029 |skill=Ominous Moonlight}} - Can be good if you are sick of dealing with healers. Bystanders of duels that can normally heal outside of the duel '''CAN''' hurt and kill the affected target.
'''Conclusion:'''
Kagerou can reveal hidden enemies (PvP) and stop damage reflection.
Oboro can increase their DPS greatly, spread and share magic damage dealt,  and can also stop enemies from being healed.
Both are valid options, it is up to you which skill set you would like to use more. If you are not sure still after this then just go with Oboro to start with due to the popularity of its skills over Kagerou and a having a little more versatility.

=== Strategy ===
The use of a tank Ninja over other tanks is the fact that you do not need an Arch Bishop, you are self sustaining. {{Skill List |id=3011 |skill=Pure Soul}} is the main attraction to tank Ninja which can heal you through most damage you take and can let you live through an Earthquake. To tank physical, it is best to use {{Skill List |id=532 |skill=Mirror Image}} to corner yourself, with your back to the wall, with the MvP attacking you. Once this is done make sure you keep hitting it for aggro and casting {{Skill List |id=531 |skill=Cicada Skin Shed}} since it will be quicker to cast than your other dodge skill. While this is happening, make sure to watch out for when {{Skill List |id=3011 |skill=Pure Soul}} will run out to recast it ASAP. {{Skill List |id=524 |skill=Throw Kunai}} will help gain aggro since you will have trouble even moving outside of your desired corner. With this build, you are able to tank MvPs without any support which will allow your team to set up a little more easily.
Underneath will show which MvPs Ninja can and can not tank. It will also list for the things to watch out for if you do tank such MvPs and why you can not tank the others.

== Magic ==
----
A more common build for a Ninja to go to due to the ease of gear accessibility.This is probably one of the best builds for single target DPS for Ninja without needing much gear compared to the other classes. This build is based around spamming your spells as fast and hard as possible to a single target. The big weakness to this build is definitely the lack of good AoE spells along with lack of elements. A pretty simple build but it is also a great one to go to.
==== Stats ====
*'''STR''' 1 (Will not do anything but add carry weight for you)
*'''AGI''' 50-80 (For faster animations per cast so you are able to spam magic more quickly)
*'''VIT''' 94 (100 in total for Stun immunity)
*'''INT''' 100-120(For Temp Boots)
*'''DEX''' 100-120(Faster cast time)
*'''LUK''' 50-80 (For Rapid Throw mainly but also gives some MATK)
'''Preferred Clan: '''Arc Wand

=== Strategy ===
Magic builds for Ninja are based around using magic as much, and as fast, as you can. The more INT is usually better due to your already quick cast times while it increases your damage and your cast speed. It is good to prioritize INT on some of your gear to shorten that cast time even more so while adding damage. Magic Ninjas lack elements to use in PvM overall but can still DPS quite well, as long as the enemy does not resist the chosen element. Easy to use and usually suited to PvP but can PvM quite well if they are weak to your chosen magic element. With the lack of gears though, water magic will be lacking compared to fire or wind damage.
== Throwing ==
----
This build is something I like to use often for farming due to the ease of use. The damage output for PvP is not that great so it is not as recommended, but if you really want to then go ahead. Your throwing skills mostly AoE which helps clear out mobs more quickly but the big issue is that 2 out of 3 of your main throwing skills have a small cooldown. == Tips and Tricks ==
A section dedicated to helping you get better at Ninja, even if it is a little bit.
A few things you should know:
- {{Skill List |id=3011 |skill=Pure Soul}} has about a 50% chance to dodge single target magic.
[[File:Pure_Soul_Dodge.gif]]
- Use {{Skill List |id=529 |skill=Shadow Leap}} to move while you are using {{Skill List |id=3011 |skill=Pure Soul}} to move around.
[[File:Pure_Leap.gif]]
- {{Skill List |id=530 |skill=Shadow Slash}} does not have an aftercast delay so only ASPD will affect the delay. Use this to close the gap and use the skills you desire, such as {{Skill List |id=544 |skill=Killing Strike}}.
[[File:Killing_Strike.gif]]
- Charms will greatly increase your magic damage if you have 10 of the same type of charm then cast the same element magic.
- Even if you do not want to change your weapon's element, use {{Skill List |id=3018 |skill=Earth Charm}} 9 times for 9 charms to increase ATK without endowing the element.
- {{Skill List |id=544 |skill=Killing Strike}}'s damage is increased by HP the most. Ranged damage is the 2nd highest option. ATK is the lowest of the three for damage.
- {{Skill List |id=289 |skill=Dispell}} will remove ALL buffs you have at your disposal, including {{Skill List |id=3011 |skill=Pure Soul}}.
- The amount of dodges left on {{Skill List |id=532 |skill=Mirror Image}} will increase your overall damage on {{Skill List |id=544 |skill=Killing Strike}}. It will make it hit multiple times instead of just 1 and, even though it shows differently, the damage is calculated all at once so people can not pot through it.
- {{Skill List |id=3021 |skill=Illusion - Bewitch}} can switch positions even if your target is in {{Skill List |id=25 |skill=Pneuma}}.
== Leveling ==
=== Lv 1 - Lv 99 ===
* '''Lv 1 - Lv 30'''
** Start out killing Poring, Fabre and Lunatics at the portal south of Izlude (@go 5).
** Once {{Skill List |id=534 |skill=Flaming Petals}} is maxed, head to Ant Hell and kill the ant mobs there until 25-30.
* '''Lv 25 - Lv 50'''
** Payon Dungeon floor 2 to kill Archer Skeletons and Soldier Skeletons until 45-50 or so.
* '''Lv 45 - Lv 71'''
** With enough INT, access to fire magic and something to make it harder to interrupt casting ({{Skill List |id=531 |skill=Cicada Skin Shed}}), go Moscovia Dungeon. Here you will want to focus more on Tree Goblins due to them being slower and having lower HP than Les.
* '''Lv 71 - Lv 85'''
** Go to Eden (@go 36) and join the Eden group. After that, go accept the quests to hunt Wraith and Evil Druid at Glast Heim. The best spawn is at Glast Heim St Abbey first floor.
* '''Lv 85 - Lv 114'''
** You are now able to do the first part of the Gramps parties.
** If you do not want to do Gramps, go accept the quests for Turtle Dungeon and complete with those quests appropriately. First area in Turtle Island.
** Once 99, accept another Eden quest for Dark Bishop. Glast Heim 2nd floor has the best spawn.
* '''Lv 91 - Lv 99'''
** Once in this level range, accept all the Turtle Dungeon quests at Eden again except Heater unless you want to go the extra mile. Highest spawn for everything but Heater is 2nd area in Turtle Island.
** You may also accept another Eden quest for Dark Bishop. Glast Heim St Abbey 2nd floor has the best spawn.
=== Lv 100+ ===
* '''Lv 100'''
** Congratulations, you are now an Oboro/Kagerou, now try to rush for {{Skill List |id=3009 |skill=Swirling Petal}} and {{Skill List |id=3022 |skill=16th Night}} after. Make sure to change into a DEX/AGI build and have a Huuma Shuriken once there.
* '''Lv 115 - Lv 144'''
** The second part of the Gramps party quests have become available to do at this point.
* '''Lv 100-110'''
** Still do not want to do Gramps? Go do the Eden hunting quests for Ice Dungeon then.
* '''Lv 125'''
** Minimum level to access Airship Assault, Devil's Tower.
* '''Lv 130'''
** Minimum level to access Old Glast Heim Normal Mode, Charleston Crisis, Buwaya Cave.
* '''Lv 140'''
** Minimum level to access Horror Toy Factory, Central Laboratory, Malangdo Culvert, Bakonawa Lake.
*** You must complete Cautious Village and Bakonawa Extermination to access Bakonawa Lake.
** Minimum level to do Dimensional Travel. This is a pre-requisite quest to access Bios Island.
* '''Lv 145 - Lv 175'''
** Third and final tier to the Gramps party quests.
* '''Lv 145'''
** Just go do Gramps already...
** Minimum level to access Sarah and Fenrir.
* '''Lv 160'''
** Minimum level to access Bios Island, Morse Cave, Temple of the Demon God, Monster Hunter, and Deserted Island.
** Recommended level to try Old Glast Heim Hard Mode if you have not done so at this point.
