== Overview ==
Elements are attributes that monsters and players have. The same thing happens with attacks being it a skill or not.
There are 4 levels and depending on the element used offensively and defensively, damage can be increased or reduced.
Levels only matter in defensive terms since different levels have different results based on the element used offensively.
'''Monsters:'''
:*On monsters this attribute is seen as the defensive property of a monster and it ranges from '''[[Elements#Level_1|level 1]]''' to '''[[Elements#Level_4|level 4]]'''.
:*This doesn't mean that a monster attacks with that same element that it has, auto-attacks from them are always neutrals ('''note:''' they're non-elemental but to be easier to understand, let's consider it neutral for now) and it's the element from the skill used that determines the element.
:*This means that cards like {{Item List2|id=4133|item=Raydric Card}} reduce the damage of the auto-attacks but not skills such as {{Skill Info|class=Mage|id=17|skill=Fire Ball}} since the element of attack is Fire even if the monster has the <code>Element: Neutral 4</code> displayed in the <code>@mi</code> of it.
:*Be aware that some skills are only reduced by certain gears/cards since they ignore everything else due to the way they work.
'''Players:'''
:*Defensively, players only have access to '''[[Elements#Level_1|level 1]]''' as a reduction so in '''Player vs Player''' environments only that table matters. The same thing should be considered when you want to know the final result from certain damage from monsters.
:*If a player doesn't use any piece of gear or card that changes its defensive element, he/she is considered the same as Neutral 1.
:*Be aware that some buffs such as {{Skill Info|class=Royal Guard|id=2322|skill=Piety}} or {{Skill Info|class=Priest|id=69|skill=B.S. Sacrementi}} change the element of the armor of the player temporarily making them be the same element as the buff.
:*Enchants that change the element of the player have a priority over cards and they don't stack so
::*{{Item List2|id=15388|item=King Schmidt's Suit<nowiki>[1]</nowiki>}} with the enchant {{Item List2|id=29305|item=Fire Property (Armor)}} enchant and a {{Item List2|id=4054|item=Angeling Card}} will make the player being considered Fire 1 instead of Holy 1.
::*{{Item List2|id=15388|item=King Schmidt's Suit<nowiki>[1]</nowiki>}} with the enchant {{Item List2|id=29305|item=Fire Property (Armor)}} enchant and a {{Item List2|id=4099|item=Pasana Card}} will make the player being considered Fire 1 instead of Fire 2.
:*Offensively, a player auto-attack is always considered neutral but it can be changed through the use of specific weapons that have an element attached or through skills, converters, or any other item that enchants the weapon with a specific element. The same thing applies to most of the physical skill but the same can't be said of most magical skills.
:*For more info about skills you can check this [[List of Skills|list]] with all the skills available in the game.
