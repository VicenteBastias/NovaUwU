=== '''Royal Guard''' ===
=== '''[[File:Pticon_3royalguard.png]] [[Royal Guard]]''' ===


Royal Guard's defining ability is to protect allies through {{Skill List|id=255|skill=Devotion}}. For game modes which require carrying objectives, the importance of Devotion cannot be understated. They can also protect any heavy damage based class and allow them to survive longer, which gives them more time to destroy the enemy team. {{Skill List|id=257|skill=Defender|simple=yes}} will let you reduce ranged damage so much it'll become negligible, and this also extends to players that you use Devotion on. {{Skill List|id=2312|skill=Pinpoint Attack|simple=yes}} at different levels will allow you to remove a Sura's Spirit Spheres or break other players' equipment. {{Skill List|id=2309|skill=Trample|simple=yes}} and {{Skill List|id=2323|skill=Earth Drive|simple=yes}} will allow you to clear Ground-based traps, but be careful since Earth Drive has a chance to break your own shield. Without a shield, Royal Guards lose a lot of their defensive buffs such as Autoguard, Reflect Shield, and Defender. {{Skill List|id=369|skill=Gospel|simple=yes}} will allow you to clear many negative (but also positive) status effects on yourself as well.

Warlocks can build highly offensive or defensive, and can hybridize between depending on their skill choices and gearing. Offensive Warlocks are generally extremely frail, but can deal heavy magic-based damage. Be wary with Crimson Rock usage as you can break enemies out of Crystallize. Finally, the damage Warlock's ace skill is {{Skill List|id=5012|skill=Intense Telekinesis}}, which will turn {{Skill List|id=2202|skill=Soul Expansion}} into a lethal magic machine gun. It has a long cool down, so use it to secure a victory or to turn a tide.
