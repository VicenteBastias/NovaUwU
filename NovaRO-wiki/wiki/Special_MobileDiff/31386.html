Duty: Exercising duty can stave off despair.
During the Valley Forge General George Washington had the troops watch Cato, a Tragedy, which was about Stoicism. In this play a key philosophy boils to: God grant me the serenity to accept the things I cannot change, courage to change the things I can, and wisdom to know the difference. Instead of falling victim to despair during the valley forge, General George Washington encouraged to have his men focus not on the weather they had no control over, nor their enemies actions at that very moment. But rather, focus on what they could change such as resisting to take shortcuts and focusing on their responsibilities to change the tides of war.
