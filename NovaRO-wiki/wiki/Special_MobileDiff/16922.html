Task difficulty based on DC number:
*5: Very easy, kids can do it
*10: Easy, most people can do it
*15: Slightly challenging, may need some skill to do it
*20: Challenging, need to be skilled or be lucky to do it
*25: Very Hard, need both skill and luck to do it
*30: Nearly Impossible, need to be an expert and be lucky to do it.
 
DC can be determined in 3 ways.
*In general role-playing circumstances, it's set by the DM. For example, moving a big boulder may be a Strength DC of 15, while moving a cart is 10.
*For spells, it's pre-determined by the caster's Spell DC, based on their Class, Ability Score, and Level.
*Determined as a contest between 2 character's rolls.
**For example, character trying to lie (''Deception'') vs innkeeper judging if he's lying (''Insight'')
**Or a thief hiding (''Stealth'') vs a city guard watching out for criminals (''Perception'')
