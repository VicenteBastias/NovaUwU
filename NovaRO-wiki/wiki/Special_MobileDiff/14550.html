If you’re starting fresh, the most newbie-friendly and affordable job classes are usually physical and ranged classes. With equipment that enables HP or SP leeching such as Thanatos Weapons from [[Ghost Palace]], [https://www.novaragnarok.com/wiki/Custom_Headgear_Quests#Headgears Rideword Hat], and [https://www.novaragnarok.com/wiki/Pet_System loyal Incubus pet], physical classes don't have to rely too much on potions. Assuming you’re playing alone and will occasionally join parties with randoms in PvM environment, here are some recommendations.


* '''Mechanic''': They are one of the few classes with exceptionally high item carrying capacity, and they have access to a lot of utility, support, and defensive skills, on top of several offensive skills. All of this makes Mechanic a great choice for solo farming and organized instance raiding. Mado Gear Mechanic is usually build as a tanky supporter and damage dealer, while Axe Mechanic leans more on the offensive with some support skills. See also:
If you’re starting fresh, the most newbie-friendly and affordable job classes are usually physical and ranged classes. With equipment that enables HP or SP leeching such as Thanatos Weapons from [[Ghost Palace]], [https://www.novaragnarok.com/wiki/Custom_Headgear_Quests#Headgears Rideword Hat], and [https://www.novaragnarok.com/wiki/Pet_System loyal Incubus pet], physical classes don't have to rely too much on potions. Assuming you’re playing alone and will occasionally join parties with randoms in PvM environment, you'd want to play a physical type class with good DPS. If you are playing with friends, or if you're going to join parties with other players, tank classes and classes with good support skills are generally always needed.
** [[Verus Equipment Exchange]]
** [[Mallinock's Mechanic Class Guide]]
** [[Vaults' Post-Verus Mado Mechanic Guide]]
* '''Guillotine Cross''': As a physical melee damage dealer, they’re good for solo zeny farming and (with proper equipment and build) for solo instance raids. Most players start out using Rolling Cutter with Katar type weapon as it is more affordable, and later on progress to wielding Dual Daggers. See also [[Kayle's PvM Guillotine Cross Guide]].
* '''Ranger''': A very popular physical ranged damage dealer, due to the ease in playing it and wide availability of its gears. Aimed Bolt type Rangers are the staple DPS for most instance raids. Arrow Storm and Auto Warg type Rangers are more often used for leveling and solo farming. Trap type Rangers are very rare, used mostly for solo play. See also:
** [[Inberun PvE Ranger Guide]]
** [[It's a Trap!!!! - Yomi's Guide to Trap Ranger]]
* '''Rebellion''': Another popular physical ranged damage dealer, they use various firearms to cast offensive and utility skills such as Platinum Altar, Bound Trap, Anti-Material Blast, and Banishing Buster, to name a few. Revolver and Gatling Gun are the most commonly used firearms. See also [[Apple's Guide to PvM Rebellion]].
* '''Oboro''': A unique class that can be build as physical or magical damage dealer. Oboro is overwhelmingly more popular than her male counterpart Kagerou, because she can cast Distorted Crescent, which can buff a target’s ATK and MATK according to their HP and SP. Kunai Splash type Oboro may use weapons and gears from [[Rock Ridge]], while Magic type Oboro may wield Daggers to further boost her magic damage. See also:
** [[Pruu's Guide to Kagerou/Oboro for PVM]]
** [[Game's Secrets of Kagerou/Oboro Guide]]

* '''Royal Guard''': Relatively more newbie-friendly than its counterpart class Rune Knight. Royal Guards have access to a lot of self-buff and party buff skills, on top of having the potential to be a tanky and formidable physical damage dealer. Since they are based on Archer classes, they’re relatively easy to level up and quite affordable for newbies to gear up. They can multi-task as additional damage dealer and debuffer, using skills such as Gloomy Shyness and Harmonize. See also:

* '''Wanderer''': They are usually recruited to BMD and instances where party members need support on SP management. Wanderers are also required to enable ensemble skills with a Minstrel (e.g. Drum on the Battlefield, Invulnerable Siegfried) and chorus skills with other performers (e.g. Song of Mana, Dances with Wargs). Just like Minstrels, they can multi-task as additional damage dealer and support, using skills such as Gloomy Shyness, Poem of Netherworld, Reverberation, Death Valley, and Severe Rainstorm. See also [[Kuno's DPS Wanderer Guide]].
| style="text-align: center;" | Tank
* '''Warlock''': They are not as complicated as Sorcerer, and a moderately geared Warlock can act as crowd control and magic damage dealer in parties. They have access to Ghost element spells, making them a good magic damage dealer in instances like [[Horror Toy Factory]]. Their skill White Imprison makes them invaluable as tanks against difficult bosses such as Biolab 4F bosses in [[Wolfchev's Laboratory]], and bosses that use Dispell such as Valkyrie Randgris. However like other magic damage dealers (and all damage dealers in general), they require a lot of funds to truly be effective. See also [[Mayo's Warlock Guide]].
| style="text-align: center;" | Support
* '''Sorcerer''': Also in demand for instance raiding due to their defensive and supportive spells, including but not limited to using Land Protector, Insignia, endowing weapons with elements, and SP management. Sorcerers require a knowledgeable player and relatively affordable gears to be a good support in parties. They can multi-task as additional magic damage dealer and occasionally as off-tank. See also [[Seandh's Sorcerer Guide]].

* '''Rune Knight''': This class is one of the most versatile physical damage dealer in the game, with a lot of options regarding weapons they can equip, and which skills and build you can focus on. Alhough RKs are not very common in PvM, they are usually build as damage dealers and may use different skills. Dragon Breath is a common build in PvM and PvP. The recent update that allows several multi-hit skills to deal critical damage have also allowed Critical build RKs to flourish. Not to mention the many buffs and effects a Rune Knight gain from their Rune Stones! This class requires dedication and a lot of experimenting to really make it work. See also [https://www.novaragnarok.com/forum/forum/38-swordsman/ Rune Knight discussions in NovaRO Forums].
| style="text-align: center;" | Mechanic
* '''Genetic''': They have access to powerful attack skills such as Acid Demonstration, and utility skills such as Thorn Trap but almost all of their key skills require and consume catalyst items. You will always have to farm these items (or buy them from other players) in order to keep playing Genetic. Not to mention that this class requires not-so-cheap gears to be an effective damage dealer, and you have to spend time to raise your Homunculus and configure its AI properly. Genetic has one of the most steep learning curve of all the job classes in Ragnarok Online, and they can also be one of the most rewarding class to play. See also [[How to Genetic? by AloeLeaflet]].
| style="text-align: center;" | OO
* '''Shadow Chaser''': A unique class that can copy skills from other classes. In PvM, Shadow Chasers are commonly built as a melee magic damage dealer (using copied magic spells via Auto Shadow Spell and Plagiarism), or as a physical ranged damage dealer using Triangle Shot and copied ranged attack skills with a bow and arrow. They are most commonly seen in PvP environment and are almost never seen in PvM. With that said, Bow Chasers may use weapons and gears from [[Rock Ridge]] and be a good additional damage dealer in parties. See also [https://www.novaragnarok.com/forum/forum/46-thief/ Shadow Chaser discussions in NovaRO Forums].
| style="text-align: center;" | OO
* '''Super Novice''': The true jack-of-all-trades, Super Novice has access to almost all First and Second job class skills. In PvM they are commonly build as Support and Flee-based Tank, using a plethora of skills to defend themselves and dodge enemies, and relying on Steel Body as a last resort. In NovaRO, the [[Meteor Strike Quest]] is implemented, which enables Super Novice to take the physical melee path. There are also Super Novices that take the magic path instead, taking advantage of recent patches such as the [https://www.novaragnarok.com/forum/news/fire-basin-card-expansion-r137/ Fire Basin Cards].
