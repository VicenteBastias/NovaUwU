= Builds[OUTDATED] =
Currently, '''Exorcism of Malicious Soul''' appears to be both a bit stronger and easier to build than '''Talisman DPS''' builds, being both strong for solo farming and party play. Also less punishing to die on. Does not mean that '''Talisman DPS''' builds are not good already though, but it will likely take a bit more commitment. It will also see significant buffs in the future. It does win out in MVP dps already , and the various ways Talisman Skills can be used give them their own little advantages.
For the more Supportive side of this Class, '''Full Support''' is essentially a choice for people with little gear that are unable to do proper DPS, or find themselves in parties with plenty of DMG already and choose to focus on survival instead. Less of a Zeny commitment than DPS, it still takes a bit of money to properly support. The '''Exorcism Semi DPS Support''' meanwhile is able to hand out all the buffs along with having a strong cleaning DPS skill, though is also a bit more difficult to play while also using expensive DPS gear all the same.
All these builds are solid and strong in their own ways.
== (Solo / Party) Talisman DPS [OUTDATED] ==
Viable for both Solo and Party play, the various Talisman Skills all have their own little ways to be useful. This guide will be focusing on using '''two''' of them at the same time, namely '''White Tiger''' in combination with any of the other 4 attack spells.
=== Let's talk Talisman Skills![OUTDATED] ===
With this build having 5 different attack skills, all being different from each other, it can be quickly confusing what you want to go for. Which Skills get Equipment for them? Which Skill has the highest DPS? What's the meta?  Which looks the coolest? Which is the most useful? Yada Yada Yada, you get the point.
So, before going into skill builds, gear and the likes, let's talk about this first, shall we?
For those of you which have skipped the Skills section, I will go ahead and summarize them here again real quick ( or, in other words, lemme copy paste this here):
Other than '''Talisman of Soul Stealing''' and '''Circle of Directions and Elementals''' (our DPS ultimate), these skills do not have any After Cast Delay and only have individual Cooldowns. They're also boosted in damage by the buff of our DPS Ultimate. All these skills are also affected by Warm Wind.
* '''Talisman of Soul Stealing:''' A single Target attack skill that deals some damage and regenerates some of your SP. It's damage and SP regeneration are both quite weak, along with it having a significant cooldown and some ACD, so it's not a skill worth investing into nor using. If you want to regenerate SP, use the Soul Reaper skill '''Kaute'''.
* '''Talisman of Blue Dragon:''' A single Target attack skill. 0,3 seconds cooldown. Up to 7500% MATK per cast.
* '''Talisman of White Tiger:''' A 15x15 AOE centered around you. 0,3 seconds cooldown. Up to 5750% MATK per cast.
* '''Talisman of Red Phoenix:''' A targeted 7x7 AOE skill. 0,45 seconds cooldown. Up to 7250% MATK per cast.
* '''Talisman of Black Tortoise:''' A non targeted 11x11 AOE skill. 0,7 seconds cooldown. Up to 9500% MATK per Cast.
* '''Talisman of Four Bearing Gods:''' A targeted 11x11 AOE skill. 1 second cooldown. Up to 12250% MATK per cast.
* '''Circle of Directions and Elementals:''' A targeted 11x11 AOE skill. Our DPS Ultimate, 150AP cost, significant Cooldown. Approximately 42375% MATK, depending on skillbuild. In order to use it, you need to be under the "Blessing of the North" Buff. In order to get this buff, you need to use the Talisman Skills Blue Dragon, White Tiger, Red Phoenix and Black Tortoise once each in this order.
Once using this Ultimate, we get the "Blessing of Four Directions and Five Elements" Buff, which lasts for 300 seconds, gives us 25 S.Matk and significantly strengthens all Talisman attacks other than Talisman of Soul Stealing.
Now, that's a lot of technical stuff, stats and all that. Let's go through each of them real quick and sort out their strengths and weaknesses, in a more gameplay aspect.
*'''Talisman of Soul Stealing:''' Does not get buffed by our Ultimate, has a high CD, some ACD, deals little damage and regenerates little SP. In other words, please do not ever bother with this skill.
*'''Talisman of Blue Dragon:''' The first of the 5 actual attack spells we have for this build, single target and having the same range as Espa, this is the closest we have to our previous SR gameplay. Among all of our skills, it is one of two that have the lowest cooldown of all, too, at 0,3 seconds. Not much else to say about this than that this is a stronger Espa on a bit of cooldown, making it more useable for people with higher ping, and giving you the opportunity to spam another skill inbetween -
*'''Talisman of White Tiger:''' The second skill of the tree and the skill we will be focusing on the most. This is the only skill not needing to select a target and casting instantly upon use. Along with being as spammable as Blue Dragon, you can use this skill on Turbo in the NovaEXT settings and use any of the other spells along with it, both of which will be then used on cooldown (given enough ASPD of course). In order for that to work, the other skill needs to be on ''Smart Cast'', I will explain that stuff in an additional section in a bit. While using this spell, bare in mind that its range is not as high as any of the other skills, so you need to get closer for it to hit.
*'''Talisman of Red Phoenix:''' Based on my testings, both on the more technical area and just gameplay, it feels like the weakest of them all to be honest. Second lowest Cooldown and AoE, but for anyone who has played more around Eshwoo before - we know that 7x7 is really not all that much. Also doesn't quite as much of a use to it as other skills in their own way, being only marginally AoE, having the same range and less DPS than Blue Dragon, this skill seems just.. not as useful? If I want to go for a targeted AoE, I rather take Talisman of Four Bearing Gods, but I suppose this would be less punishing to die on? The skill itself is not bad, but it feels underwhelming compared to the others. If you like it, please don't let me stop you from using it.
*'''Talisman of Black Tortoise:''' It effectively has the highest range out of all of our skills, has a pretty big AoE (being only 2x2 smaller than the Warlock Skill '''Comet'''), has quite some burst to it and you feel the impact of it. Absolutely in my top 3 (out of 5, yes, I don't care) in terms of how much I like it. Its greatest use is in Farming I think, being able to cast it around walls, having a high Range, bursting and still having a fairly manageable cooldown. IMO the best skill to go with in dungeons like Bio5, and if you can oneshot with it, most likely the best farming skill simply because of how flexible it's targeting system makes it.
*'''Talisman of Four Bearing Gods:''' The hardest hitting Talisman Skill we have, but also completely reliant on having the Ultimate buff, meaning if you die while focusing on this skill, your damage just takes a nosedive. Very much an issue with all of these skills, yes, but this one has this issue to the greatest extend, just having your damage split by seven. However, if you're confident in not dying, this is a decent skill to use for both Solo and Party play, being a big targeted AoE with massive damage, making it useful against both MVP and masses of mobs.
* '''Circle of Directions and Elementals:''' Our DPS Ultimate and the very foundation of this build. Reason for that is the buff you get after using it. Without that buff, our damage takes a significant hit, especially Four Bearing Gods, as mentioned this one just takes a complete nosedive and becomes borderline unusuable. Remember to use the 4 God Talisman skills in order so that you can actually use this Ultimate. 
Now, having all that out of the way, let's talk about why White Tiger and not any other skill and how NovaEXT, specifically Turbo and Smart Cast, works.

=== Talisman Build [OUTDATED] ===
Let me preface this by telling you one thing : If you're looking to deal the highest amount of DPS this class has to offer, go for an Exorcism build instead. Dealing more damage than Exorcism is nearly impossible, and best you can do is break even in Single Target DPS with '''Talisman of Blue Dragon''' and '''Talisman of White Tiger'''.
However, this does not mean this is useless or worse than Exorcism.
What this build will lack in damage, it will make up with its other strengths, namely range, safety and versatility.
Its damage is also not thaaat much worse, so don't worry about it, personally I do use both builds depending on what I do. So, before I go jump into gear and the likes, let's talk about its strengths first
==== Strengths over the Exorcism build [OUTDATED] ====
Having less damage than Exorcism, it will not be as useful in areas like Ancient Juperos 3, unless you have an ungodly amount of gear to actually oneshot the monsters there with Talisman skills, at which point this build would become better.
Why? Because first of all, most of your skills are not force targeted on yourself, giving them a tremendously bigger range than Exorcism. The one exception to this is, of course, White Tiger, which essentially is a mini Exorcism.
Not only does that make it easier to snatch mobs across the map, it also makes it safer because they will have much less of an opportunity to attack you. This is especially relevant in areas like Bio 5.
Second of all, they're much more spammable. Even if you use Four Bearing Gods, which has as much of a cooldown as Exorcism, you also have White Tiger to fight with. If mobs just keep on swarming in, this can be very useful.
This build also has a significant advantage in bossfights on your own - you do not have to have a stat contest with them. With Exorcism, you have to use the skill, gather Souls, then use it again, giving you much less time to run. You also either have to debuff the boss or stand in your Totem, which costs more time aswell. Meanwhile, if you're reliant on kiting a boss, you can simply use the Soul Reaper spell '''Esha''' to slow the boss, and run around while using your Four Bearing God skill on cooldown (FBG is best for this due to it having the highest cooldown, giving you more time to simply run, can always spend some time Roaring at the enemy if you feel you can).
However, dying is a lot more punishing as you do rely on your Ultimate Buff. While Exorcism builds can somewhat shrug off dying, Talisman DPS will suffer a lot until you get enough AP to reuse your ultimate. Especially '''Talisman of Four Bearing God''' becomes close to unusuable in this timeframe.
Essentially, what you give away in damage, you get back in versatility, making this as potent of a build as Exorcism to play. Just make sure you survive, no matter what. Now, let's finally get to the part you have all been waiting for.

Choose whichever Talisman attack skill you want along with White Tiger, you will have one point left which I personally simply put into the Totem because why not.
There is the possibility of only going one Skill and adding the points to Soul Mastery for increased damage. You can also skip up on buffs if that's what you want to do for the same reason.
==== Stats and Traits[OUTDATED] ====
You will need a large amount of '''INT''', '''DEX''' and '''AGI''' for this build. Needing high attackspeed, that much is a given. As you could see in the damage tests from earlier, I try to put as much '''AGI''' as possible to max out an ASPD to a point where it doesn't eat too many of my stat points.
'''DEX''' I try to keep reasonably high too, it giving ASPD and MATK makes it very useful.
I dislike playing with less than 130 '''INT''' for no other reason than SP, but you can definitely take some points out of here if you wish.
With ASPD based solo builds, I try to greed away from '''VIT''' as much as I humanly can, but all that depends on the content you're running and how confident you are. Just remember that dying is very painful for Talisman SA.
'''LUK''' is always good to have, giving Curse resistance and a decent amount of MATK. However, not a priority.
'''STR''' for more weight of course. Smallest priority, but nice to have regardless if possible.
When it comes to Traits, you almost always max out SPL to 98-100 (depending on how much you need for that last S.MATK) and put the rest into either CON for more damage, or WIS / STA.
How exactly you go about this depends on your gear, preferences, needs and Yada Yada Yada
==== Equipment[OUTDATED] ====
Currently, there are 2 BiS builds, one using Automatic Armor, Thanatos Accessories and Bacsojin Hat-LT, which is best against Demons, Angels and Dragons, and one based around the Celine set, which is better against everything else. Both have high damage against everything however, so it depends on personal preference which you want to go for. The Celine set is overall going to be more expensive to start out with, so Newbies may be adviced to go for the other build path, as that one has more and cheaper alternatives to start with. Also, if you consider going Blue Dragon and White Tiger mostly, there will be a new Accessory in the near future that combos with {{Item List2|id=550068|item=Spirit Pendulum-LT [2]}} to give both these skills some damage. You may want to consider not going Celine in this case to have an easier time replacing your Accessories.

====== Alternatives [OUTDATED]======
First of, all the Shadow Equipments do not have any alternatives, you can however focus on the {{Item List2|id=24284|item=Gauntlet}} and {{Item List2|id=24315|item=Shield}} first, especially the Shield, and ignore the rest of it for the time being. The shield is important as it gives you a lot of Normal penetration for essentially free, even on +0. It's a massive damage boost for little Zeny, as these 2 items are based on older Monster Hunter enemies.
Then you can go for the Shawl and Brooch to get another 40% pen against everything flatout. The shoes are only there for the 7 stats bonus combo.
Now, for the rest of the gear:
When it comes to '''Upper Headgears''', SA has a history of being.. neglected. Partially because our third class was released really really late, partially because of weird stuff Gravity tried to enforce on our class ( like still using Esma?? ), so when it comes to alternatives, there is only really three : Either have a highly enchanted {{Item List2|id=19487|item=Temporal Circlet}} at the ready for some reason (please do not make one anymore), use the {{Item List2|id=34279|item=Antiquity Cap}} or the {{Item List2|id=19428|item=Illusion Morpheus' Hood}} in combination with the {{Item List2|id=550030|item=Illusion Thorn Staff of Darkness}}. Remember the double {{Item List2|id=29101|item=Spellbound Nives}} Lv1 / 2 enchants if you opt for this! Going for the Illusion Combo also opens up more alternatives in the Shoes area.
For the '''Middle Headgear''', you can simply use a {{Item List2|id=32879|item=Magical Booster [1]}} with 3% Matk and any other stat. The difference of 3% Matk is quite small, but the difference in price between this and the {{Item List2|id=410080|item=Sunglasses}} is humongous.
Same thing for the '''Lower Headgear''' , where your alternatives are {{Item List2|id=5463|item=Rainbow Scarf}} and {{Item List2|id=32200|item=Rosary in Mouth}}. Technically there is also {{Item List2|id=32201|item=Magical Rosary in Mouth}}, but that one is really expensive aswell. Overall you can ignore the lower headgear till you have everything else, as all of the options are not really worth the money until then.
As for the '''Weapon''' Slot, there is obviously the downgrade of {{Item List2|id=550015|item=Patent Spirit Pendulum}}, but also a popular option for Mages in general - the {{Item List2|id=550030|item=Illusion Thorn Staff of Darkness}}.
Being cheap to aquire, upgrade and enchant, it definitely is a very good option to start out with.
For '''Shields''', there are a few alternatives aswell : First of, the Illusion item {{Item List2|id=460017|item=Illusion Guard}} makes for strong and cheap alternative, enchant it with 2 Level 1-2 {{Item List2|id=29101|item=Spellbound Nives}} and it will do you well. The Guard gives you 2 ASPD, but that's sadly not close to the PKS, which gives you 10% ASPD on top of that. As an additional option, there is the {{Item List2|id=32369|item=Mad Bunny}}, the cheapest but also weakest option.
'''Robes''' only really have 3 alternatives, the {{Item List2|id=20934|item=Illusion Engine Wing B-Type}} and {{Item List2|id=480021|item=Automatic Engine Wing B-Type}}. The former is fine for beginners to go for, with either {{Item List2|id=39538|item=Caster}} x2 or {{Item List2|id=39537|item=Fast}} x2 and {{Item List2|id=39542|item=Above All}} x1 enchants, but I wouldn't quite deem this necessary, unless the Int Manteau is absolutely unreachable for some reason. The Automatic Engine is a viable option for Soloplay, it also having ASPD enchants making it easier to reach high ASPD, but will always be worse in Party Play, if you see yourself in that area aswell. An alternative for newbies however would be {{Item List2|id=480019|item=Grace Magic Manteau}} from the tutorial. Can't get a cheaper alternative than that.
As for '''Shoes''', your alternative is {{Item List2|id=22197|item=Illusion Leg B-Type}}. As we have 1,5 seconds fixed cast time, this is the only alternative. The enchants are cookie cutter with {{Item List2|id=39541|item=Fixed Casting}}, {{Item List2|id=39548|item=Spell Buster}} and  {{Item List2|id=39531|item=Vital}}.
However, if you do use the {{Item List2|id=19428|item=Illusion Morpheus' Hood}} and {{Item List2|id=550030|item=Illusion Thorn Staff of Darkness}} combo, you do open yourself up to a few more alternatives, namely {{Item List2|id=470021|item=Grace Magic Boots}} and {{Item List2|id=22004|item=Temporal Boots Of Dexterity}}, both giving you 0,5 fixed cast time reduction. Together with the Costume Stone, the Shoes and the Illusion Combo, you gain enough to make it through. There is also the Thana Accessories, so you technically over do it, but oh well.
If you continue going with Illusion / Automatic Leg when using the Illusion Combo, you can however consider skipping out on the Garment Costume Stone, as you will also have enough with the Thanatos Combo, Illusion Combo and the Leg. ( Just be careful not to use Illusion Leg AND Brilliant Light Sapphire Thanatos Accessories, as that will leave you with 1,4 seconds only)
When it comes to '''Accessories''', we will probably soon-ishly get a new Accessory that will boost the skills '''White Tiger''' and '''Blue Dragon''' in combo with {{Item List2|id=550068|item=Spirit Pendulum-LT [2]}}. No idea what the second Accessory would be at that point, but may very well be just one of the two Thanatos Accessories tbh.
