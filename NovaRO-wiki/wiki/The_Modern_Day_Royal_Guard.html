Status in the Meta

This class, Like the other 4-2/Support Classes are meant to be played in a party setting where they shine the most. Imperial Guard is a class that can somewhat solo once properly geared but requires tons of patience and love for the class. This classs is meant to be an effective tank that has DPS capabilities (Thanks for reminding me Egg!) We might be devo slaves but with our defensive skills and a decent HP pool even built as a DPS/Midline DPS (close to 130k HP with full buffs with a 80-90k HP base) we can fulfill our role easily than some classes and sometimes be the last to go down in a party play with minimal resistance. If you desire to play a clas that can dish out good dps then I advice that you close this guide but if you want to be a good tank that can dish out mid level dps while contributing to a party (and if you love the look of the class and its mount) then I highly encourage you to keep reading!

Do note we still lack alot of gears thats why this class is somewhat limited in builds in the current meta but with the current rework we can somehow manage to survive and hold our own. (Please implement the full skill shadow gears).

In the current Meta we are more DPS oriented than before, making us frontliners, we should not have anymore problems in using our skills but we need gears to really push out and excel in our role in a party.

And with the recent changes, Royal Guards have become formidable in solo content while being useful in specific hard content as a support making this class extremely versatile while being tanky which alot of classes fail to do effectively.

In short whatever path you take Damage wise make sure you make the most of it and enjoy playing this once disgraced class.

Stats

Here's a General Breakdown on how each stat works for us:

Stats
Attribute Notes
STR
 An Primary stat for our attacks since most of our kit scales from ATK (120). Full Tanks would only need to put enough points to carry pots (30-50).
AGI
 A secondary stat that helps in increasing our ASPD and decreasing soft Animation (100-120). Full Tanks have no need for ASPD. You can dump your remaining points here (1-10).
VIT
 A Tertiary stat that boosts our HP and resistance to some status effects (100 total). Full Tanks Primarily have this to boost their HP (120-130).
INT
 A Secondary for decreasing our cast time and increasing our SP (100-110). Full Tanks Primarily have this to Increase the Dodge chance of Prestige (120).
DEX
 A Primary Stat in decreasing our Cast Time ( Shield Chain) and increasing our HIT (120). A secondary-tertiary for Full Tanks just to decrease cast time of Devotion (60-70).
LUK
 This is where you dump your Remaining stat points (1-10). This is a secondary for Full Tanks to increase Perfect FLEE and to maximize the magic dodge chance of Prestige (120).

Traits

Traits are new stat that increases aspects of your character way more than your regular stats and these traits are very powerful as they increase in the percentages not on raw values.<br< These traits cap out at 100 and as a 4th job you do not get regular stats anymore what you get are trait points given at every level
(if your level is a multiple of 5 you get 7 trait points and every level you get 3 (with 200 being a level and a multiple of 5 you get 10))

Stats
Attribute Notes
POW
 A trait that increases your status ATK (the left side of your atk in your main stats) and final physical damage by the % (P.Atk), Increases the damage of Shield Shoooting and Overslash based also on this trait. If you're a DPS Imperial Guard this trait is your primary trait to max out
STA
 aka your RES (Resistance), a new stat that reduces damage by a percentage amount capping out at 50% and RES increases by 1 for every STA and increases by 5 every 3 STA. For Tanking and getting the most out of your Guardian Shield
WIS
 The Magical version of RES called MRES (bruh) MRES follows the same formula as RES but just Magical!
SPL
 The Magical version of POW (stat MATK and S.ATK). works the same way as POW in terms of the formula and skills like Cross Rain and Judgement Cross increase in damage based on this trait.
CON
 A trait that increases alot of factors (P.Atk(for melee a small amount), S.Atk(by a small amount), HIT, and FLEE) but is the primary factor for some classes that rely on Ranged skills as this is their primary trait to boost. If you wanna get extra damage and some hit you can take this trait.
CRT
 a trait that increases Heal Plus (a trait that increases the final heal % of skills that heal) and ofcourse Crit Damage. beneficial for skills like Cannon Spear to increase the skill's damage.

Leveling to Royal Guard in a Hour

Strategy

If you're a new player just follow the NEW Tutorial the game provides upon creating your character and once you finish it you'll skip right through to your second job and get free items to help you out in your journey(1-59). Once you finish the tutorial just do the Eden boards quest and make sure to type @al to get all the loot every mob drops upon death and place everything in storage for your Merchant to sell em for you at marked up prices gaining you zeny early on. Just take all of the quests thats available for you level. It doesn't matter if you finish them all or not you can always turn them in as you level. the more quests you stack the easier it is to gain EXP as a Royal Guard saving you time and effort in leveling. as for stats just focus on STR AGI DEX for you to get more hits in and the highest damage possible you can deal in these early stages of your journey. just spam your Magnum Break and Holy Cross till you get to 85/50. To find the location of the mobs you can use third party sites like Ratemyserver to help you out. If you'd like to know their elemental weakness you can type @mi<space>monster_name.

Once you reach 85 as a non-transcended second class go to the eden group and join the Gramps Quest (the board near the old man near the counter) its where you'll get most of your EXP in order to get to 99/50 as this is a quest best done with a Party(if you'd notice the kill count is at 400 of each mobs its designed to be done in a large 12 man party. You can find people looking for parties by their Chatrooms looking like this:"LFM 85+") and once you turn 99/50 dont turn it in yet go to the main office and change to a High Class Novice. WARNING DO NOT GO STRAIGHT TO THE THIRD CLASS WITHOUT TRANSCENDING AS THE SKILLS YOU NEED FOR CERTAIN BUILDS ARE AVAILABLE UPON TRANSCENDING ESPECIALLY Shield Chain. ONCE YOU GO THIRD CLASS YOU CANNOT GAIN THOSE SKILLS BY ANY MEANS.

DO NOT TURN IN YOUR GRAMPS QUEST JUST YET Now that you've transcended as a High Novice just go kill some Porings until you're Job Level 10. Once you reach that level you can go back to the main office and change into a High Swordsman. Now once a High Swordsman do the Cautious Village quest and once you finish it you'll be warped straight to 69/50 as a High Swordsman. Now go back to the main office and change into the Transcended 2nd Class: Paladin.

NOOOOOWW Do you remember that Gramps board quest? General Gameplay strategy as a New Player

How to Poke/Smite and Block

As a Starting Royal Guard Over Brand is your best friend to clear mobs in an AOE but you would notice that the knockback has been removed, and nonw has a 0.5 sec Fixed Cast time due to the rework so doing the Eden academy quests to get the  Noblesse,  Imperial,  Grace sets is crucial to get rid of the Fixed Cast time the skill has allowing you an easy early game advantage and also a respectable damage. Always talk toHeadmaster Emme to get her quests and do them to unlock the NPCs selling those sets and their refine tickets to +9.

You may also slowly farm Lapis level Costume enchants and slowly transform them into Jades to eventually exchange them in honed Ambers to quickly progress in getting that Casting Stone Onyx from the Costume Enchant System

Shield Chain is a good ranged attack that is much better to use than Banishing Point or Cannon Spear in terms of damage and early on you dont have enough Attack Speed to spam Banishing Point fast so you will be relying on those skills to carry you till 175 and with your Temporal Dex Boots using Shield Chain should be faster in cast time. At 180 I highly advice you take level leeching to End game dungeons to get you to 200 quick.

With the new Eden academy patch and alot of recent releases, Eden Academy has taken its place in terms of serving new and returning players in showing people the ropes on how to play the game and use the various in game commands(and custom in game) the server has to offer. Just remember to keep talking to Headmaster Emme and do her quests to gain access to the Noblesse/Imperial/Grace npcs. If you need more space in a mob situation have Magnum Break ready to give yourself some room to cast your skills. In a party setting you can help out do dps with the new Moonslasher - Overbrand combo and have more freedom in damage thanks to the reworked Earth Drive. RG's do not have the highest ATK or have the best items to start with so you'd only be relying on your weapons to help you out. Make sure to save up for a  White Knight Card and a  Khalitzburg Knight Card to improve your quality of life especially in farming. Having the card combo really eases up your life if I must say so myself.

NOTE:
I HIGHLY DISCOURAGE YOU to build a dedicated tank build early on in your leveling, its simply not worth the effort if the party members can just kill the mobs in one to two shot. Just Help out in clearing the mobs and luring it to the party it should make your life easier and faster!

In the current meta everyone has the power to atleast deal decent amounts of damage with little to no gears so make sure to utilize every skill point, stats, and zeny to your advantage. in a gramps party you are an invaluable tank early game due to your innate tankiness so take advantage of that too but never go full tank.

Royal Guard's power lies in its innate sustain (Having self heals/buffs/etc) so with proper investment you will be able to do most content with ease.

Tips and Tricks

How to play like a Sorcerer, you cycle your skills

For Shield Press and Earth Drive you will have to learn to cycle both skills in order to provide consistent DPS if you dont have the necessary gears to reduce the cooldown of these skills. Personally I use Shield Press first then cast Earth Drive in a certain rythm make sure you check the animation of the damage split to know when to cast your skills.

Did I mention I love Naofumi?

Bored of playing Shield Chain? finding it too clunky to play with? Wanna play Cannon Spear but dont want to play Crit? Do you wanna prove your strength to the world that shunned you using your shield? worry not for this build is enough to satisfy your imperial Guard/Shield Hero offensive needs!

From a skill that used to be a support skill for the other shield skills (Earth Drive/Shield Press/Shield Chain) it now became a beast with the recent rework! Giving it a whopping 14900% base power, a 0.7s cd and an AOE Shield Shooting became a powerhouse for the Imperial Guard Class' arsenal of wide attacking skills. Personally even with the 0.7s cd and a very fast acd it doesnt feel as slow as other spammy skills in fact the multi hits makes up for its lack of a fast cd which is enough to stagger most mobs and proc effects on gears that requires multiple hits. Probably one of the best skills for the Imperial Guard class along with Overslash as they allow for cycling gameplay without worrying about whether you can hit all mobs or not. For leveling as a new Imperial Guard the strong base power is enough to even dent the new 200 mobs even with RES.

Strategy




This is how my skills look like for this build. You can opt using the Melee Shield build's skill distribution since you will always get Shield Shooting at max level anyways plus side is that you can have most of the defensive buffs the class offers while having decent offensive ones. For my build I opted to get max Overslash since I also play Melee Shield and Overslash compliments that build you can opt to do the same with your build if you want more skills that can do more damage and also cycle multiple skills to make your gameplay less boring than just spamming 1 button. Personally what I love about this build is that you can have decent damage but have access to the innate tank your class offers and even have room for Devotion should the situation call for it (Just use the skill reset/manager npc to move stuff a bit!). In short you are useful as a back dps or as a tank/off tank and that is versatile as you can get!

Tips and Tricks

Used to be just a buff skill now it can do some serious dent on tough enemies!

There isnt much to say except to stack a ton of Ranged Modifiers, Raw ATK, and a high refined level 5 weapon for the extra P.Atk. From what you could see now it only hits for this much with the limited Ranged modifier gears I have and also a lack of a proper Lvl 5 weapon to use this skill to its max potential but hopefully with more content present more gears would appear to boost this skill!

Strategy

Do just wanna launch your spear like the power of a Cannon? Then this build is for you!

An Unexpected build to be viable for us.

Exceed Break and Cannon Spear of the Skills of Royal Guards that can CRIT; A skill that buffs your next normal auto attack to deal great damage in a single strike. Weapon weight also increases its damage so in order to maximize the damage we'll stack as much CRIT Rate, CRIT % modifiers, ATK % modifiers, and also the heaviest weapon we can use to increase its damage. Tips and Tricks
Devotion Build

In Devo tanking (also known as Off-Tanking) it is important to stack as much HP as you can so you could soak up the most damage possible for people you will protect. The main skill you will be using is ofcourse Devotion, you will be using this to take the damage for your party/guild members in order to give them a chance to live a little longer in fights. By doing this you'd find yourself always spamming healing items to restore your HP so stock up as much as you can.

Devotion in general explanation casts a blue link that allows the Royal Guard to take damage in behalf of the linked party member but it also has extra mechanics:

  • The linked party member cannot be interrupted while casting as long as the link is present
  • Shield Buffs the Royal Guard currently has gets passed on to the linked Party Member once Devotion is cast in a PVP setting, but requires the Royal guard to recast the said buffs once Devotion is cast on a party member in a PVM setting.
  • The link has a max range of 11 cells, if you or the linked party member goes out of the range the skill will not work but will continue once the distance is met again.
  • The number of members you can link to increases each level up to a cap of 5.
  • YOU CANNOT USE THIS ON OTHER ROYAL GUARDS sad RG noises :(

With those info in mind you should be able to atleast get a hold of how to use the skill.

Who to Cast Devotion

Here are some classes/classification to consider casting Devotion to. Take note in a PVP/WOE setting mae sure to pay attention to your GL and teammates for proper communication:

  • DPS(Mainly RK, GX, Ranger, etc): TOP PRIORITY, They are the front-liners who kill things for you. They are notorious for commonly having numbers above 500k-1m in terms of damage. Having them dead leaves the whole party/guild in a vulnerable position(Possibly killing your whole party). It is better you die from taking damage from them than them dying as they are a huge asset to the team. These classes tend to move alot due to them having close-ranged skills in order to engage with their targets so it is important that you follow them closely to keep the Devotion up.
  • Support(AB, Wanderer/Minstrel): TOP-MID PRIORITY, These are the ones responsible for keeping you and your party alive and kicking. They Provide you with invaluable buffs that enables classes to perform at their full potential. Based on their skill set they tend to have the most defensive buffs making them somewhat self-sustaining in terms of survival making them not much a priority to Devote to in some situations. Depending on the situation or depending on the call of the guild leader you will need to stick with them to ensure their survival.
  • Tanks(Sorcerer, Doram, Sura, etc):MID Priority, They function mostly as secondary supports that can tank damage should the initial tank fall. In some parties they act as the main tank taking damage for the party. It is your job to buy them the most time in survival by you guessed it, casting Devotion on them too. if they die, same thing with DPS, the whole party dies too so make sure you keep a close distance on them.

Proper positioning and enough mob lure is the key to making a good party run. In tanking it is important to only draw a good amount of aggro from few mobs to give your DPS a chance to focus on them. Too many mobs can destroy your FLEE so like they say "Bite only what you can chew."

For General tanking just get enough mobs to atk you and just bring them over to your dps' for them to kill it. Rinse and Repeat

Magic Build: The (Ironic)Sword Build (Ray of Genesis/Cross Rain)

Overview

General Guide


Do you believe in god? Do you like Magic? Do you like Magic Knights? Do you like smiting the heathens with the wrath of God himself? Well look no further, Tarnished one. Royal Guards hold the power of the Divine (And pagan Neutral) through Ray of Genesis!




Recommended Stats:

  • STR 70-100
  • AGI 50+
  • VIT 100+
  • INT 120+
  • DEX 120+
  • LUK 50



  • POW 1
  • STA 1
  • WIS 1
  • SPL 100
  • CON 97
  • CRT 1



The Stat distribution focuses on mostly INT, DEX, LUK for the maximum Magical damage, Cast Time Reduction possible with atleast some points in STR for weight purposes.

Smite the non-believers! Demolish thy foes with Glorious Light! Big thank you Dano and Rekij for showing me your builds! and also Big thank you to Chimpanc Inalmbrico for providing me with initial info for this build!



Ray of Genesis/Genesis Ray is one of our Magic bestowed by Odin himself to his faithful, smiting enemies with his divine light. A somewhat counterpart to the Arch Bishop's Adoramus; This build focuses on dealing as much Holy/Neutral Magical damage in an aoe. After the recent reworks and gear updates the server had this build went from being one of the most unused, disregarded, into one of a build worthy of playing and inveting time and money. Currently as it stands this build is very effective against targets that are naturally weak to Holy (Shadow/undead/Demon) and can also turn into Neutral should we face targets that are of the holy variant. As for the stats you may want to focus on INT and DEX for these stats are responsible for reducing our cast time and increasing our damage for this skill with Agi, Vit, Luk to provide some aspd, suvivability/tanking capabilities, and a chance to dodge magic (Via Prestige's LUK parameter)


Previously, players who want to play this build would result to getting as much damage as possible using this skill by stacking alot of MATK, MATK%, Elemental %, etc to alleviate the downside of its cooldown of 2 seconds but with the recent updates we are now able to spam this skill at 2x per second via the combination of the recently Released  Illusion Excalibur [1] to combo with the  Illusion Sacred Mission [1] (provides 1s cooldown) and also the recent Genesis Shadow Gears release (provides 0.5s cooldown) making this build more than enough to compete in regular content with relative ease

With the recent reworks Genesis Ray now has a base 3500% at max level regardless of element (Previously 2300% without inspiration/3000% with inspiration) and one of the main aspects of this skill is that it changes to Neutral via Inspiration in case you get screwed by Holy targets. Take Note that Magic is locked into one element so learning/Building to use Neutral is another strategy you might encounter. in this part of the guide I will be focusing on the general aspects of the Build in terms of gear but in terms of what is Best in Slot I will leave it up to the readers to do their own calculations.

Also dont forget to use Holy Shield to boost your holy element Ray of Genesis its basically a free buff.
