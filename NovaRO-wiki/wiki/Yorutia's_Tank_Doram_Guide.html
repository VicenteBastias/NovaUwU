Preface

Hi there, this is Yorutia (aka Yorunyaa), and welcome to my personal guide for (full-/support-)tank dorams!

I am probably not the most qualified person to be writing this guide, as I am sure there are many tank doram players out there that are way more experienced than me. However, given the complete lack of information on the wiki on this super fun and, in my opinion, underrated build, I've decided to write this guide to hopefully show people the viability and appeal of tank dorams. So know that by no means is this gonna be a definitive guide for tank dorams. On the contrary, I am expecting this guide to generate many discussions, or invoke comments or suggestions from the more knowledgeable playerbase.

Two more remarks before we begin:

First, please note that this is a specific guide for tank dorams, not a general doram/summoner guide. If you want to learn about doram/summoner in general, see the page Summoner instead.

Second, you may find this guide focusing quite heavily on late/end game. This is because tank doram is primarily a late-game build. There is simply not much use for a tank in early/mid game, and I simply would not recommend tank doram as a starter build. With that said, if you do need some help in early-/mid-game leveling or farming, check out the general Leveling Guide, and perhaps Ced's Physical Doram Guide (you can switch to tank build later on). Or if you have the patience, you may also just tank/support in Gramps parties all the way till max level.

Overview

This guide is going to cover two similar but slightly different builds, with rather distinct play styles: the full-tank doram and the support-tank doram.

A full-tank doram focuses on their own sturdiness, and walks in front of their entire party to lure enemies and absorb damages (and debuffs), making the lives of their squishy party members much easier.

Meanwhile, a support-tank doram focuses more on improving the survivability of their party members (be them DPS or another tank) by shielding and healing them, all while still being quite tanky themselves.

If you need a quick overview to decide whether or not to try out this build, here are some points to consider:

Full-tank/support-tank dorams:

  • Pros
    • Arguably the tankiest build in the game (do you know that dorams have the highest base HP in the game, even higher than Royal Gaurds or Rune Knights?)
    • Require less funds than most DPS builds to work
    • Invaluable in parties, especially for challenging late-/end-game contents
    • Relatively simple (yet not brainless) gameplay
    • Self-sufficient; do not need to spam consumables or rely on ally to sustain
    • You can fill the screen with flying tunas!!
  • Cons
    • 404 damage not found... absolutely abysmal damage output
    • Practically impossible to solo due to the lack of damage just mentioned
    • Although not as expensive as DPS builds, still requires some investment to be relevant late-game
    • Not a good starter build (because of the above-mentioned points)
    • Many people think it's just a meme build (but I assure you it's not!)

If these all sound good to you, then great! Get ready to be a pro tank cat!

If it does not sound like something for you, no problem, and I wish you good luck in finding other builds/classes that spark your interest.

And if you are still hesitant, just read on! You might find yourself falling in love with tank doram as you find out more features about it.

Stats

Stats are pretty straight forward for tank dorams. Your top priority is to boost your Max HP as much as possible (since both Tuna Party and self-cast Tuna Belly scale with your Max HP, see Skills), which means you want a lot of VIT. Resistance to negative status effects is then your second priority.

  • STR (1+): Only useful for increasing weight limit. Dump your leftover points here.
  • AGI (100+): Increases your FLEE and ASPD – FLEE can be helpful in mitigating damage in some situations, although not critical for tank dorams; ASPD is useful for spamming no-cooldown Tuna Party or Tuna Belly. Also grants immunity to Sleep and Bleeding at 100†, and resistance to Burning.
  • VIT (120): Your primary stat. Boosts your Max HP and makes you more tanky, as simple as that. Also increases health potions effectiveness, and grants immunity to Stun and Poison at 100†. However, I would not advise spending points beyond 120 base VIT, as it is too expensive and forces you to sacrifice a lot of other stats.
  • INT (100): Improves SP pool and regen for care-free skill spamming. Also grants immunity to Silence and Blind at 100†, and resistance to Fear.
  • DEX (50-100): Only useful for reducing variable cast time (VCT) and slightly increasing ASPD. Your bread-and-butter skills all have zero VCT, so reducing VCT is not your priority. With that said, it is still good to have some VCT reduction for skills like Lope. (Note that VCT reduction through stats is non-linear, so putting too many points is suboptimal.)
  • LUK (100): As for every class, a nice all-round stat that is always good to have some. Gives you a bit of Perfect Dodge and FLEE to help mitigate some damage. Also grants immunity to Curse and Confusion at 100†, and resistance to Burning and Fear.

†If your enemy has a higher base level than you, a penalty applies and you might not get full immunity. For the actual immunity/resistance formulae, see here.

Skills

The Summoner's skill tree is incredibly simple. There are only three branches, Life (animals), Land (veggies) and Sea (seafood). Each branch is completely linear. For tank dorams, you'd want to dump basically all of your skills points into the Sea branch, sparing just a few for some utilities from other branches.

With that said, while the skill tree is simple, the skills themselves still require some explanations in order for one to understand their full potential. So allow me to explain a few most imporant ones here (in extensive details).

Core Skills

There are three core skills that are truly the bread and butter for tank dorams: Tuna Party, Tuna Belly and Stoop

Skill Description / Properties Explanation / Remarks
Tuna Party

Summon a tuna that shields an ally from damage (and circles around the target like an UFO) for 30 seconds.

  • Levels: 5 (selectable)
  • Target: Ally
  • Range: 9
  • SP Cost: 10 + (10 x Skill Level)
  • Cast Time (Variable + Fixed): 0.0s + 0.0s
  • After Cast Delay: 1.0s
  • Cooldown: 10.0s + (4.0s x Skill Level)
  • Shield Amount: (10 x Skill Level)% of your Max HP (doubled if Spirit Of Sea is learned)
  • Shield Duration: 30.0s

Recommended Level (Learn): 5
Recommended Level (Use): 1, 2 or 5 (see explanation below)

What this skill actually does (mechanics):
This skill is incredibly strong. At level 5, with Spirit Of Sea learned, it grants a shield that equals to 100% of your Max HP, meaning that you can essentially double your Max HP for 30 seconds!

Moreover, this overpowered tuna is not like your regular Priest/Arch Bishop's shields, in that:

  • It blocks both physical and magical damage
  • It has no limit on the number of hits that it can take (only total damage)
  • It cannot be Dispelled
  • It scales off your Max HP, not your target's (meaning that you can cast it on a level 1 Novice and have them tank an MVP, LOL)
  • It also prevents knock-backs (important for Stoop)

The shield also applies after hit check and damage reduction, meaning that defensive stats like DEF/MDEF, FLEE and Perfect Dodge, or items like  Khalitzburg Knight Card, are still useful.

Limitations and how to overcome:
The only limitation of this skill is its cooldown (CD). It has a whopping 30.0s CD at level 5 (same as the shield duration). However, it can be easily reduced by using  Cute Grass Necklace [1] or  Fresh Tuna Talisman [1]. Equiping two  Cute Grass Necklace [1] will reduce the CD by 14s, meaning that a level 1 cast will have no CD at all, and a level 5 cast will have only 16.0s CD (almost halved!). If you go as far as to also equip two  Automatic Module (Power of Sea), you can even cast level 2 without CD.

Usage:
Depending on the situtation, sometimes it is actually better to spam level 1 or 2 with no CD than to use the max level. The total amount of damage that a level 1/2 spam can absorb over time can be higher than using a level 5 every 10.0s-16.0s (given that you are not expecting some big "one-shot damage"). If you are playing as a support tank, you will also want to cast level 1 or 2 with no CD on multiple party members. A level 2 shield already has a strength of 40% of your Max HP, which is usually more than your DPS friends' Max HP already.

Remark:
This skill has a very interesting property that it has no animation on the caster (you) at all, but on its target instead. This means that your ASPD will not limit the spamming speed. So given enough after cast delay (ACD) reduction, you can easily summon, like, 7 tunas per second, and shield the entire party in a wink (using turbo). Be careful, however, to not spam it continuously on one ally, as the animation on them can prevent them from moving or using their skills.

Tuna Belly

Summon(?) a delicious toro sushi that heals an ally for a fraction of their Max HP.

  • Levels: 5 (selectable)
  • Target: Ally
  • Range: 9
  • SP Cost: 10 + (10 x Skill Level)
  • Cast Time (Variable + Fixed): 0.0s + 1.0s
  • After Cast Delay: 1.0s
  • Cooldown: (3.0s x Skill Level) − 1.0s (− 5.0s if Spirit Of Sea is learned)
  • Heal Amount: (10 x Skill Level − 10)% of the target's Max HP

Recommended Level (Learn): 4
Recommended Level (Use): 2 or 4 (see explanation below)

What this skill actually does (mechanics):
Just a simple single-target healing that scales with the target's Max HP. Nothing fancy but does the job (really well).

Since it scales with the target's Max HP, it is extremely effective on tanks with large HP pool like Devotion Royal Guards, Tiger Cannon Suras, and of course, yourself. Unlike the Acolyte class' healing skills, Tuna Belly bypasses various restrictions, and is able to heal even players in Undead status, Machanic's Madogear, or Lord Knight's Berserk.

Limitations and how to overcome:
Tuna Belly really has not much limitations once you are geared. However, without gears, its 1.0s Fixed Cast Time (FCT) and 1.0s ACD can really restrict when and how you can use it. So to combat this, you would need to have 1.0s FCT reduction and tons of ACD reduction (aim at >56% so that in a party with a Bard, you can get >86% total reduction thanks to A Poem Of Bragi). FCT reduction is now quite easy to achieve since the introduction of  Casting Onyx [G]. ACD reduction has to be obtained from various gears. I will write more about gearing in the Equipment section.

Usage:
Once again, similar to Tuna Party, you may find yourself wanting to use level 2 or 4 of the skill depending on the situation.

Level 4 heals for 70% Max HP nominally, but with the healing bonus from Power of Sea and a single  Cute Grass Necklace [1], that already amounts to 87.5% Max HP, which is all you'll ever need from a single heal.

However the issue with using level 4 is the long CD. Using level 2, on the other hand, has no CD at all when Spirit Of Sea is learned. So with 1.0s FCT reduction and enough ACD reduction, you can spam the skill and heal much faster and more consistently than any Arch Bishop could ever dream to (in terms of single-target healing). With healing bonuses, level 2 Tuna Belly can usually heal for about 40% Max HP. Imagine spamming, like, five 40%-Max-HP healings per second, which is just god-like. Ever since I got my core FCT and ACD reduction gears, I rarely find myself using level 4 Tuna Belly anymore.

You should never need to use level 5.

Remark 1:
Some people consider Tuna Belly a pure support skill. But in my opinion it is also an excellent full-tank skill. It allows you to rely less on external healing (your support friends may not always be able to heal you in an emergency, and don't forget the times you get the Undead status debuff). I always spam Tuna Belly on myself whenever I am tanking a bunch of mobs, even if I have my Tuna Party shield on. This way, even if the shield breaks for whatever reason, I can instantly heal back up and continue to tank via insane self-healing.

Remark 2:
An extra detail related to RO skill cast mechanics: Tuna Belly has an animation delay that cannot be reduced by ACD reduction or ASPD. You might notice this simply by spamming the skill, where you will occasionally see two quick successive casts, just to be followed by a long delay (roughly half a second), no matter how high your ACD reduction and ASPD are. Luckily, moving or being attacked cancels such animation and effectively nullifies the delay. What this means is, when you are taking damage from tanking mobs (which is exactly when you would need the Tuna Belly), you can actually spam faster. So practically, a full-tank can still utilise up to 86% ACD reduction and 193 ASPD despite the animation delay. A support-tank, however, will always spam a bit slower (unless you actively cancel the animation by moving).

Stoop

Turn into an impenetrable furball for 6 seconds, reducing all incoming damage by 90% (because you are so adorable that enemies simply cannot, in all good conscience, attack you at full strength).

  • Levels: 1
  • Target: Self
  • SP Cost: 10
  • Cast Time (Variable + Fixed): 0.0s + 0.0s
  • After Cast Delay: 1.0s
  • Cooldown: 15.0s
  • Damage Reduction: 90%
  • Damage Reduction Duration: 6.0s

Recommended Level: 1

What this skill actually does (mechanics):
Stoop is a bit like a variant of Monk's Steel Body, in the sense that they both reduce incoming damage by a massive fixed percentage. But compared to Steel Body, Stoop is more temporary (lasts only 6 seconds), does not allow movement, and allows normal attacks and skills usage.

Limitations and how to overcome:
The biggest limitation of Stoop is that it is instantly cancelled the moment you start moving. This also includes "forced movement", i.e. enemies' knock-backs. And once it's cancelled, you must wait for the 15.0s CD before it can be reused.

What all these imply is that the timing for using Stoop is extremely important. If you are out of position when you Stoop, there's no way to reposition without cancelling the effect. Also, if you Stoop at a moment when an enemy is about to knock you back, the skill would just be wasted.

Luckily, you already have in your kit exactly what are needed to overcome this limitation: Tuna Party's shield prevents knock-backs, so as long as you Stoop with an active shield, you will be safe. Remember that Tuna Party's damage reduction applies before the shield depletion, meaning that it is highly unlikely for the shield to run out while you are Stooping. And for positioning, you might want to Lope to a good spot before Stooping (note that using Lope while Stooping will cancel the effect).

Usage:
Other than what's mentioned above, the usage is simple: when you are taking or about to take a lot of damage, Stoop. Just bear in mind that there is a 15.0s CD, and therefore using the skill does create a 9.0s window where you are more vulnerable. To make full use of the skill, just be a bit more aggressive when Stoop is off CD, but a bit more careful otherwise.

Remember that you can use Tuna Belly to heal yourself or Tuna Party to fortify while Stooping.

Remark 1:
The 90% reduction is a separate effect from DEF/MDEF and class / size / racial / elemental reductions. It applies after soft DEF/MDEF (flat reduction). So unfortunately, it does not make soft DEF/MDEF any more useful. Any kind of DEF/MDEF piercing from enemies will not affect the 90% reduction. Basically, whatever damage you would have taken without Stooping, will be cut to 10% with Stooping. Simple and intuitive.

Remark 2:
In Bio 5, Stooping at the right time can sometimes let you survive a Max Pain and avoid a party wipe. (For those who don't know, some monsters can enter a state called Max Pain, in which they reflect all incoming damage to every player on screen. The most famous Max Pain user is probably Margaretha Sorin (Bio 5 Arch Bishop), whose Max Pain is triggered by players' ranged physical attacks (that did not one-shot her)). If your Bio 5 party has someone that can trigger Sorin's Max Pain (e.g. a Sharp Shooting Ranger that could not one-shot), pay attention to the iconic orange glow of Max Pain on Sorin, and try to Stoop when you foresee a potential party wipe. (It is sooo satisfying when you actually pull that off.)

Other Notable Skills

Skill Description / Properties Explanation / Remarks
Lope

Leap toward a targeted location.

  • Levels: 3 (fixed)
  • Target: Ground
  • Range: [2 + (4 x Skill Level)] cells
  • SP Cost: 10 x Skill Level
  • Cast Time (Variable + Fixed): 1.0s + 0.0s
  • After Cast Delay: 1.0s
  • Cooldown: 2.0s x Skill Level

Recommended Level: 3 (well, not that we have a choice...)

A very handy skill for you to quickly reposition yourself, which is something invaluable for a tank. Use it whenever you are out of place (i.e. further away from threats than your DPS, for whatever reasons; jump towards the threats in this case), or if you are taking or about to take too much damage and/or want to preserve your HP/shield a little bit (jump away from the threats in this case, but not directly towards your DPS or too far away that loses aggro; this can buy you a few good seconds from taking melee damage).

Contrary to popular belief, Lope cannot be used to leap over obstacles. But this is actually a good thing, because this also means that you can use it anywhere, even on maps/instances where teleportation is restricted. (Why would you want to jump over walls as a tank/support anyway? To separate yourself from your allies?) Just remember to always target a spot with a clear line of sight, otherwise it won't work.

Also beware that with a normal tank build, this skill will likely have a little bit a VCT, and is interruptable. You might not be able to cast it while being attacked by too many mobs. Unless you have a shield, that is.

Arclouse Dash

Summon the spirit of the Arclouse to buff a single target with bonus AGI and Movement Speed.

  • Levels: 5 (selectable)
  • Target: Ally
  • Range: 9 cells
  • SP Cost: 10 + (2 x Skill Level)
  • Cast Time (Variable + Fixed): 1.0s + 0.0s
  • After Cast Delay: 1.0s
  • Cooldown: 10.0s x Skill Level
  • AGI Bonus: 15 + (5 x Skill Level)
  • Movement Speed Bonus: −0.04 sec/cell
  • Bonus Duration: 50.0s + (10.0s x Skill Level)

Recommended Level: 1-5

This skill is in the Life (animal) branch, but it is worth spending a few points for it.

The up to +40 AGI bonus is quite nice as it gives a decent amount of FLEE, and ASPD for spamming no-CD skills. But what is truly useful here is actually the Movement Speed Bonus.

As a tank, positioning is the most important aspect of your gameplay. You are supposed to stand on the very front line and lead the party, but that is difficult to do if you are walking slower than everyone else. Summoner has not much built-in mobility (there's Lope, but it has an irreducible 6.0s CD), so a reliable Movement Speed buff is priceless. I emphasised "reliable" because, (again) unlike Acolyte's Increase Agility, Arclouse Dash cannot be Dispelled, can still apply under Undead status effect, and is not cancelled by Quagmire (you get all these very often when tanking in Bio 5, for example). Plus, you do not have to rely on others to buff you.

Always keep the buff active for yourself (unless you are using  Guyak Pudding). If you also got yourself a  Halter Lead, you can move just as fast as any other "conventional" tanks.

Note that the Movement Speed bonus is the same non-stackable bonus as Increase Agility. Like most other Movement Speed bonus, it stacks with  Halter Lead, but not Wind Walk,  Speed Potion or  Guyak Pudding.

Grooming

Clean yourself up: purges various negative status effects, and momentarily increase FLEE.

  • Levels: 5 (selectable)
  • Target: Self
  • SP Cost: 15
  • Cast Time (Variable + Fixed): 0.0s + 1.0s
  • After Cast Delay: 1.0s
  • Cooldown: 70.0s - (10.0s x Skill Level)
  • FLEE bonus: 100
  • FLEE bonus duration: 2.0s + (1.0s x Skill Level)
  • Purgeable status effects: Bleeding, Deep Sleep, Fear, Freezing, Frozen, Mandragora Howling, Poison, Silence, Sleep, Stun

Recommended Level: 5

Honestly, this skill isn't very useful under most circumstances, because most of the curable status effects actually forbid you from using the skill in the first place... The only status effects that can be and are useful to remove are Fear, Freezing (≠ Frozen) and Mandragora Howling.

The +100 FLEE bonus can be useful sometimes, but FLEE does not help you much in surviving a large mob due to the mob penalty (FLEE is reduced by 10% for every additional enemy attacking you beyond two). With that said, it might still be a good habit to Groom from time to time on maps where you don't have to worry about Fear, Freezing or Mandragora Howling.

Purring

Make a pleasant healing sound: applies the effects of Grooming to all party members in sight.

  • Levels: 5 (selectable)
  • Target: Self
  • SP Cost: 75 - (5 x Skill Level)
  • Cast Time (Variable + Fixed): 0.0s + 1.0s
  • After Cast Delay: 1.0s
  • Cooldown: 65.0s - (5.0s x Skill Level)
  • FLEE bonus: 100
  • FLEE bonus duration: 5.0s + (2.0s x Skill Level)
  • Purgeable status effects: Bleeding, Deep Sleep, Fear, Freezing, Frozen, Mandragora Howling, Poison, Silence, Sleep, Stun

Recommended Level: 5

This is slightly more useful than Grooming. It has more or less the same effect, but works on the entire party, and has slightly longer duration but also longer CD. It is more useful because you can actually use the skill while your allies are under status effects but not you. But the long CD is making it quite difficult to use. Anyway, it's nice to keep in mind that you have this is in your kit.

Equipment

Goals

Before we dive into the list of recommended / note-worthy equipment, I think it'd be useful to first briefly go through what we are trying to achieve here.

The stats that we are looking for are (some of these have been mentioned in the previous sections, but let's summarise nonetheless):

  • Max HP (VIT / Bonus Max HP %):
    • Both Tuna Party and self-cast Tuna Belly scale with your Max HP.
    • Even without these skills, Max HP by itself is a very effective tank stat in RO.
    • Given the fact that Dorams have the highest base HP in game, increases from VIT / Bonus Max HP % are extra benefitial.
You want as much MAX HP as possible.
  • Damage reduction (DEF / MDEF / Resistance):
    • Damage reductions practically increase your "effective HP" when tanking.
    • DEF / MDEF from equipment (aka "Hard" DEF / MDEF) cuts incoming physical/magical damage by a fixed percentage.
      • The damage reduction formulae can be found here: DEF / MDEF
      • A convenient way to understand the formulae is that every 400 DEF / 100 MDEF increases your effective HP in tanking physical / magical damage by approximately 100% (the actual effect is slightly less).
    • Class / Size / Racial / Elemental resistance cuts incoming damage from particular types of monsters / attacks by a fixed percentage.
      • Resistance effects stack multiplicatively with DEF / MDEF and resistance of other types, but additively with resistance effects of the same type.
      • Note that damage reduction (unlike damage increase) is more effective when stacked additively than multiplicatively.
As a full tank, you want as much damage reduction as possible; as a support tank, damage reduction is nice to have but not a priority.
  • Tuna Party Cooldown Reduction:
    • The more frequently you can recast Tuna Party, the more effective HP you have as a tank / your allies have with you as support.
    • The maximum reduction achievable (as of the time of writing) is 20.0s.
As a support tank, you want at least 14.0s reduction to spam level 1 Tuna Party, or ideally 18.0s to spam level 2; as a full tank, you should have 12.0 - 20.0s reduction (depending on play style and trade-off with other stats).
  • Fixed Cast Time Reduction:
    • Reducing Tuna Belly's 1.0s FCT is extremely useful.
As a support tank, having 1.0s FCT reduction is a must; as a full tank, you still want at least 0.5s reduction, ideally 1.0s.
  • After Cast Delay Reduction:
    • Skills that has no CD and 1.0s ACD (level 2 Tuna Belly) can utilise up to 86% ACD reduction.
    • Since Tuna Party has no self animation, at least theoretically, it can utilise up to 100% ACD reduction (when casted on different party members).
As a support tank, you want at least 56% reduction from equipment, such that with A Poem Of Bragi you reach 86%; as a full tank, high ACD reduction is essential for reliable self healing, but is not necessarily a priority (depending on play style).
  • Attack Speed:
    • Similar to ACD reduction, it is needed for spamming skills like level 2 Tuna Belly.
    • Not crucial, and also quite difficult to get from tank gears.
    • An ally Wanderer's Swing Dance plus an  Awakening Potion would be enough to cover your need in most cases.
Nice to have some if possible, but prioritise other stats first.

 Alternative (for full tank)

While this provide 2.0s less CD reduction than  Cute Grass Necklace [1], it adds a chance to auto-cast Tuna Party on yourself when tanking mobs.

This is quite useful for full tanks, because the auto-casting works even if the skill is on cooldown meaning it cannot be used manually. The auto-casting also will not trigger or reset the cooldown of the skill. In other words, the auto-casting just gives bonus shields with no down side at all.

Note, however, that the auto-casting is only triggered when the player receives actual damage. This implies that it cannot be used to refresh the duration of an active Tuna Party, as the player will take no damage under the active shield.

Remark: Wearing one  Cute Grass Necklace [1] and one  Fresh Tuna Talisman [1] with two  Automatic Module (Power of Sea) exactly nullifies the 18.0s CD of level 2 Tuna Party.

As you can see from the table, Illusion Goibne's Set is a simple and effective choice due to its sheer amount of Max HP, VIT and DEF.

On the other hand, Automatic Set is a good all-round choice. While it does not have a Max HP / VIT bonus as high as the other sets, it compensates by having the highest DEF and/or MDEF, and resistances to all enemies (from the garment enchants). The VIT Soutane and Gold Dragon Plate sets are viable alternatives, especially when gearing up against specific monsters. However, do take note that they will not provide (enough) FCT reduction for instant-casting Tuna Belly or some other skills. (I personally love the self-healing play style, and therefore tend to not recommend these sets.)
