Eden Group is a secret organisation formed to help adventurers by providing various quests which rewards with EXP and exclusive equipment. Eden Group leveling quests are extremely beneficial to players as it allows a faster-paced leveling compared to regular hunting, and the equipment quests gives top-tier weapons and equipment for players from level 10 up to 100.

In NovaRO, Eden Group is also expanded upon by the addition of Eden Academy, where Noblesse, Imperial, and Grace sets are provided to members instead of their original sources (16.1, 16.2, and 17.1 respectively). This expands the gears given to players to reach up to Level 150.

Eden Group Tips

  • You can walk to Eden Group HQ by going to Izlude and entering the Northmost building, and passing through the Eden Academy Reception.
  • You can warp to Eden HQ by using @go Eden or @go 36. Using the  Eden Group Mark as a consumable will also warp you into Eden HQ (very useful for Hardcore players).
  • You can warp to Eden Academy by using @go Academy, which is in the same map as Eden HQ, just in a different room.
  • You can use the @edenquest at any moment to guide you through the Eden quests. It will give indications on what you have to do next. Alternatively, keeping tabs of the quest in Alt+U is also recommended.
  • On top of levelling quests and equipment exchanges, Eden also has useful NPCs such as:
    • The Claw - A machine that dispenses hats in exchange for Gold Coins. Cycles through sets of hats every 1st and 15th of every month.
    • Reno (Crystal Synthesis) - Official NPC that provides Crystal Synthesis Quests that reward the player with currency used to improve certain gear. He also provides a buff to the player that lasts a considerable amount of time.
    • Ramses (Master Cook) - Custom NPC that is meant to support players in bulk cooking various Stat Dishes. The NPC still asks for the required materials and also calculates success based on the Player's Stats.
    • Cat Hand Services - Official NPC that provides an alternative quest to give player the proper Quest Requirements that is equivalent to finishing the Continental Guard Quest. A Requirement for starting the New World Quests as well as the Banquet of Heroes Quest lines.
    • Royal Errand Boy - Official NPC that initializes the Banquet of Heroes Questline as soon as you meet the requirements (Level 100 and finished with Continental Guard Quest or Cat Hand Dimensional Travel). Will be hidden once you finish the Banquet Questline.
    • Normalson - Official NPC functioning as a standard Refiner. Present at the 2nd Floor.
    • Ben Recycle - Custom NPC that provides a service to convert certain equipment into Rough Elunium or Rough Oridecon. Present at the 2nd Floor.
    • Nightmarish Jitterbug Instance - An Instance that is part of the Demon God episode but not necessarily a required one. Shows the primary storyline for the Newoz guild crew as well as giving various equipment.
    • Warp to Paradise Market - an Official Map designated as a master NPC marketplace meant to standardize certain materials to a Zeny value. Almost all NPC sold equipment is present here. There are also NPCs that offer currency exchanges (New World Currencies, Dragon Orbs, and Dynite Ores). You can also warp here using @go Eden Market or @go 52.
    • Veteran Sean - Custom NPC in charge of the Weekly Item Delivery Turn-ins.
    • Maggie Arth - Custom NPC in charge of the Weekly Treasure Hunt Quests.
    • Nelloz - Custom NPC in charge of the Weekly Instance Expedition Quests.
    • Bailey Miller - Custom NPC that provides Daily Quests which reward Endeavor Tokens.
    • QOL NPCS such as Kafra, Zoe, Warper, Healer, and Tool Dealer are also present near the spawn point of Eden HQ. Note that you cannot use this kafra to turn Eden HQ into a save point. Another base Kafra Employee is present at the Second Floor as well.

Registration

From left to right: Gramps, Instructor Ur, Instructor Boya, Neede, and Secretary Lime Evenor.

To be able to participate in the quests, players must first Register to be a member of the group.

Registration can be done by talking to Secretary Lime Evenor and selecting "Join the Eden Group". After Registration, Lime Evenor will give the player an  Eden Group Mark, and as part of the Eden Equipment Update will then offer the player a selection between  Eden Equipment Chest [Physical] and  [Magical] (with a label on a recommended set based on your class) as well as a supply of 250  Light Yellow Potions, 100  Light Blue Potions and 100  Novice Fly Wings. The player is then able to partake in the various board quests Eden Group offers.

There is no level or job class pre-requisite for joining Eden Group and receiving the supplies, but do note that the basic Eden armors and weapons have a level requirement of Level 10. The chest would also provide a basic Eden Weapon based on your job class. Opening the chest on a Novice will not give you a weapon.

The "Registration" is actually a check on whether or not you have the  Eden Group Mark in your inventory. Placing the item in Character Storage or destroying it via Item Crusher NPC will stop you from being able to receive Board Quests as well as being able to redo the Registration. You can receive another Eden Equipment Chest this way (should you open it as a Novice), but you will not receive another set of potion and fly wing supply.

Eden Equipment

Acquirable from the Eden Equipment Chest container that Lime Evenor provides upon Registration. These equipment are wearable by all classes Level 10 onwards. These serve as the updated set of equipment that Eden used to provide via the Eden Equipment Quests under Instrutor Boya and Instructor Ur. Despite being wearable at a low level, its effects scale with the player's level, ie. providing additional effects on certain level thresholds.

Additional equipment can be received while progressing with the Eden Academy Quests. You will also be provided a Kafra Uniform, a selection between Drooping Gunslinger and Neko Headdress, and a basic Set of Eden Shadow Gears.

Basic Eden Armor and Weapons have No Slots and are Unrefinable.

As noted earlier, the Eden Equipment Chest will provide the player with a weapon based on their job class. Opening the chest using a Novice or High Novice will not grant a weapon. If this happens, you can stash the Eden Group Mark into your character inventory to re-do the Registration process and receive another Eden Equipment Chest.

Board Quests

Aside from the NPCs that provide equipment quests, there are various Mission boards placed around the Eden Group HQ. These missions reward players with large amounts of EXP for doing simple tasks such as hunting monsters or gathering certain miscellaneous items. The boards for levels 11 to 85 are located in Eden Group HQ first floor, and the ones for levels 86 to 175 are in the second floor.

Board quests above level 100 will also reward you with 3  Paradise Coins for Hunting missions and 2 for Item Gathering missions. These coins are initially traded for equipment sets with Administrator Emil at moc_para01 110/97 but has since seen revitalized use in being the primary currency for the new Eden Academy Supplies and Noblesse, Imperial, and Grace sets.

NPC Provided Quests

Eden Equipment Quests (Obsolete)

Prior to the Eden Academy patch, which introduced the updated sets of Eden Equipment, Eden Group used to have a set of quests that aimed to provide the player with armors and weapons apt for their level range. The provision of these old sets of gear is deemed obsolete due to the fact that the Basic Eden Equipment sets are now provided on Registration rather than after a quest. The quests though, are kept as is as they are an option for levelling via their nature of monster subjugation and quest exp.

NPCs such as Administrator Michael and Blacksmith Toren are slated to be removed in a future patch.

Instructor Boya (Level 12 - 60)

The first Equipment Quests are given by Instructor Boya. To begin, talk to the NPC and select Participate in Training. Depending on what level you are, she will direct you to an NPC located somewhere in Midgard.

When you find the NPC, they will instruct you to kill a certain amount of monsters in the area. Return to them after you finished killing the monster, and at the end they will ask you to return to Instructor Boya.

Instructor Ur (Level 60 - 100)

Once you reach level 60 and advanced into your second job, the next set of Equipment Quests will be given by Instructor Ur. To begin, talk to the NPC and agree to participate in the advanced training program. Depending on what level you are, he will direct you to one of the few NPCs located around Midgard.
