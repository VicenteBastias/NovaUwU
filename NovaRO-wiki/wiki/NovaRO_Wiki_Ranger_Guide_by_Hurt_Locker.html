Sharp Shooting Build

Overview

Being a transcendent ability and having a VCT reduction from level 100, as well as having a small AOE, Sharp Shooting is one of the best starting builds for a beginning ranger, but it's also a favorite build for veterans.

Sharp Shooting is one of the few builds in the game that promotes real gear progression, this means every new piece of gear you get will feel like a real upgrade.

Sharp Shooting excels at clearing groups of monsters quickly and no problem against mob with "agi up" (critical build, no hit), great against most bosses, but fights against Green Aura bosses.

Requirements

For the Sharp Shooting build (known as SS) it requires 70+ Critical Rate, 193 ASPD, 72% ACD (After Cast Delay), 0.5 Fixed Casting and 96-98% VCT (Variable Cast Time); NEVER get to the insta-cast, because spam gets "stuck" and you lose DPS (Damage Per Second) and it is best to always be "Close" but not so low, otherwise you will lose spam. Note: While one can simply say that you need 72% Acdr and 193 ASPD, this is not entirely true. There is one more factor that plays a very important role: Ping. Bottom line: You should test yourself how much acdr% and ASPD you need, because if your ping doesn't allow it, investing in more ASPD and acdr will be worthless. If you want the long explanation, BI has already done a test with many participants, here BlackIntel Guide.

Aimed Bolt Build

Overview

Although it is a single target ability, Aimed Bolt is simply the strongest DPS setup for a Ranger. However, with great power comes great requirements.

Aimed Bolt is a very investment-intensive build and as such is only recommended for veteran players who already have ways of funding their characters. What makes builds so expensive is the need for very specific bows, however if the player is able to overcome this hurdle they will be massively rewarded.

Aimed Bolt isn't made for taking out groups of monsters, and relies heavily on his "hit", not to miss, and "miss", but as the most powerful ranger ability, it shines more as a major boss build, which makes it a super build. strong for both end game dungeons and MH2.

Requirements

For the Aimed Bolt (known as AB) build, it requires 193 ASPD, 92% ACD (after Cast Delay), 1 Sec Fixed Casting, 1 Sec Cooldown Aimed Bolt (fixed CD skill; only reduced by a weapon) and 100% VCT (Variable Cast Time); ACD and Fixed will always be KEY/IMPORTANT for this build (above ASPD and VCT) as it requires almost 100% ACD, 1 second for Fixed and reduces Aimed Bolt's 1 second cooldown for perfect spam.

While it has seen better days, Arrow Storm it's a build that makes use of its huge AOE. Having the White Wing Set as early as level 100, it is recommended as leveling tool, however, this doesn't mean the skill doesn't have potential.

Having such a huge damage modifier (up to 2700% Atk) it's obvious it needs a balance. Such balance comes in the form of a massive 3.2 seconds cooldown. This cooldown can be reduced to 0.7 seconds with the right combo pieces, however, with the cooldown still being very high, every Arrow Storm casted counts.

Thanks to its huge damage modifier and its huge AOE, Arrow Storm is mostly used as an early leveling tool, but it is also used as a big area cleaner and a decent mid game instance build. It beats early game bosses and some mid game instance bosses, but Arrow Storm should not be seen as a bossing build.

Requirements

For the Arrow Storm (known as AS) build, it requires 183-193 ASPD, 3.2 Sec Cooldown Arrow Storm (fixed CD, can be reduced with gear), skill 0% ACD (After Cast Delay), 0.5 Fixed Casting, and 100% VCT (Variable Cast Time); Always try to get to insta-cast, because without it and you lose DPS (Damage Per Second), ACD is not necessary to use for this build (since Fixed AND VCT are more important).

Crescive Bolt Build

Overview

At last it has arrived! the new version of SS mixed with AB, is Crescive Bolt an incredible high DPS skill with continuous damage that can melt any mvp or anything you hit it with, but not everything is simple or easy. Since this skill increases the more times you hit, it not only increases its damage (4 hits in a limited way) but also its maximum sp cost, so it represents a challenge in terms of gear, economy and experience to know how to balance this skill and be usable for solo, because in party (carrying mandatory Wandy/Trouvere) the wear is not excessive and also at the critical rate level, since it does not have the passive skill that the Sharp Shooting +50 Critical Rate, that is, we must reach above 140+ Critical Rate in any way.

Requirements

For the Crescive Bolt build (known as CB) requires 140+ Critical Rate, 183 ASPD, 50% ACD (After Cast Delay), 0.7 Fixed Casting and 100% VCT (Variable Cast Time); The key to this build is to have the Perfect Fixed, VCT, as the ACD can vary according to your ping/ms. It is more delicate when it comes to spamming.

Overview

Solid Trap, Flame Trap, Deep Blind Trap and Switf Trap They are the best skills at the moment that deal significant and decent damage over their predecessors.   • GS = Gale Storm
  • CG = Calamity Gale

  • RGCT = Reduce Global Cool Time
  • VCT = Variable Cast Time
  • ASPD = Attack Speed
  • CD = Cooldown
  • AP = Activity Point
  • POW = Power, grants 5 Status Physical Attack per point and 1 P.ATK every 3 points.
  • STA = Stamina, grants 1 RES per point and 5 RES every 3 points.

  • SPL = Spell, grants 5 Status Magic Attack per point and 1 S.MATK every 3 points.
  • CON = Concentration, grants 2 HIT and 2 FLEE per point, and 1 P.ATK and S.MATK every 5 points.
  • CRT = Creative, grants 1 H.PLUS per point and 1 C.RATE every 3 points.
