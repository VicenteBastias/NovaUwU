Latest Arch Bishop changes and their roles in a Party

Click "Expand" to show contents----->

With the advent of new Arch Bishop skills such as, Convenio , the Arch Bishop's role (and responsibility) as Support in the party became even more dynamic, these responsibilities are:

  • Leader of the Party (you HAVE to be the party leader to use Convenio).
  • The Healer and supplier of buffs for the party as well as debuffing the enemy.
  • Keeping the party together and picking up any one who strays away from the group (if they're not the Lurer/Tank); a capability which is made possible by Convenio.
  • By extension, they can also act as a Tank with the aid of their own buff skills, along with Safety Wall spamming (what they call "Turbo Safety Wall") activated manually or with the help of NovaExt extension from the NovaRO patcher ("NovaExt Settings" can be found on bottom right icons in the Nova RO patcher).

This guide, as a result, will focus more on the AB's role as the Party Leader, the utilization of Teleport/Fly Wing plus Convenio to keep the party together and the one, along with the Lurer/Tank who controls where the rest of the party will go, directing the whole party on a spot of the Gramps map where most mobs reside.

In any case, being able to practice some of these skills in Gramps parties can become a good starting point to master most of your skills and strategies when the time finally comes for you to become the Full Support AB on parties at Instances.

Sloppy supporting and healing techniques and strategies, most of the time, aren't acceptable at the advanced gameplay demands of these game contents (especially the more difficult ones), so while you're still leveling in Gramps quests, I highly suggest you start practicing good habits, movements and techniques starting from there.

The Pre-level 85 leveling method I used

Click "Expand" to show contents----->

This leveling method didn't require me to buy any SP/HP restoration consumables, except Concentration Potions, due to my utilization of @go <preferred town> command and each town spawn point's Healer NPC as substitute for HP and SP restoration consumables.

  1. Following Nova RO Wiki's Leveling Guide above.
  2. Create Acolyte up to Priest Job Class using the Battle Priest Build (Strength)
    • You can use my personal Skill Build for Battle Priest and Battle High Priest
    • Editing Macros to provide easy transport and utilization of town's Healer NPC.
    • How I edited my Macro: Esc > BM/Shortcut Settings > Macro Tab > Change any of the "alt+" macros into the Right Click Button (it will appear as "App" in-game) on my keyboard (usually near the right CTRL button of most Keyboards) > click ok > alt+M > put: @go <insert town of preference> beside the key you changed.
    • Now if you're low on HP and/or SP, just press the "App" button in your keyboard and you'll get transported immediately to the town of your choice.
    • From there, use the Healer NPC to replenish your HP and SP, then use Warper NPC again to transport you to the map specified in your current Eden Quest.
    • Rinse and repeat this as you progress in your Eden Group Equipment Quest.
  3. Allowing @autoloot command always activated and selling the loots to NPC whenever I go back to town (so I can buy Blue Gems and/or Concentration Potion).
  4. Buff yourself with Increase Agility, Blessing and perhaps level 4 Angelus, can also add Impositio Manus, and just use normal auto-attacks on your enemies. Use Heal skill when needed or Teleport skill to escape.
  5. Go through the very start of Eden Group's Equipment Quest
  6. Steps to easily and quickly finish the Level 60-69 part of the Eden Quest:
    • It will be easier if you use one of your already-max leveled character to hunt for the materials 7 Sharp Leaf (@alootid +7100) and 5 Shoulder Protectors (@alootid +7196).
    • The Dryads that drop the Sharp Leaves are just in that map (Lukuka Forest: um_fild01) where the NPC is, once you're outside the Comodo Beach Dungeon for the last part of this Level 60-69 quest.
    • However it's way faster to hunt the Wootan Fighters that drops the Shoulder Protectors on Hoomga Forest: um_fild02 (the entrance is at the East Portal of um_fild01 map), where they're more abundant and can thus save you more time.
    • After hunting those items with your max-leveled character, just put the items on the Kafra Storage and have your Priest get them or utilize Dual Login and just trade those 5 Shoulder Protectors and 7 Sharp Leaves to your Priest, then talk to the NPC and you'll automatically pass the test.
  7. Continue up until Instructor Ur's level 80-89 Quest (I didn't do the 90-99 part and went straight for Gramps instead)
  8. Once reached around level 85+, proceed to following the level 85+ part of Nova RO's Leveling Guide (ctrl+f > From level 85)
  9. Before you start doing Gramps quest as a Priest: reset your stats and skills first into the Full-Support build; you can use my suggested Full Support Stats Build and Skill Build in this guide.
  10. On the part when it says "Turn in both Gramps and hit 99/69 immediately.":
    • Allocate your stats and skills, the same ones you used as Battle Priest (Strength)
    • Go to Eden Group Building's 2nd floor
    • Click on the 91-99 Mission Board (near the stairs from whence you came)
    • Select "Rachel/Veins"
    • Proceed to hunt 30 Siromas (they are more easily accessible and are not aggressive)
    • Go to Ice Dungeon (Warper NPC > Dungeons > Ice Dungeon)
    • Complete the hunting quest, utilizing your "App" button to return to town quickly once you get low on HP/SP, and going back to the dungeon to hunt Siromas again using auto-attacks enhanced by your own buffs, until quest completion.
    • Check the level 91-99 Mission Board again and Turn-In the hunting quest and you should become level 99/70 at this point.
    • After reaching level 99/70, reset both of your stats and skills through Reset Man at Main Office (type command: @navi npc_in 110, 29)
    • Then start following the Stats and Skill Build I'll recommend after this section.
  11. Continue following the Nova RO's Leveling Guide, doing Banquet of Heroes story quests as mentioned in the leveling guide, and continue doing Gramps quest while practicing your skills and techniques as Full Support AB with the help of this guide.

The Stats Build

This is the Stat Build that I've used when I was still leveling my Arch Bishop in Gramps; I prioritized being able to survive and even tank (high VIT) as well as being able to quickly cast my skills (high DEX), since in Gramps parties (and I think even in other parties at some Instances), the party is always on the move.


Click "Expand" to show contents----->

From level 99/70 High Priest (NOTE: These stats are approximates only, with the help of iRO Wiki's Renewal Stat Calculator):

  • 90 points on DEX
  • 70 points on INT
  • 50 points on AGI
  • 50 points on VIT
  • Put any leftover points to DEX and save the points (because the goal is to get to 100 points of DEX immediately).

After becoming Arch-Bishop, this is the suggested Stat Build to aim for as you level:

  • 100 points on: VIT, INT, DEX
  • 90 points on: AGI (with Increase Agility/Canto Candidus Buff, you'll get a total of 100, if not more, AGI stat points)
  • Prioritize: 100 points on DEX first, followed by INT, then VIT, and work towards 90 points on AGI last.
  • This is built in a way that can provide you with resistances, even immunity on most status ailments in the game as discussed in other articles.

Suggested Final Stat Build:

  • After achieving 100 points on Vit, Int and Dex, and 90 points on Agi, you can press forward to achieving 120 points on TWO of these stats (except perhaps Agi); it's up to you on what to raise up to 120 points, but I recommend achieving 120 points on both Vit and Dex (for Temporal Boots of Vit and Dex bonuses), for the purposes of being able to Switch Gear (alt+D) between Fast-Cast Full Support Mode to Tanking Mode.
  • Thus, the final base (without bonuses) stat-build at max level is:
    • 120 VIT and DEX
    • 100 INT
    • 90 AGI
    • 1 LUK
    • 2 STR
  • You can always adjust these stats based on what you deem necessary; especially once you've finished your Gramps leveling phase.

Acolyte

Click "Expand" to show contents----->

  • Maxing out the essential buffs (Increase Agility and Blessing) and Heal will improve the potency of your advanced Skills as an AB later on.
  • From my experience, you'll barely have to use Decrease AGI and Signum Crusis (and on some degree, even the skills Ruwach, Warp Portal and Pneuma) in Gramps runs.
  • Putting Angelus at level 2 is the minimum to unlock Kyrie Eleison which is a prerequisite to Praefatio.
  • But you can still level Angelus up to level 4 like in this build for a bit of versatility.
  • It is possible to raise Angelus to level 10 by sacrificing 1 point in Teleport, and not adding points on Warp Portal and Pneuma; but I wouldn't recommend increasing Angelus' level further than 4 (citing the 3rd point above).
  • (NOTE: The 5th point above only applies on Gramps runs; Pneuma is a very useful/even necessary skill on Instances and other contents outside of Gramps, and Warp Portal can allow you to save Warp Points (using /memo) on some areas/maps that the Warper NPC exclusive to Nova RO Server might not be able to transport you to).


High Priest/ess

Click "Expand" to show contents----->

  • Skills I haven't unlocked in this build are virtually useless in Gramps runs as a Full Support AB, Priest/ess or High Priest/ess; don't bother with those skills.
  • Impositio Manus is a good buff to give towards Physical Damage Dealer allies, but it gives all your skills a cooldown after use (also called a "Global Skill Cooldown"/"Global Skill Delay") which can be quite an annoyance in some situations.
  • Assumptio is a really good skill to give to either yourself or to your Tank/Lurer allies, or even those with squishy defenses to help them survive.
  • Kyrie Eleison gives anyone you give it to a barrier that prevents damage, this means you can also use this to protect yourself, or any other allies that cast skills (like Mage classes) from being interrupted in their skill casting.
  • Kyrie Eleison is sufficient enough at level 8; its Praefatio version (even at level 5) can block more attacks than level 10 Kyrie Eleison would; giving you two more points to max Impositio Manus, which the damage dealers in your party will definitely appreciate.
  • Magnificat is a very important skill to max; you and your party members need all the SP REGENERATION BOOSTS available to them.
  • Maxing out Resurrection (more powerful "Heal" in a sense, on a downed ally) and Meditation Passive (increase SP Recovery) can help you a lot as Full Support AB role.
  • Platinum Skill: Redemptio: This is basically a free-skill (no stat points needed), and it revives multiple members of your party at ONCE.
    • The Cast Time is 4 Seconds and naturally cannot be interrupted by any attacks, nor hastened by any skills or equipment.
    • You can use this in emergencies and if you need to revive more than one party members at once.
    • Take note that you'll be very vulnerable once you finish casting this skill; thus prepare your HP/SP restoration consumables.
    • You might also want to protect yourself with Kyrie Eleison and Safety Wall first before casting the skill if there are enemies around.
    • If you cannot see this skill unlocked in your Skill Tree Window; go to Main Office in Prontera and talk to Platinum Skill NPC (@navi npc_in 123, 40).
    • For more information about the skill, visit this iRO Wiki Page
  • Safety Wall : Maxing out this skill is also very important especially once you utilize a technique called "Turbo-Safety Wall" and in being able to keep yourself and your allies safe from Melee Physical damage. As this skill's level increases, the cast time becomes significantly shorter.
  • You can sacrifice points from Assumptio (it's mostly over-written by Praefatio/Kyrie Eleison anyway) and Lex Aeterna (Vituperatum is an AoE Lex Aeterna; more useful for Gramps maps vs Mobs, albeit this one has a cast time and consumes Blue Gemstones unlike its single target counterpart).
    • The above can be done to raise Sanctuary to level 7(which reaches its max healing value starting from that level), though from my experience I hardly ever used Sanctuary (and even Arch Bishop's Epiclesis for that matter) since in Gramps, the team is always on the move; rarely sitting still as the mobs are cleared almost immediately.
  • However, the main advantage of Assumptio is that it doesn't expire prematurely unlike Kyrie Eleison/Praefatio which expires prematurely once it already finished blocking a certain number of hits.
    • Thus you can also choose to buff your Tank/Lurer with level 5 Assumptio instead; which is the reason why Assumptio is level 5 in this example build.


Arch Bishop

Click "Expand" to show contents----->

  • IMPORTANT! >>> You should unlock Convenio IMMEDIATELY once you're Arch Bishop JOB LEVEL 22.
  • Recommended skills to level up (from Arch Bishop Job Level 2 - Job Level 22):
    • Clementia lv3
    • Canto Candidus lv3
    • Ancilla lv1
    • Coluceo Heal lv3
    • Praefatio lv5
    • Oratio lv5
    • Convenio lv1, for a total of 21 Skill Points
  • Convenio is a very important skill for this particular strategy, and the other skills I mentioned that you'll have to level up first are the essential ones you and your party will need ASAP.
  • NOTE: Your Convenio skill WILL FAIL if you cast it while your character is:
    • Standing on a cell occupied by Safety Wall
    • Standing on a cell occupied by Pneuma
    • Standing on a cell occupied by Land Protector/Magnetic Earth
    • You can activate /m2e command to easily see where some of these skills' occupied cells are.
    • As of now, the only ways you can prevent cast interruption while casting Convenio in a mobby area is to use Kyrie Eleison on yourself/Praefatio or equipment with a Coyote Card, Phen Card, or Ancient Decoration of Rift + Ancient Armor of Rift Equipment Combo.
  • After unlocking Convenio: You must unlock and max out Sacrament skill IMMEDIATELY, since this is the buff you yourself will definitely need to further increase the casting speed of your skills even with Eden Group Equips at 100 Base DEX stats; you can--and should-- also give this buff to other allies that mostly casts skills such as Job Classes originating from the Mage/Magician branch, or even other Job Classes who has Cast Times on their most-used skills.
  • Once you've unlocked those essential skills I mentioned above, feel free to level up the other skills that I suggested on this build in whatever order you feel is necessary.
  • NOTE: It's NOT recommended to aim at unlocking Convenio at Job Level 15 (the earliest you can unlock it), since the AoE Increase Agility and Blessing are two of the most immediate buffs your party will need. It's also not advisable to leave Clementia and Canto Candidus at level 1 while you get Convenio ASAP because their skill range at that level is very limited to benefit most of your party members. Unlocking Convenio at Job Level 22 is still a better course of action.
  • PLEASE: Don't waste skill points on Judex and Adoramus, your role is Full Support, NOT DPS/AoE Damage Dealer! And this particular strategy is designed for ABs who have little to no funding at all.
  • You can only double as Support and Damage Dealer for the party if you are WEALTHY enough to have sufficient equipment to make it work! (More at Reno's Exorcist AB Guide)
  • Expiatio is a good buff to give your Physical AND Magical Damage Dealer allies as well (with the new update, as of this writing, it now pierces BOTH Physical AND Magical Defense), so you can use this in combination with Odin's Power and Impositio Manus (Impositio Manus is more useful for Physical Damage Dealers though).
    • NOTE: Despite the description of Expiatio adding "Holy Power", it DOESN'T actually add Holy Element to your weapon (the one that does this is Aspersio instead). The "Holy Power" part is just there for decoration, a flavor-text.
  • If chaining these three skills, a recommended sequence will be: Odin's Power > Expiatio > Impositio Manus (this has to be last due to it causing a Global Cooldown on your skills).
  • You can use Offertorium AFTER casting Sanctuary (to conserve SP), to boost its healing power. (Credits to Reno's AB Guide)
  • You can deactivate the effects of Offertorium by using Magnificat skill and vice-versa. (Credits to Reno's AB Guide)
  • Likewise, you can also use Offertorium to temporarily boost the power of your Heal/High Heal, Sanctuary or Coluceo Heal, if for some reason you find one or many members of your party having less than half of their HP.
  • Level 1 Offertorium combined with one or two Coluceo Heals are mostly enough to restore your party's HP to near max/full, and sometimes Offertorium + Highness Heal might be necessary to be used on Tanks/Lurers. Afterwards just cancel this skill immediately by using Magnificat.
  • Don't bother and waste SP on healing Mechanics that are using Madogear ; your heals and some of your buffs won't work as explained in the article I linked.
  • Max out Sacrament, Coluceo Heal, Clementia, and Canto Candidus as these skills are the ones your party needs the most regardless of their Job Class (and YOU need to maintain constant Sacrament buff too along with other allies that casts skills).
  • Praefatio at level 5 is more than sufficient for supporting your party in Gramps runs; it also has less cooldown than if it's on level 10, allowing you to use it more frequently.
  • Remember you can always use Kyrie Eleison to refresh the barrier granted by Praefatio on your party members .
  • You can use Epiclesis from time to time if your team is having SP problems; though most of the time, the party is always on the move, thus maintaining Magnificat always active is the better course of action.
  • Ancilla: This will be the Skill (and the Item it produces from your Blue Gemstones) that will be one of your ultimate and most reliable means of SP restoration through a technique I call "Ancilla Cycle" (more on this later on the Chapter 8 of this guide).
    • In addition, Ancilla also boosts your Healing Power and SP Recovery; this skill is a must-get and a must-use!
  • Clearance: Make sure to read the skill information correctly; this skill will only remove buffs and debuffs on yourself, your party members and normal, non-MvP boss enemies, don't waste time and SP using this skill on players that are NOT in your party or Enemy players in WoE/PvP.
    • For a list and information of the Buffs and Debuffs cleared by this skill, see this iRO Wiki Article and this Forum Page (credits on Reno's Full Support Arch Bishop Guide).

Useful Commands

These are some of the commands that you can use to make your job as Full Support Arch Bishop more convenient.

A full list of these commands along with their respective effects are in this article, and this one too for NovaExt (a built-in extension system with your Nova RO game app).

The ones I'll list here are the ones that you'll likely be using the most, in Gramps parties or maybe even in some Instances.


Click "Expand" to show contents----->
  • @noshare: Prevents the party's auto-looted shared loots from getting in your inventory; you must avoid getting overweight because it will interfere with your SP regeneration; using SP restoration items is not recommended especially as a new player due to the amount of possible Zeny sink this might cause, plus, you have your Ancilla skill to solve your SP problems (more on this later).
  • /snap: If this is OFF, your cursor won't snap and stick to any enemies. Sometimes you REALLY have to turn this off in order for your to place Safety Wall on your own character especially if you are surrounded by mobs.
  • /skillsnap: You may have turned off /snap, but with /skillsnap on, you're still in DANGER of your cursor sticking to a monster instead of your own tile since this particular Mouse Cursor Snap activates while you're using a targeted skill.
  • /itemsnap: Turn this OFF to prevent your cursor from automatically sticking to the items scattered on the ground which can also mess up with your Safety Wall aim.
    • So to use your Turbo Safety Wall effectively and accurately, you must TURN-OFF BOTH /snap, /skillsnap and /itemsnap.
    • Now's probably a good time as well to get used to targeting monsters with your Auto-Attacks or Skills without relying on these two Mouse Cursor Snaps.
  • @noks: This prevents other people/parties from being able to hit the enemies that you and your party is currently attacking/being attacked with.
  • @nopickup: This can help prevent you from (accidentally) picking up items that dropped on the floor when you should be moving there to heal/buff someone instead.
  • /fpsunlock: Can help making your Nova RO game client to run smoother graphically; allowing you smoother transition between movements so you can do your job even better as a Support, especially during very busy/chaotic situations.
  • /fps: Displays your current FPS and Vsync. Used to check the effects of /fpsunlock
  • /box: If enemies enter this boxed area, you'll get detected by them.
    • It also displays the relative maximum distance of the casting range of both Pneuma and Safety Wall (and, as mentioned by Arch Bishop Phillip in other parts of this webpage, it also seems to be the range wherein you're able to give your allies the targeted heals and buffs like Expiatio, Odin's Power, Impositio Manus, and Sacrament among others) from where you're standing, without having to make your character move from their spot.
    • I did try it out myself and indeed; most, if not all of your targeted heals, buffs, debuffs and skills can be cast inside this boxed area (or at the very least, near the BORDERS of this box), without having your character move from their place.
    • Thus you can also use this command to help you gauge your distance while assisting your allies if you're debuffing enemies with your Odin's Power (to reduce their DEF and MDEF), and Lex Aeterna/Vituperatum, or buffing/healing your allies in a relatively safe distance without having to walk into/near them.
  • /m2e: Highlights the tile on which AoE skills have an effect. Useful to seeing the area of effect of skills such as Pneuma and Safety Wall
  • /circle3: This displays the range of your AoE buff skills; only party members inside this circle will get affected by your AoE buffs (i.e. Magnificat, Canto Candidus, Clementia, Praefatio).
  • @showbuffs: Useful in keeping track of some (NOT all) of your buffs on your teammates (visible on your alt+z window and in Party UI boxes).
  • /dead: Highlights dead cells (un-traversible cells) in black. Useful for traversing around unfamiliar maps.
  • @hold 1: Prevents your character from (accidentally) moving while spamming targeted buffs and heals on your allies. Recommended to put this command in your in-game Macro Settings (Esc>BM/Shortcut Settings>"Macros" tab>Change one of the keys to one you don't use the most like the `(or ~) key).
    • NOTE: You have to type something else after pressing the modified macro for 2-4 times otherwise the game will block you from using the macro key, thinking that you're spamming a message 3 times; if you get stuck in the Hold position, you/your allies might get in danger, so you better prevent the game from blocking your access to @hold 1 command.
    • A quick way to prevent the system from blocking your command is to type something, such as the punctuation and symbol keys near your Enter key, example:
      • Press Enter once
      • Type one symbol like \ or ] or ;
      • Press Enter twice
      • Then you can prevent the system from blocking you from using the Macro'd @hold 1 command for another 4-5 presses.
  • @showsp: This displays the SP of your allies instead of their HP. Though this is more useful for Scholars/Sorcerers with their Soul Exhale/Soul Change skill, you can also utilize this to check if anyone in your team needs SP restoration (especially the damage dealers); if that is the case, you can use that as a cue to use Epiclesis to help them replenish SP.
  • /turbo (or press the Pause - Break button in keyboard, usually near Scroll Lock): Used to toggle Turbo Mode/Smart Casting on and off; a feature of NovaExt built-in with your NovaRO Game App (more on this later).

  • Open Nova RO Patcher (will need to exit the game first).
  • Click the the third bottom-leftmost Icon on the patcher ("NovaExt Settings"):

Gear Swapping (Alt+D): Change Multiple Equipment in a flash

Click "Expand" to show contents----->

Another useful in-game feature implemented as part of the Beta-client in Nova RO , this will enable you to change multiple parts of your equipment AT ONCE without having to double click them from your inventory or put them in your Hotkey Slots.

Thus making it possible to quickly transition between, for example, having an equipment that is geared for Full Support-mode to immediately transitioning towards equipment that's geared for Tanking-mode.

Remember that this action has 10 seconds cooldown.

There are some configurations you must do first:

  • Open your Equipment and Inventory Windows (Alt+Q and Alt+E).
  • Click "Swap Equip" on your Equipment Window.
  • On the extended Equipment Window that appears right under, drag and drop the 2nd set of equipment you wish to use.
  • They will then have two red arrows in them in your Inventory Window as seen from these screenshots (I already set mine so it's already like that).

Once you're done placing your desired 2nd set of equipment on the "Swap Equip" extended window:

  • Just click the "Change" button or simply press the shortcut keys Alt+D to activate "Swap Gear".
  • You're now equipping the equipment you placed on the Swap Equip extended window from earlier.
  • To transition back to previous equipment set, just click "Change" again or just use the Alt+D shortcut keys (will only work once the 10 Seconds Cooldown is over).
  • Take note that if you double click any of the equipment in your inventory that has those two red arrows, they will be removed from the Swap Equip Window.
  • Losing those two red arrows on a piece of equipment will make it become excluded from the gears that will be swapped when you use the Swap Gear function.

Solutions to (Nine) Common Problems you Might Encounter

Problem 1: Your SP being drained too fast even with Magnificat maintained

Click "Expand" to show contents----->

As already mentioned on other parts of this webpage, you can solve this problem by utilizing the Ancilla skill and item. To reiterate what Arch Bishop Phillip mentioned in this webpage regarding Ancilla skill, here's the method of replenishing your SP fast and efficiently using only Blue Gemstones (way cheaper AND RECOVERS MORE SP (once transformed into Ancilla), than the SP restorative items in NPC shops like Grape Juice and Mora Mandarin):

Preparation

  • Bring at least 300 or more Blue Gemstones per run (be it Gramps, Instances or other content).
  • Put Ancilla SKILL in hotkey slot and use it.
  • Hotkey the resulting Ancilla ITEM beside Ancilla SKILL (you can only hold up to three Ancilla item at a time).

Ancilla Cycle for SP Restoration

  • Use Ancilla ITEM ONCE
  • Use Ancilla Skill ONCE
  • Use Ancilla Item ONCE
  • Use Ancilla Skill ONCE
  • And just keep repeating until your SP becomes nearly full again.
  • IMPORTANT NOTE: Don't try to make three Ancilla stones at once while inside Gramps map/Instances or other in-game content (except in towns where you have access to Inn/Healer NPC); if you want to recover SP, CREATE one stone and USE one stone and repeat, ONE-AT-A-TIME.


Problem 2: Can't determine if Odin's Power, Impositio Manus and Sacrament buff on ally is still active or has already expired

Click "Expand" to show contents----->
  • As mentioned in other parts of the webpage and other guides; just use the timer on your Lauda Ramus and/or Lauda Agnus skills.
  • If multiple damage dealers are in the party, you might have to use the Lauda skills first before applying the Odin's Power and/or Impositio Manus buff to them; so you can replenish the buff immediately before it expires.
  • In some situations, you have to apply Odin's Power buff on some mini-boss/bosses (MvP) enemies as well to weaken their Physical AND Magical Defense; but do so at your own risk as sometimes their attacks can get way too powerful. If applying on enemies (Shift+Left Click), using Lauda skills is also the method to keep track of the buff duration.
  • As for the Sacrament buff; you can see a $ sign beside the names of the allies you buffed with it if you have @showbuff activated.
    • But a better practice is to always cast Sacrament on yourself first then follow it up with other allies who will need that buff.
    • Afterwards, just use your own Sacrament's timer to determine if it's nearly time for you to refresh your own Sacrament buff and on your other allies' as well, after all, you yourself also NEED the Sacrament buff.


Problem 3: Key damage-dealer allies or teammates who exited the Gramps map and went back in can't catch up/got separated from the whole team, getting mobbed and in danger

  • When this situation happens, use Convenio skill, even if only one of them are far away from the group; they have to see the enemy being defeated in their screens so that their kill count increases after all, so don't hesitate to use Convenio even if the rest of the party members are within the carnage scene.
  • In addition, you can transport them to safety through Convenio if in case they are indeed getting mobbed and cannot move on due to hit-stuns.


Problem 4: There's another party in your current area in the map and you can't shake them off/some or most of your members are following the other party by mistake

  • This is one of the instances wherein you should use Teleport/Fly Wing IMMEDIATELY.
  • Don't be picky with the area whether it has a lot of mob or not, as long as it's quite far away from your previous position, then use Convenio in that place to prevent your members being mixed up with the other party/the other party keeps on following YOUR party for some reason.
  • Afterwards you can just teleport again to find a good spot with lots of mobs and then use Convenio once again.
  • It's very important to separate yourselves from the other parties in Gramps maps ASAP, because this can become very detrimental to your hunting quests as well as can annoy both of the parties involved as well, and you have the ability to separate your own group as the Party Leader through the use of Convenio.


Problem 5: There's a huge mob of enemies ahead and one of your powerful damage dealers is getting ready to defeat them all before other party members catch up to the scene

Click "Expand" to show contents----->
  • Again, use Convenio in this instance; usually (and IDEALLY) when your DPS saw you casting Convenio, they should wait for a bit until everyone is already in the scene before killing the mobs.
  • Don't hesitate to use Convenio in these kinds of situations; since you have the capability (and the responsibility) to ensure that your party members will arrive at the scene ASAP so that their quest Kill Counts will increase.
  • A mob that only benefited 1-3 members of the party is a waste of mobs and a waste of time, since you also have a responsibility--and should have the courtesy--to remain in the party and help out until everyone's quest is finished; which also applies to other members of the party too especially the damage dealers.

  • Use Kyrie Eleison (on yourself)/Praefatio. This should buy you some time to cast important skills such as during casting Convenio in a particularly mobby area without being interrupted.

    • And as a precaution, you must turn off your /snap, /skillsnap and /itemsnap first so that your cursor will remain on your own character's tile, and not sticking to the mobs that are near you.

Gramps Hunting Quest Gameplay General Strategy and Guide

The main part of the guide. This is where the information I've been giving you so far from the previous sections will come together and be put to use. This is more or less a guideline of sorts, that personally served me well when I was still leveling at Gramps.

This section deals with the processes as well as notes on good habits and mindset you must practice and have as a Full Support Arch Bishop. This mostly applies on Gramps quest, though some of these can also apply on other in-game content as well such as Instances.


Things to do while waiting for/recruiting party members and before starting the run

    • Clementia
    • Canto Candidus
    • Praefatio (refresh the barrier using Kyrie Eleison on Lurer/Tank or you can just use Lv. 5 Assumptio on them)

    • Spamming the same buffs in quick, successive manner even if they're still far from being expired is not the very SP efficient thing to do; give your SP the time it needs to regenerate.


Good habits to practice while in Gramps and beyond

  • Make it a habit to look at these places frequently:
    • At the Party UI: to see who needs healing (and their buffs if you activated @showbuffs)
    • The Buff icons on your screen: to see which buffs need refreshing/keeping track of Odin's Power, Impositio Manus, and Expiatio's timers (through Lauda Ramus/Lauda Agnus and Increase Agility/Blessing respectively).

  • If you notice someone's HP/Icon is grayed out in the Party UI, open alt+z party window and check if they're offline, if they are online, it means they're far away from the group or is in need of Resurrection skill (also pay attention to your chat window, in the tab that you customized that I showed you on the "In-game tools to utilize" section).
  • Once again, maintain for yourself and any Mage/Minstrel/Wanderer/Skill Caster-type character in your party the level 5 Sacrament buff for faster cast time.
  • Use your own timer of Sacrament. Develop a habit of: if you buff yourself with it, be sure to buff the other members who needs it ASAP as well. Or you can see this as $ sign beside your ally's name if you activated @showbuffs.


About utilization of Teleport + Convenio

Click "Expand" to show contents----->
  • ALWAYS catch up to the Lurer/Tank, so after they gather the mobs, use Convenio to grab your party immediately near the mobs even if AT LEAST ONE of them are far away.
    • Remember you must gather all your party members to the scene as much as possible so that their kill counts will increase and no mobs (and time) are wasted.
  • Don't leave/Teleport and Convenio somewhere else too abruptly; you must wait until your party is already or nearly done wiping out the mobs in the area before moving on.
  • Use Convenio if someone's far away from the group while those ahead is killing the mobs; so before they kill off the mobs, use Convenio to make your other party members catch up on kill counts.
  • While you are casting Convenio, check your Buff Icons to see if any buffs needs refreshing; if there is, take note of that and be prepared to cast it immediately after casting Convenio.
    • Reapply the almost-expiring buffs IMMEDIATELY after using Convenio while your party members are still within your screen because some of them love TO GET THE HELL AWAY FROM YOU immediately after Convenio.
  • Most of the time, the ones I cast after using Convenio is Praefatio (if it's not on cooldown) and/or Coluseo heal.
    • For some reason, you will take a lot of damage in a mobby area after casting Convenio as all of the enemies' attacks are concentrated on you; so take note of this and be ready to heal yourself (using Highness Heal on yourself will be effective in this case).
  • If there is ANOTHER PARTY NEARBY, Kyrie Eleison yourself (if this buff is already expired on your character), then use Teleport.
    • Don't be picky with the place as long as it's far enough from the other party and Convenio immediately.
  • If mobs are thin when you Teleported, use Teleport again and look for a mobby area, then use Convenio there.
    • Or just follow the Lurer/Tank and you two might be able to find a mobby area nearby.
  • If the mobs are thinning as you and your party members are traversing the map, use Kyrie Eleison on yourself, use Teleport and look for a decently/overly mobby area then use Convenio there.
    • This is the moment where you shine as one of the "Pseudo-Lurers/Mob-Finders" in the party.


Party Management, Additional Buffs Repertoire

  • Once you're high level enough, feel free to add more buffs to your repertoire aside from the Priority FOUR (Six) BUFFS I mentioned.
  • Great additions are:
    • Lauda Agnus
    • Lauda Ramus
    • Renovatio
    • Expiatio lv5 (on Physical AND Magical Damage Dealers)
    • Odin's Power on the Magical and Physical Damage Dealers (use Lauda Ramus/Agnus right after to serve as skill timers)
    • You can also use Impositio Manus on the Damage Dealer allies if you can spare a moment (due to the Global Skill Delay) or while fighting a really powerful enemy (use Lauda Ramus/Lauda Agnus right after, to serve as Skill Timers too).


Becoming a Sub-Tank (and also making your Tank/allies safe): Turbo-Safety Wall

Click "Expand" to show contents----->
  • It is possible for you to act as a Tank for parties.
  • Equipment that make you have more HP (as well as having UNINTERRUPTIBLE Skill Casting) will help you during Tanking-mode.
  • For Tanking/Luring equipment reference, you can check them on the General Arch Bishop page (along with a few more tips and tricks from other contributors).
  • You also need to utilize the skills: Kyrie Eleison/Praefatio, Assumptio, Pneuma and Safety Wall, all depending on the Situation, you may even have to use Sanctuary and/or Epiclesis if the situation calls for it.
  • Another method of tanking is through the use of the technique called "Turbo-Safety Wall", utilizing NovaExt configurations, which was discussed earlier on the "Useful In-game Tools to Utilize" part.
  • That technique allows you to spam Safety Wall over and over; no worries because spamming the skill over and over on a tile that already has Safety Wall/Pneuma on it WON'T CONSUME any SP and Blue Gemstones; it will only consume those once Safety Wall's casting is successful.
  • You can use this technique to tank Melee Attacks from enemies, if you're tanking Ranged Attacks, use Pneuma instead.
  • Note that you can also use those techniques on your allies instead, and on your tank, too, thus keeping them safe and your tank, being more tankier.
  • However, you can't use Safety Wall if the cell is occupied by a Land Protector/Magnetic Earth skill, or it's a Dead Cell (un-walkable cell).
  • And once again, it's highly recommended to turn off /snap , /skillsnap and /itemsnap so you can use Turbo Safety Wall, or just manual Safety Wall spamming with greater Accuracy.
  • You can also use this technique in conjunction with the command /box so you have a guide to see how far you can use Safety Wall WITHOUT moving from your spot.
  • In combination with above; you can also use @hold 1 (or press the Macro Key that you modified; I was able to discuss the full mechanics and precautions on the earlier parts of the guide as well at "Useful Commands" part) so you can spam/rapid-fire any of your Targeted-Buffs and heals without worry of moving accidentally to your demise or missing your allies.


Debuffing the Enemies

Click "Expand" to show contents----->
  • From time to time/if you can spare some SP and Blue Gemstones, you can utilize Vituperatum (the AoE Lex Aeterna) on the mobs if your party's damage-dealers cannot reliably wipe out the mobs immediately.
  • If you and your party are fighting a powerful enemy instead like an MvP Boss enemy, use Lex Aeterna instead (especially if your damage dealers can kill off the MvP's slaves easily) since it's more spammable, no cast time and no Blue Gemstone cost.
  • Another Skill Combination that you can try in most situation:
    • Use the Aspersio buff on your Physical damage dealers.
    • Afterwards, use Oratio, at least level 5 (65% success rate, 10% Holy Resistance Reduction) to weaken the Holy Element Resistance of all the enemies on your screen.
    • This will allow your damage dealer allies to deal even more damage.
    • This will work, as far as I know, on a lot of enemies, as long as their Element IS NOT HOLY.
    • You might find some more use for this on the High Turn-In (TI) quest of Gramps (145+), since sometimes, damage dealer allies requires around 2-3 AoE skills to clear a mob especially if they're under-geared/under-leveled, and even on MvP Boss enemies that are naturally weak and will take more damage to the Holy Element.


The Mindset that I recommend you put in mind, and maintain as much as possible

Click "Expand" to show contents----->
  • A rule of Rescue Personnel: Remember to save and keep yourself safe first before saving others, don't add to the casualty, ensure the scene is safe first, then prioritize the one that will do the most damage on your enemies, for killing your enemies are one of the best way to clear the path for you to save the others as well and keep them safe.
  • Always be mindful of the present, your mind and heart calm, relaxed, and at peace.
  • Having a good presence of mind will help as well in your task as Full Support not just in Gramps but also in other in-game content.
  • With a calm heart and mind, you'll be able to make better decisions and actions even if the current situation is very chaotic (you'll most likely encounter this more on harder in-game content).


That's all the information I can give for now. Thank you for taking the time to read this guide if you did and I hope you're able to learn something that will be useful for you as a Full Support Arch Bishop be it Gramps, Instances or other in-game content.

I wish you good fortune on your journeys.
