Overview

Requirements
Base Level: 180
Starting Point: (jup_fild01 303, 349)Copied!
Items: -
Prerequisite(s): Deciphering the Ancient Tablets
Rewards
Experience: -
Items: up to 20  Ancient Drives

Ability to craft Shadow Gear

With the help of the adventurers, the two teams lead by Richard Rittenburg and Immanuel Rittenburg were able to locate a place which could be the main source of robots in the area. Upon arriving, they'll discover what could have been an abandoned lab... But is it really?


Walkthrough

  1. Speak with Richard Rittenburg at (jup_fild01 303, 349)Copied! to create the instance.
  2. Click on the Door device to enter the instance.
  3. Once inside, a dialogue will trigger.
  4. Talk to ALExIS to continue.
  5. First Room
    1. Clear out the mobs in the first area, making your way to the bottom right.
    2. ALExIS will show up again, click on him to trigger the next dialogue. The first boss will appear.
    3. Once the boss is defeated, click on the Computer to unlock the next path.
    4. Clear up the remaining mobs to enable a portal to the second room.
  6. Second Room
    1. Talk to ALExIS to continue. This room contains lasers, so watch out. They do hurt. The lasers cannot reach the entrance of the room, so it is probably best to stay near the door for the first time.
    2. Make your way to the bottom right room, near 129/216. After the dialogues, more monsters will appear in the room that must be defeated.
    3. Once the mobs are defeated, click on the Computer to unlock the next path.
    4. Clear up the remaining mobs to enable a portal to the third room (95/172).
  7. Third Room
    1. Talk to ALExIS to continue. This room contains Turrets, so watch out. They are ranged monsters that cannot be attacked, so Pneuma or Neutral Barrier is required to progress. They ignore Defender and other similar defenses.
    2. Make your way to the room below, near 69/113. Speak with ALExIS to trigger the first boss fight with the Anomaly. For more information about its mechanics, please refer to Skills and Mechanics.
    3. Once the boss is defeated, click on the Computer to unlock the next path.
    4. Clear up the remaining mobs to enable a portal to the fourth room (94/86).
  8. Fourth Room
    1. Talk to ALExIS to continue. This room has no special mechanics.
    2. Make your way to the bottom room, near 130/40. After the dialogues, more monsters will appear in the room that must be defeated. Be careful of the Ancient Apocalypse, it is a mini-boss. For more information about its mechanics, please refer to Skills and Mechanics.
    3. Once the boss is defeated, click on the Computer to unlock the next path.
    4. Clear up the remaining mobs to enable a portal to the fifth room (141/74).
  9. Fifth Room
    1. Talk to ALExIS to continue. This room has Electic Floors, so watch out. These are static areas on the ground that will damage you if you touch them. They also apply the Electrocuted debuff that heavily reduces your capacity to heal.
    2. Make your way to the room nearby, at 178/107. After the dialogues, more monsters will appear in the room that must be defeated.
    3. Once the mobs are defeated, click on the Computer to unlock the next path.
    4. Clear up the remaining mobs to enable a portal to the sixth room (163/161).
  10. Sixth Room
    1. Talk to ALExIS to continue. This room has no special mechanics.
    2. Clear the mobs in the first area to unlock the next one.
    3. Talk to ALExIS to continue. This room has no special mechanics.
    4. Make your way to the top room, near 184/309. The room contains 4 Ancient Apocalyspes, so be very careful. If you take them on all at once, you will struggle.
    5. Once the mobs are defeated, click on the Computer to unlock the next path.
    6. Clear up the remaining mobs to enable a portal to the Central Mainframe room (189/325).
  11. Keep going forward until you reach the MVP room.
  12. Speak with ALExIS to trigger the boss fight. For more information about its mechanics, please refer to Skills and Mechanics.
  13. Once the MVP is defeated, talk to Rittenburg to leave the area.

Skills and Mechanics

Only notable skills will be listed. The bosses below do have more skills.

Ancient Apocalypse (first)

This is the first boss you'll meet, in the first room of the instance. It is more less alone, so it can be handled easily. It has relatively high HP.

Mechanic Condition Description
Asura Strike Every 10 seconds This attack will most likely kill you. It can be avoided with Safety Wall.
Hell's Judgement Every 10 seconds Nothing to worry about there, it hurts but not enough to kill anyone.
Jupitel Thunder Always, frequent

Ancient Anomaly

This boss can catch you off guard if you're not careful. There are 4 turrets in the corners of the room which will kill anyone not under pneuma. It is recommended to simply stand in the middle of the room alongside the MVP.

Mechanic Condition Description
Turret Activation Every 20 seconds The turrets in the corners will activate for 10 seconds every 20 seconds. You will see a progress bar on top of them as well as an announcement to indicate when they will activate. Simply put a Pneuma or a Neutral Barrier to ignore their damage, or stand in the middle of the room since they cannot reaach you there.
Dispell Every 30 seconds
Power Up Below 80%
Every 30 seconds 		This increases the damage, but it already hurts enough that you won't notice much of a difference.
Damage Heal Below 30%
Every 2 minutes 		This can be hard to notice as the damage numbers will show up from you hitting the slaves of the MVP. But watch out for its HP suddenly not moving or going up (or if you can see the green numbers). This buff can be dispelled with either Dispell or Clearance.
Earthquake Below 30%
Every 25 seconds 		This can be rough to tank. Having Devotion would really help as the Royal Guard will end up taking all the damage, and most definitely die in the process.

Ancient Apocalypse (second)

This boss hurts. The Ancient Apocalypse will also move quite a lot, swap location with players or snap to them. He does deal a fairly high amount of damage, so I'd recommend having your priest back a bit as someone is bound to die in this fight. It is alone the first you meet him though, so it is manageable.

Mechanic Condition Description
Dispell Always, moderate
Gate of Hell Always, moderate
Sky Net Blow Always, moderate

ALExIS

ALExIS doesn't actually hurt a whole lot by herself. The challenge of this fight are the mechanics during the 5 phases of the boss. The first 3 phases can happen in any order. To trigger a phase, the MVP must take roughly 20% of damage. Once this triggers, the MVP will become immune to damage until the phase is cleared.

Mechanic Condition Description
Laser Phase 1 or 2 or 3 The arena will be filled with lasers going from one side to the other. These lasers do not hurt as much as the first ones, so they can be easily outhealed with sanctuaries and other healing skills. They deal fixed percentage damage. If you are hit, you will also receive a burning status, but that can be mostly ignored.
Turrets Phase 1 or 2 or 3 Multiple Turrets will show up in the arena. You will have a few seconds to react once this happens. If you group up on the other side of where the mobs spawn and use a pneuma on your party, it should be fairly easy to handle. If you stay on the side where the mobs spawn however, you will have a hard time.
Electric Floor Phase 1 or 2 or 3 Multiple red areas will appear on the floor indicating where the Electric Floor mechanics will happen. If you're not careful and touch one of them, you will gain the Electrocuted debuff for 10 seconds and also take 30% of your current HP. This debuff heavily reduces your capacity to heal yourself.
Laser
Electric Floor 		Phase 4 Same as the above, except you now have two mechanics at once.
Turret
Electric Floor 		Phase 5 Same as the above, except you now have two mechanics at once. This stage also adds 2 Ancient Apocalypses at the top of the map to kill.
Phase Clear Any phase For each phase, a giant red warning area will appear on either the left or the right side (they always alternate). This indicates that mobs will spawn in the area and it is highly recommended to go to the other side. You must also watch out for the current mechanic and prepare for it. Clearing up all the mobs that appear will remove the immunity on the boss and allow you to proceed.
Battle Reset Any phase If the whole party dies during one of the phases, you will received a warning. After 60 seconds, the MVP will heal back 10% of its HP and the phase (mobs and mechanic) will be cleared. You will have to do the phase again.
