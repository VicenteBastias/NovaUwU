Overview

Element (also known as Property) is an attribute that monsters and players have (defensive). It is also an attribute of attacks, including skills (offensive).

There are 10 different elements, namely:

  • Neutral
  • Water
  • Earth
  • Fire
  • Wind
  • Poison
  • Holy
  • Shadow
  • Ghost
  • Undead

Depending on the combination of offensive and defensive elements, damage can be increased or reduced (see below for details).

Defensively, each element is subdivided into four levels, with increasingly extreme effect on damage amplification or reduction.

While monsters can be of any element at any level, players only have access to level 1 defensive elements.

Offensively, elements do not have different levels.

Monster elements

  • The element of a monster can range from level 1 to level 4, and it determines the monster's defensive property.
  • The element of a monster does not determine the element of its attacks.
    • A monster's auto-attacks are always non-elemental.
    • A monster can use skills that deal damage of a different element than itself, meaning, e.g.
      • A Strouf can deal Wind damage by using Lightning Bolt, even though it itself is a Water 3 monster.
  • Note that, albeit rare, some monsters can change their defensive element after spawn.

Player elements

  • Defensively, players only have access to level 1 elements. But other than this, defensive elements work the same way on players as they do on monsters.
  • By default, a player's defensive element is Neutral 1, but can be altered by armors, cards, enchants or skills.
  • Multiple effects that change the element of a player do not stack with each other; and only one of them takes effect.
    • Enchants have a priority over cards, meaning, e.g.
      •  King Schmidt's Suit[1] with the  Fire Property (Armor) enchant and a  Angeling Card will make the element of the player Fire 1 instead of Holy 1 (only the enchant takes effect).
      •  King Schmidt's Suit[1] with the  Fire Property (Armor) enchant and a  Pasana Card will make the element of the player Fire 1 instead of Fire 2 (no stacking).
  • Offensively, depending on the class, players can have access to many different elements.
  • By default, the element of a player's auto-attacks is Neutral, but can be altered by the weapon equipped, ammunitions, consumables or skills.
    • Most physical skills have the same element as the player's auto-attacks, but there are exceptions (e.g. Magnum Break).
    • Most magical skills have a fixed element that is independent of the player's auto-attack element, but there are exceptions (e.g. Reverberation).
  • On Nova RO, PVP uses a special table for elemental damage modifications.

Non-elemental damage

Non-elemental damage is a special type of damage that is not affected by elemental modifiers.

Auto-attacks of monsters and Homunculi are a source of non-elemental damage.

It should not be confused with Neutral property damage, which is reduced when dealt against Ghost element monsters or players.

  • As such,  Ghostring Card does not reduce damages from monsters' auto attacks.

Note however that, confusingly, most items or cards that are described as having the effect of reducing Neutral damage actually reduce both Neutral and non-elemental damage (e.g.  Raydric Card).
