The Oboro (Obo) is the final version of the female Ninja class. Similar to 3rd classes, their base level caps at 185 and their job level at 65. They excel on farming with a physical build, due to their high sustain and survivability, but can also deal high amounts of magic damage, making the class very versatile. With many tricks up their sleeves, they're also great contenders for PvP.

Oboros differ from their male counterparts due to 3 skills: Moonlight Fantasy, Ominous Moonlight and Distorted Crescent.

A Ninja requires job level 70 and base level 99 in order to become an Oboro.

The male counterpart of the Oboro is the Kagerou.

Player Guides

Builds

Throwing

The prefered build for Kagerou. Has solid farming potential, due to the AoE damage of Swirling Petal, Kunai Splash and Kunai Explosion, reaching high enough damage to farm confortably in the most common farming places and Instances. They also have a competent melee burst in their rotation, thanks to Cross Slash .


With Earth Charms, you can get up to 150% Weapon Attack bonus at 10, making them a better option than other elemental charms.

Skills such as Cicada Skin Shed and Pure Soul make Ninja classes hard to kill in PVM, with the later enabling them to farm until 90% weight without the need of HP or SP consumables.


Due to the high, 3 second cooldown, of Swirling Petal, using a Golden Ninja Set is highly advisable when starting. You can easily obtain (and enchant) it at Rock Ridge, for Rock Ridge Coins.


Kagerou takes the edge over Oboro for this build, due to their exclusive skill Shadow Warrior, which increases the damage of Swirling Petal, Kunai Splash, Cross Slash and Kunai Explosion by 20% while active.


Magic

The preferred build for Oboro. Flaming Petals, with the 200% boost from Fire Charms provides great damage and can be spammed, as it has no After Cast Delay. That being said, ASPD plays a great part on how much it can be spammed, given that you're instant casting your spells.

As most magic damage builds, INT is your primary stat for damage, together with DEX for casting time and AGI for ASPD. Focus on getting instant cast, good enough ASPD and INT, in that order, as ASPD becomes a DPS multiplier once you get to instant cast.


Freezing Spear, paired with Ice Charms, can be used to deal with Fire property monsters. With a similar, 200% bonus from the Charms, paired with the situational bonus from Watery Evasion, Freezing Spear can be the most devastating spell of the build.

Wind Blade is also a viable option of reliable damage, although being more difficult to scale it via gears and cards, it gains a similar, 200% boost from using 10 Wind Charms.


For magic, Oboro takes the edge over Kagerou, since their exclusive skill Distorted Crescent add a great amount of MATK when used correctly (Having an even amount of SP left, after the skill cast).

Leveling Process

Ninja (Lv 1 - Lv 99)

  • Note: You may at any time during this process do the Cautious Village Quest as Oboros do not need to Rebirth, keep in mind you will need your own usable equipment if you choose to skip normal leveling.
  • Lv 1 - Lv 15+
    • For newer players it is especially recommended to do the tutorial level provided by the NovaRo team. (It is also quite easy and possibly faster.)
    • Get DEX to 30 and AGI to 10 during the tutorial (DEX first) this will later enable you to fight Skeletons in Payon cave without missing.
  • If not doing the tutorial use this:
    • Make sure to talk to Captain Carroc to find out how to play if you do not already know how. Talk to him again after getting off the boat.
    • Talk to the Agi Acolyte after that to get a speed boost then head to the center of town and talk to the shop helper just northwest of the fountain. Listen to her, then take the experience quest. Tell her you will do it and go buy a red potion from the store.
    • After this she will ask you to sell a poor can. This whole process will teach you about NPC selling and buying and will net you a big amount of experience.
    • Make sure to spend your skill points! Head over to Prontera using the custom server command @go prontera or @go 0. Here you will be greeted and guided to the Main Office building. Follow the red arrows on the ground inside and head to the upper left to the Job Master. Speak with him to become a Taekwon Class. Make sure to use your Novice Package provided to you by NovaRO.
      • Optional: Take the Novice quests and Red Herb Quest
  • Lv 16 - Lv 26
    • Warp to Payon Cave and kill Zombies and Skeletons until you are in the next bracket (26-50).
    • Use your Novice Main Gauche from Mom and level up the fire magic branch ( Flaming Petals down). Make sure to equip your Ring of Experience from the Novice Package! If this is not your first character, purchase many (200+)  Flame Stone from the Ninja Dealer in Prontera (eventually you will need these to make leveling a lot easier with AoE attacks).
  • Lv 26 - Lv 50
    • Go straight to Eden (@go eden / @go 36) and talk to Lime Evenor behind the desk (select Join the Eden Group), follow her dialogue and then move on to Instructor Boya next to her (again follower her dialogue). Instructor Boya's Eden Equipment Quest in Payon Cave: Talk to the Eden Group Member outside of Payon Cave before you start hunting Skeletons and Poporings. When you've completed them, report to the Eden Group Member, then to Instructor Boya. You will receive your first set of Eden Equipment from Administrator Michael in the room behind the Blue Door.
    • Once done move onto the floors below and grind to Base level 50 while leveling up Ninja Aura, Ninja Mastery and Cicada Skin Shed.
  • Lv 50 - Lv 70
    • Go back to yet another Instructor Boya's Eden Equipment Quest in Orc Dungeon: Talk to the Eden Group Member outside of Orc Dungeon before you start hunting Orc Zombies and Orc Skeletons. When you've completed them, report to the Eden Group Member then to instructor Boya. You will receive your second set of Eden Equipment from Administrator Michael in the room behind the Blue Door.
    • There are many many monsters in this area and it may be tough to level up at first. You should really be using AoE skills now sell loot from Payon Cave to get multiple  Flame Stone from the Ninja Dealer in Prontera as each cast of these skills will consume one.
  • Lv 70 - 85
    • Instructor Ur's Eden Equipment Quest in Glast Heim: Talk to the Eden Group Member outside of Glast Heim Churchyard before you start hunting Wraiths and Evil Druids. When you've completed them, report to the Eden Group Member then to instructor Ur. You will receive your third and final set of Eden Equipment from Blacksmith Thorn in the room behind the Blue Door.
    • Doing this quest allows you to enchant your level 3 Eden weapon once with Weapons Expert BK
  • Lv 85 - Lv 99
    • Level range to join the first bracket of Gramps quests. Note that Gramps quests are rotated at the start of every month. You can also hunt them outside of Gramps map. Use the @whereis (monster name) command to find out where a monster spawns.
    • Join a party for the Gramps quests in Eden, using the #lfg channel or a chat room in Eden Headquarters, be very specific as to where you want to go and what level/class you are. Learn what elements the monsters are and how to counter them before entering as this will make your quest easier. Align your element to the monster's weaknesses and hit with melee or Es skills while buffing with; Ka and Spirit skills on; yourself and party members.
    • Learn what monsters you are fighting there, fire may not be as effective as before, sometimes elemental magic in general will not be! If such is the case you can always grab the water and ice branch ( Freezing Spear down) and slow your enemies with ( Watery Evasion whie freezing them with ( Snow Flake Draft.

Oboro (Lv 99+)

  • If you haven't yet done so, do the Cautious Village Quest in Malaya.
    • Banquet for Heroes quests after your second gramps (up to and including Ritual of Blessing and all kitchen quests) combined with Rock Ridge quest: Fistful of Zeny and Rock Ridge Daily Quests (Contract with the Wealthy Merchant) should bring you at, near or above level 115 for the next gramps sets (Use Battle or Field and Job Manuals here as these quests give mega experience).
      • Remember, Contract with the Wealthy Merchant is available daily for extra experience.
    • Minimum level to access Sara's Memory, Bangungot Hospital.
      • You must complete Cautious Village and Nurse in Port Malaya quests to access Bangungot Hospital.
      • For Sara's Memory I recommend a Water element skill
      • Grab a Holy element skill for Bangungot Hospital (You will be fighting Demon class Enemies not undead)
  • Lv 115 - Lv 144
    • Level range to join the second bracket of Gramps quests.
  • Lv 100-110
    • Gelkah's Quests In Eden there are several NPCs in the back room near across from where you would get your Eden equipment. Talk to Gelkah for the first of many repeatable quests.
  • Lv 111-120
    • Mingmin's Quests. Also in the back room of Eden.
  • Lv 120-130
    • Melody Jack's Quests. Also in the back room of Eden.
    • Minimum level to access Nightmarish Jitterbug, Ghost Palace.
      • For Nightmarish Jitterbug grab an Aoe Skill that is not Fire or Wind element as half of the monsters will only take half damage either way, Holy element is the way to go here.
  • Lv 125
    • Minimum level to access Airship Assault, Devil's Tower.
      • Airship Assault has a strong boss if you are near this level, he can be hurt by holy element skills although one type of monster in there is not hurt and is actually healed by this element.
      • In Devil's Tower all monsters but one are dark element and will be hurt by Holy element.
  • Lv 130-140
    • Ragi's Quests. These are quite hard and basically require parties or leeching off of others you know well but give good exp.
    • Minimum level to access Old Glast Heim Normal Mode, Charleston Crisis, Buwaya Cave.
      • Again Holy element is effective in Old Glast Heim and are good as many monsters will use Agi Up, rendering normal attacks hard to hit with.
      • Charleston Crisis can make use of any element, go nuts!
      • Buwaya Cave will need the Wind element as all monsters there are Water element.
  • Lv 140
    • Minimum level to access Horror Toy Factory, Central Laboratory, Malangdo Culvert, Bakonawa Lake.
      • You must complete Cautious Village and Bakonawa Extermination to access Bakonawa Lake.
    • Minimum level to do Dimensional Travel. This is a pre-requisite quest to access Bios Island.
      • Horror Toy Factory has strong dark monsters that should be defeated for extra rewards however dark monsters take reduced damage from most elements.
      • Central Laboratory will have three unpredictable MVP characters and element here should be chosen to compliment your party members.
      • Fish monsters are mostly Water as is the case in Malangdo Culvert, use Wind element.
      • As with Buwaya Cave this MVP is similar and needs the Wind element.
  • Lv 145 - Lv 175
    • Level range to join the third bracket of Gramps quests.
      • Proceed the same way you would as in earlier Gramps quests, find out the monsters before hand, your party compliments and role.
  • Lv 145
    • Minimum level to access Sarah and Fenrir.
      • Sarah and Fenrir has strong dark monsters that should be defeated for extra rewards however dark monsters take reduced damage from most elements.
  • Lv 160
    • Minimum level to access Bios Island, Morse Cave, Temple of the Demon God, Monster Hunter, and Deserted Island.
      • Bios Island has Earth, Fire, Water and Undead Monsters, wow most elements arent effective here Fortunately this really is a party instance so try to grab one.
      • Morse Cave has a large amount of Neutral monsters but Fire element should make short work of the Earth boss.
      • Temple of the Demon God and Monster Hunter have various monsters and will often require a full sized party to complete (12 and 5 respectively). Learn about your party members thoroughly and do not get in the way. If you can deal damage deal damage, tanks will tank of course.
      • Deserted Island has Water monsters, use Wind element here.
    • Recommended level to try Old Glast Heim Hard Mode if you have not done so at this point.
      • The same instance procedures as Old Glast Heim Normal, Fire element is effective in Old Glast Heim and are good as many monsters will use Agi Up, rendering normal attacks hard to hit with.
